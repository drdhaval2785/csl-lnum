{%daś-āparādha%}¦ (CII 3, 4), literally, ‘the ten offences’; but
<div n="lb">really, the fines for the ten offences; cf. {%sa-daś-āparādha%} (IE 8-5);
<div n="lb">power to punish and realise fines for the ten offences; also
<div n="lb">called {%daś-āpacāra.%} According to the Buddhists, the ten sins
<div n="lb">were murder, theft, adultery, lying, calumny, insult, idle
<div n="lb">talk, hatred, covetousness and dogmatic error (A. Getty, {%The%}
<div n="lb">{%Gods of Northern Buddhism,%} p. xxv, note). For a Brahmanical
<div n="lb">list, see {%Dāśāparādhika;%} cf. {%pañca-mahāpātaka, pañc-āparādha.%}
