<L>957<pc>054<k1>Boga<k2>Boga
{%bhoga%}¦ (IE 8-4; CII 3; EI 23, 33), literally, ‘enjoyment’
<div n="lb">(cf. {%bhukti%}); a {%jāgīr%} (cf. {%Mahārāja-Sarvanātha-bhoga, Mahā-%}
<div n="lb">{%sāmantādhipati-Śrīdhara-bhoga,%} etc.); possession (cf. {%bhog-%}
<div n="lb">{%ādhīnā gṛhītā%}). Originally ‘enjoyment’; then ‘property’,
<div n="lb">‘a {%jāgīr%}’; then also a territorial unit which was generally the
<div n="lb">subdivision of a district (IE 8-4; EI 25; 28; CII 4). See
<div n="lb">{%bhukti, āhāra.%}
<div n="P">{%Cf. Kaivarta-bhoga%} (IE 8-4; EI 2; CII 1), the fishermen's
<div n="lb">preserve.
<div n="P">({%IE%} 8-5; EI 29, 30; HRS), periodical supplies of fruits,
<div n="lb">firewood, flowers and the like which the villagers had to
<div n="lb">supply to the king; sometimes explained as ‘tax in kind’
<div n="lb">(CII 4).
<div n="P">({%EI%} 1), [an object of] enjoyment.
<div n="P">({%SITI%}), tax-free land set apart for the enjoyment of a
<div n="lb">person for the performance of specified services; same as
<div n="lb">{%mānya.%} See {%bhogottara, Bhogin,%} etc.
<div n="P">{%Cf. bhoge%} (LP), ‘for the right of enjoyment’.
<div n="P">{%Cf. sa-bhoga%} (IA 9), privilege of the donee of rent-free
<div n="lb">land; probably refers to {%aṣṭabhoga-tejaḥsvāmya%} (q. v.).
