{%bhūmicchidra-nyāya%}¦ (IE 8-5; EI 30; CII 3, 4; HRS),
<div n="lb">literally, ‘the maxim of the fallow land’; the principle of the
<div n="lb">rent-free enjoyment of land by one who brings it under cultiva-
<div n="lb">tion for the first time. See {%Ep. Ind.,%} Vol XXIX, p. 86. The
<div n="lb">maxim is based on the old custom of allowing a person, who
<div n="lb">first brings a plot of fallow or jungle land under cultivation
<div n="lb">for the first time, to enjoy it without paying rent. {%Bhūmi-%}
<div n="lb">{%cchidra%} gradually came to mean ‘uncultivable land’. See
<div n="lb">{%bhūmicchidrapidhāna-nyāya, avanirandhra-nyāya.%}
