{%Kumār-āmātya%}¦ (IE 8-3; CII 3, 4; BL; HD), probably
<div n="lb">‘an {%Amātya%} enjoying the status of a {%Kumāra%}’. Cf. Tamil
<div n="lb">{%piḻḻaigaḻ-tanam%} (SITI), ‘the status of a {%Piḻḻai%} (prince)’, an
<div n="lb">officers’ cadre mainly composed of the junior members of
<div n="lb">the royal family. See {%Proc. 6th AIOC,%} pp. 211 ff.; Vogel,
<div n="lb">{%Ant. Ch. St.,%} p. 123.
<div n="P">({%IE%} 8-3), in one case, a {%Kumār-āmātya%} was at first a
<div n="lb">{%Mantrin,%} but later became a {%Mahābalādhikṛta;%} in another case,
<div n="lb">a {%Kumārāmātya%} was also a {%Mantrin.%} Cf. {%Kumāra-mahāpātra,%} etc.
<div n="P">{%Cf. Kumāra-varga=piḻḻaigaḻ-tanam%} (SITI), literally, ‘the
<div n="lb">status of the {%Piḻḻai%} or prince’; supposed to be an officers’ cadre
<div n="lb">composed mainly of the junior members of the royal family.
<div n="P">({%IE%} 8-3), also called {%Khādya(kū*)ṭapākika, Sāndhivigrahika,%}
<div n="lb">{%Mahādaṇdanāyaka,%} etc., additionally.
