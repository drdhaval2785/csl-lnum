<div n="lb">Ud xv.1; °nānusmṛti, id., ŚsP 60.8; on this and ŚsP
<div n="lb">1443.8 (where text ānāpā-nusmṛtir) see s.v. {@anusmṛti@}.
<div n="lb">The word is an old dvandva; āna (= prāṇa) plus apāna
<div n="lb">(cf. Skt. prāṇāpāna, on which see G.W. Brown, JAOS
<div n="lb">39.104 ff.). In Pali commentarial diction replaced by
<div n="lb">assāsa-passāsa (= {@āśvāsa-praśvāsa@}, q.v.). Tib. on
<div n="lb">Mvy 1166 ānāpāna (-smṛtiḥ) renders dbugs rṅub pa daṅ
<div n="lb">ḥbyuṅ ba, {%breathing in and out%}; the same or related terms
<div n="lb">are used for āśvāsa and praśvāsa. It is clear that Tib.
<div n="lb">understands āna = āśvāsa as {%inbreathing%}, apāna = pra-
<div n="lb">śvāsa as {%outbreathing%}. There is BHS evidence supporting
<div n="lb">this interpretation of {@āśvāsa-praśvāsa@}, q.v.
