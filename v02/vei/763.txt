{@Kauṣītaki@}¦ (‘descendant of Kuṣītaka’) is the patronymic of a
<div n="lb"/>teacher, or series of teachers, to whom the doctrines set forth
<div n="lb"/>in the Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> and in the Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyaka,<sup>2</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and the Śrauta and Gṛhya Sūtras,<sup>3</sup> are referred. He is rarely
<div n="lb"/>mentioned elsewhere.<sup>4</sup> The doctrine of Kauṣītaki is called the
<div n="lb"/>Kauṣītaka.<sup>5</sup> The pupils of Kauṣītaki are known as the Kauṣī-
<div n="lb"/>takis in the Nidāna Sūtra,<sup>6</sup> and in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>7</sup>

<div n="lb"/>they with Kuṣītaka are stated to have been cursed by {@Luśākapi.@}

<div n="lb"/>leading teachers, {@Kahoḍa@} and {@Sarvajit,@} the former of whom is
