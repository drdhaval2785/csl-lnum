{@Ekāṣṭakā.@}¦ — That Aṣṭakā is the eighth day after the full
<div n="lb"/>moon appears clearly from the Atharvaveda.<sup>1</sup> Ekāṣṭakā, or
<div n="lb"/>‘sole Aṣṭakā,’ must denote not merely any Aṣṭakā, but some
<div n="lb"/>particular one. Sāyaṇa, in his commentary on the Atharva-
<div n="lb"/>veda,<sup>2</sup> in which a whole hymn celebrates the Ekāṣṭakā, fixes
<div n="lb"/>the date meant by the term as the eighth day in the dark half
<div n="lb"/>of the month of Māgha (January — February). The Ekāṣṭakā,
<div n="lb"/>is declared in the Taittirīya Saṃhitā<sup>3</sup> to be the time for the
<div n="lb"/>consecration ({%dīkṣā%}) of those who are going to perform a year
<div n="lb"/>long sacrifice. See also {@Māsa.@}
