{@Viś@}¦ is an expression of somewhat doubtful significance. In
<div n="lb"/>many passages of the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> the sense of ‘settlement’ or
<div n="lb"/>‘dwelling’ is adequate and probable, since the root {%viś%} means
<div n="lb"/>to ‘enter’ or ‘settle.’ In other passages, where the Viśaḥ
[Page2-306+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>stand in relation to a prince, the term must mean ‘subject’;<sup>2</sup>
<div n="lb"/>so, for example, when the people of {@Tṛṇaskanda@}<sup>3</sup> or of the
<div n="lb"/>{@Tṛtsus@} are mentioned.<sup>4</sup> Again, in some passages<sup>5</sup> the general
<div n="lb"/>sense of ‘people’ is adequate; as when the Rigveda speaks of
<div n="lb"/>the ‘Āryan people,’<sup>6</sup> or the ‘divine people,’<sup>7</sup> or the ‘Dāsa
<div n="lb"/>people,’ and so on.<sup>8</sup>
<div n="P"/>Sometimes,<sup>9</sup> however, the Viś appear in a more special sense
<div n="lb"/>as a subdivision of the {@Jana@} or whole people. This is, how-
<div n="lb"/>ever, not common, for in most passages one or other of the
<div n="lb"/>senses given above is quite possible. Moreover, it is very
<div n="lb"/>difficult to decide whether the Viś as a subdivision of the Jana
<div n="lb"/>is to be considered as being a local subdivision (canton) or a
<div n="lb"/>blood kinship equivalent to a clan in the large sense of the
<div n="lb"/>word, while the relation of the Viś to the {@Grāma@} or to the
<div n="lb"/>{@Gotra@} is quite uncertain. In one passage of the Atharvaveda<sup>10</sup>
<div n="lb"/>the Viśaḥ are mentioned along with the {%sabandhavaḥ%} or relatives,
<div n="lb"/>but no definite conclusion can be drawn from that fact. Nor
<div n="lb"/>does the analogy of the Roman {%curia%} or the Greek <lang n="greek">φρήτρη</lang> throw
<div n="lb"/>much light, as these institutions are themselves of obscure
<div n="lb"/>character, and the parallelism need not be cogent. It is, at
<div n="lb"/>any rate, possible that the Viś may in some cases have been no
<div n="lb"/>more than a Gotra or clan, or different clans may sometimes
[Page2-307+ 72]
<div n="lb"/>have made up a Viś, while Grāma is more definitely, perhaps, a
<div n="lb"/>local designation. But the Vedic evidence is quite inconclusive.<sup>11</sup>
<div n="lb"/>{%Cf.%} {@Viśpati.@}
<div n="P"/>In the later period the sense of Viś is definitely restricted in
<div n="lb"/>some cases<sup>12</sup> to denote the third of the classes of the Vedic
<div n="lb"/>polity, the people or clansmen as opposed to the nobles ({@Kṣatra,@}
<div n="lb"/>{@Kṣatriya@}) and the priests ({@Brahman, Brāhmaṇa@}). For the
<div n="lb"/>position of this class, see {@Vaiśva.@}
