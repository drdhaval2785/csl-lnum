{@Jaṅgiḍa@}¦ is the name of a healing plant mentioned in the
<div n="lb"/>hymns of the Atharvaveda.<sup>1</sup> It was used as an amulet against
<div n="lb"/>the diseases, or symptoms of disease, {@Takman, Balāsa,@}
<div n="lb"/>{@Āśarīka, Viśarīka, Pṛṣṭyāmaya,@}<sup>2</sup> fevers and rheumatic pains,
<div n="lb"/>{@Viṣkandha@} and {@Saṃskandha,@}<sup>3</sup> {@Jambha,@} and so on. But it is
<div n="lb"/>also regarded as a specific against all diseases, and as the best
<div n="lb"/>of healing powers.<sup>4</sup> It is said to be produced from the juices
<div n="lb"/>({%rasa%}) of ploughing ({%kṛṣi%}),<sup>5</sup> but this need only mean that it grew
<div n="lb"/>in cultivated land, not that it was itself cultivated. What plant
<div n="lb"/>the name designates is quite uncertain, for it disappears in the
<div n="lb"/>later literature. Caland<sup>6</sup> takes it in the Kauśika Sūtra to be
<div n="lb"/>the {%Terminalia arjuneya.%}
