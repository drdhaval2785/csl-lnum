{@Niṣka@}¦ is frequently found in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and later<sup>2</sup> denoting
<div n="lb"/>a gold ornament worn on the neck, as is shown by the two
<div n="lb"/>epithets {%niṣka-kaṇṭha%}<sup>3</sup> and {%niṣka-grīva,%}<sup>4</sup> ‘having a gold ornament
<div n="lb"/>on the neck.’ A Niṣka of silver is mentioned in the Pañcaviṃśa
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa.<sup>5</sup> As early as the Rigveda<sup>6</sup> traces are seen of the
[Page1-455+ 42]
<div n="lb"/>use of Niṣkas as a sort of currency, for a singer celebrates the
<div n="lb"/>receipt of a hundred Niṣkas and a hundred steeds: he could
<div n="lb"/>hardly require the Niṣkas merely for purposes of personal
<div n="lb"/>adornment. Later the use of Niṣkas as currency is quite clear.<sup>7</sup>
