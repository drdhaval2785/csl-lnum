{@Ni-vid@}¦ denotes a brief invocation of the deity that is invited
<div n="lb"/>in a liturgy in honour of the god. The Brāhmaṇas<sup>1</sup> repeatedly
<div n="lb"/>mention Nivids as inserted in the Śastras (recitations), and the
<div n="lb"/>{@Khilas@} of the Rigveda<sup>2</sup> preserve among them a set of Nivids.
<div n="lb"/>But it is doubtful<sup>3</sup> whether the habit of using such brief
<div n="lb"/>formulas — the Nivid is usually not more than a Pada or
<div n="lb"/>quarter-verse in length — is known to the Rigveda, though it
<div n="lb"/>has been seen even there,<sup>4</sup> and the word Nivid is several times
<div n="lb"/>found in that Saṃhitā,<sup>5</sup> but hardly in the technical sense of
<div n="lb"/>the Brāhmaṇas. In the later Saṃhitās<sup>6</sup> the technical sense is
<div n="lb"/>common.
