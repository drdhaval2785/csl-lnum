<div n="lb"/>The term is, however, generally<sup>2</sup> applied to the team of the
<div n="lb"/>Maruts, when its sense is doubtful. The commentators usually
<div n="lb"/>explain it as ‘speckled antelope.’ But Mahīdhara,<sup>3</sup> followed by
<div n="lb"/>Roth,<sup>4</sup> prefers to see in it a ‘dappled mare’: it is true that the
<div n="lb"/>Maruts are often called<sup>5</sup> {%pṛṣad-aśva,%} which is more naturally
<div n="lb"/>interpreted as ‘having dappled steeds,’ than as ‘having Pṛṣatīs
<div n="lb"/>as steeds.’<sup>6</sup> In the later literature, which Grassmann<sup>7</sup> prefers
<div n="lb"/>to follow, the word means the female of the dappled gazelle.
