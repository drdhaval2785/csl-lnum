{@Karmāra,@}¦ the ‘smith,’ is several times mentioned with
<div n="lb"/>approval in the Vedic Saṃhitās.<sup>1</sup> In the Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> smiths
<div n="lb"/>appear with fishermen ({%dhīvānaḥ%}) and chariot-builders ({%ratha-%}
<div n="lb"/>{%kārāḥ%}), all being classified as clever workers ({%manīṣiṇaḥ%}):
<div n="lb"/>possibly a quasi-caste of smiths was already developing from
<div n="lb"/>the guild organization that probably existed.<sup>3</sup>
<div n="P"/>Little is known of the smith's methods of work and of his
<div n="lb"/>tools. No doubt he smelted ({%dhmā%}) the ore in the fire; hence
<div n="lb"/>he is called {%dhmātṛ,%} the ‘smelter.’<sup>4</sup> Mention is also made of
[Page1-141+ 50]
<div n="lb"/>his bellows of birds' feathers.<sup>5</sup> He made metal vessels ({%gharma%}
<div n="lb"/>{%ayasmaya%})<sup>6</sup> to be put on the fire: even the Soma cup could
<div n="lb"/>occasionally be made of hammered metal ({%ayo-hata%}).<sup>7</sup>
