<div n="lb"/>Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> and the Chāndogya Upaniṣad,<sup>2</sup> where he
<div n="lb"/>is teacher of the strange {@Kṛṣṇa Devakīputra.@} That the name
<div n="lb"/>is certainly a mere figment is shown by the fact that this
<div n="lb"/>‘dread descendant of the Aṅgirases’ has a counterpart in
<div n="lb"/>{@Bhiṣaj Ātharvaṇa,@}<sup>3</sup> ‘the healing descendant of the Atharvans,’
<div n="lb"/>while in the Rigveda Sūtras<sup>4</sup> the {%Atharvāṇo vedaḥ%} is connected

<div n="lb"/>with {%bheṣajam%} and the {%Āṅgiraso vedaḥ%} with {%ghoram.%} He is
<div n="lb"/>accordingly a personification of the dark side of the practice
<div n="lb"/>of the Atharvaveda.<sup>5</sup> He is also mentioned in the Aśvamedha
<div n="lb"/>section of the Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā.<sup>6</sup>
