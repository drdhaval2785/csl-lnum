{@Divodāsa Atithigva@}¦ is one of the leading princes of the
<div n="lb"/>early Vedic age. He was a son of Vadhryaśva,<sup>1</sup> and father,
<div n="lb"/>or more probably grandfather, of {@Sudās,@} the famous king of the
<div n="lb"/>{@Tṛtsu@} family, among the {@Bharatas.@} Probably Pijavana was
<div n="lb"/>the son and Sudās the grandson. Divodāsa was naturally a
<div n="lb"/>{@Bharata,@}<sup>2</sup> and, like Sudās, was an opponent of the {@Turvaśas@}
<div n="lb"/>and {@Yadus.@}<sup>3</sup> His great enemy was {@Śambara,@} the {@Dāsa,@} who
<div n="lb"/>was apparently chief of a mountain people,<sup>4</sup> and whom he
<div n="lb"/>repeatedly defeated.<sup>5</sup> He was also, it seems, like his father
<div n="lb"/>Vadhryaśva,<sup>6</sup> an energetic supporter of the fire ritual, for Agni
<div n="lb"/>is once called by his name in the Rigveda.<sup>7</sup> On the other hand,
<div n="lb"/>he was defeated, with {@Āyu@} and {@Kutsa,@} by Indra's aid. In
<div n="lb"/>several passages he seems closely connected with the singer
<div n="lb"/>family, the {@Bharadvājas.@}<sup>8</sup>
<div n="P"/>From one passage,<sup>9</sup> where Divodāsa is said to have fought
<div n="lb"/>against the {@Paṇis,@} the {@Pārāvatas,@} and {@Bṛsaya,@} Hillebrandt<sup>10</sup>
<div n="lb"/>has inferred that he was engaged in conflicts with the tribes
<div n="lb"/>of Arachosia, and interpreting the name as the ‘heavenly
<div n="lb"/>Dāsa’<sup>11</sup> conjectures that he was himself a Dāsa. This con-
<div n="lb"/>clusion is not probable, for the {@Sarasvatī@} on which the battle
<div n="lb"/>in question took place, and which can hardly be the Haraqaiti
<div n="lb"/>of Arachosia, would naturally designate the later Sarasvatī,
<div n="lb"/>while the Pārāvatas are mentioned in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāh-
<div n="lb"/>maṇa,<sup>12</sup> as in the east, about the {@Yamunā.@} Bergaigne's
[Page1-364+ 60]
<div n="lb"/>opinion<sup>13</sup> that Divodāsa and Atithigva were different people
<div n="lb"/>cannot be supported in view of the complete parallelism in the
<div n="lb"/>acts of the two persons.<sup>14</sup> See also {@Pratardana.@}
