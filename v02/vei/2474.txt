<div n="lb"/>the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> and later.<sup>2</sup> It is still one of the most valuable
<div n="lb"/>of similar plants in India. The seeds<sup>3</sup> were pounded ({%piṣṭa%})
<div n="lb"/>according to the Atharvaveda.<sup>4</sup> These beans ripened in the
<div n="lb"/>winter ({%hemanta%}).<sup>5</sup> In the ritual the human head for the
<div n="lb"/>sacrifice is bought for twenty-one Māṣas<sup>6</sup> : it does not appear
<div n="lb"/>that the word here means a weight of metal, as it often does
<div n="lb"/>later.<sup>7</sup> A taboo on beans is found in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās.<sup>8</sup>
