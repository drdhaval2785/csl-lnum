<L>3355<pc>2-397<k1>SmaSAna<k2>SmaSAna
{@Śmaśāna@}¦ is the name of the ‘burial mound’ in which the
<div n="lb"/>bones of the dead man were laid to rest ({%cf.%} {@Anagnidagdha@}).
<div n="lb"/>It is mentioned in the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup> and often later.<sup>2</sup> The
<div n="lb"/>Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup> prescribes a four-cornered mound facing
<div n="lb"/>the south-east, on ground inclined to the north, out of sight of
<div n="lb"/>the village, in a peaceful spot amid beautiful surroundings, or
<div n="lb"/>on barren ground. For an Agni-cit (‘builder of a fire-latar’)
<div n="lb"/>a funeral mound like a fire-altar is prescribed. The Easterners
<div n="lb"/>({%Prācyāḥ%}) made their mounds round.
