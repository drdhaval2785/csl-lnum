{@Rāja-kartṛ,@}¦<sup>1</sup> or {@Rāja-kṛt,@}<sup>2</sup> ‘king-maker,’ is the term applied
<div n="lb"/>in the Atharvaveda and the Brāhmaṇas to those who, ‘not
<div n="lb"/>themselves kings,’<sup>3</sup> aid in the consecration of the king. In the
<div n="lb"/>Śatapatha<sup>2</sup> the persons meant and specified are the {@Sūta,@}
<div n="lb"/>‘charioteer,’ and the {@Grāmaṇī,@} ‘village chief,’ probably a repre-
<div n="lb"/>sentative chief from the village nearest to the place of consecra-
<div n="lb"/>tion, as Eggeling<sup>4</sup> suggests. In the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa,<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>according to the commentator's explanation, the father,
<div n="lb"/>brother, etc., are meant; in the Atharvaveda,<sup>2</sup> also, the
<div n="lb"/>meaning of the expression is not stated in the text.
