{@Daṇḍa,@}¦ ‘staff.’ ({%a%}) This word is often mentioned in the
<div n="lb"/>ordinary sense; for example, when used for driving cattle<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>({%go-ajanāsaḥ%}), or as a weapon.<sup>2</sup> A staff was given to a man
<div n="lb"/>on consecration for driving away demons, according to the
<div n="lb"/>Satapatha Brāhmaṇa.<sup>3</sup> The staff also played a part in the
<div n="lb"/>initiation ({%upanayana%}) of a youth on attaining manhood.<sup>4</sup> In
<div n="lb"/>a modified sense the word is used to denote the handle of a
<div n="lb"/>ladle or similar implement.<sup>5</sup>

<div n="P"/>({%b%}) The ‘staff’ as the symbol of temporal power, implying
<div n="lb"/>punishment, is applied by the king ({%rāja-preṣito daṇḍaḥ%}).<sup>1</sup> The
<div n="lb"/>king, in modern phraseology, was the source of criminal law;
<div n="lb"/>and he clearly retained this branch of law in his own hands
<div n="lb"/>even in later times.<sup>2</sup> The punishment of the non-guilty
<div n="lb"/>({%a-daṇḍya%}) is given as one of the characteristics of the non-
<div n="lb"/>Brahminical {@Vrātyas@} in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa.<sup>3</sup> See
<div n="lb"/>also {@Dharma.@}
