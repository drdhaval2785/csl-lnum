{@Kaṇva@}¦ is the name of an ancient Ṛṣi repeatedly referred to
<div n="lb"/>in the Rigveda and later.<sup>1</sup> His sons and descendants,<sup>2</sup> the
<div n="lb"/>Kaṇvas, are also often mentioned, especially in the eighth
<div n="lb"/>book of the Rigveda, the authorship of that book, as well as of
<div n="lb"/>part of the first, being attributed to this family. A descendant
<div n="lb"/>of Kaṇva is also denoted by the name in the singular, either
<div n="lb"/>alone<sup>3</sup> or accompanied by a patronymic, as Kaṇva Nārṣada<sup>4</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and Kaṇva Śrāyasa,<sup>5</sup> besides in the plural the Kaṇvas Sauśra-
<div n="lb"/>vasas.<sup>6</sup> The Kaṇva family appears to have been connected with
<div n="lb"/>the Atri family,<sup>7</sup> but not to have been of great importance.<sup>8</sup> In
<div n="lb"/>one passage of the Atharvaveda<sup>9</sup> they seem to be definitely
<div n="lb"/>regarded with hostility.
