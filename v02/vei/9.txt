{@Akṣu.@}¦ — The word occurs in two passages of the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and one of the Rigveda.<sup>2</sup> Roth<sup>3</sup> renders it by ‘net,’ while
<div n="lb"/>Böhtlingk<sup>4</sup> suggests ‘axle of a car.’ Geldner<sup>5</sup> sees in it a stake
<div n="lb"/>or pole used with a fishermen's net ({@Jāla@}),<sup>6</sup> the pole of a wagon,<sup>7</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and the pole of a house, whether vertical or horizontal, he leaves
<div n="lb"/>uncertain (see {@Vaṃśa@}).<sup>8</sup> Bloomfield<sup>9</sup> takes it as a covering
<div n="lb"/>of wickerwork stretched across a beam and sloping down to
<div n="lb"/>both sides — like a thatched roof, and this best explains the
<div n="lb"/>epithet ‘thousand-eyed’ ({%i.e.,%} with countless holes) ascribed to
<div n="lb"/>it. In the other Atharvaveda passage<sup>10</sup> he accepts the sense
<div n="lb"/>‘net,’ and doubts if the word in the Rigveda is not an adjective
<div n="lb"/>({%a-kṣu%}) as it is taken by Sāyaṇa. See also {@Gṛha.@}
