{@Yamunā,@}¦ ‘twin,’ the name of a river, so called as running
<div n="lb"/>parallel with the Ganges, is mentioned thrice in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and not rarely later. According to the Rigveda,<sup>2</sup> the {@Tṛtsus@} and
<div n="lb"/>{@Sudās@} won a great victory against their foes on the Yamunā;
<div n="lb"/>there is no reason<sup>3</sup> whatever to accept Hopkins'<sup>4</sup> view that the
<div n="lb"/>Yamunā here was another name of the {@Paruṣṇī@} (Ravi). In the
<div n="lb"/>Atharvaveda<sup>5</sup> the salve ({@Āñjana@}) of the Yamunā ({%Yāmuna%}) is
<div n="lb"/>mentioned along with that of {@Trikakud@} ({%Traikakuda%}) as of value.
<div n="lb"/>In the Aitareya<sup>6</sup> and the Śatapatha<sup>7</sup> Brāhmaṇas the {@Bharatas@}
<div n="lb"/>are famed as victorious on the Yamunā. Other Brāhmaṇas<sup>8</sup>
[Page2-187+ 62]
<div n="lb"/>also mention this river. In the Mantrapāṭha<sup>9</sup> the {@Sālvas@} are
<div n="lb"/>spoken of as dwelling on its banks.
