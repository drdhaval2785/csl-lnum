{@Pra-pitva@}¦ is found in several passages of the Rigveda as a
<div n="lb"/>designation of time. In one passage<sup>1</sup> the sense is made clear
<div n="lb"/>by the context: ‘at the rising of the sun’ ({%sūra udite%}), ‘at mid-
<div n="lb"/>day’ ({%madhyaṃdine divaḥ%}), and ‘at the Prapitva, bordering on
<div n="lb"/>the night’ ({%apiśarvare%}). In another passage<sup>2</sup> the sense of ‘late
<div n="lb"/>in the day’ also seems adequate, while the phrase<sup>3</sup> {%abhipitve%}
<div n="lb"/>{%ahnaḥ,%} ‘at the close of day,’ also denotes the evening. According
<div n="lb"/>to Geldner,<sup>4</sup> the sense of the word is the ‘decisive moment’ in
<div n="lb"/>a race or a battle, and so the ‘end of the day.’<sup>5</sup> {%Cf.%} {@Ahan.@}
