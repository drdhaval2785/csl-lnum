{@Upa-śrī, Upa-śraya,@}¦ are two readings of the same term. The
<div n="lb"/>former is found in one recension of the Kauṣītaki Upaniṣad,<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>while the latter is probably the reading of the other recension
<div n="lb"/>of the Upaniṣad,<sup>2</sup> and certainly the reading in one passage of
<div n="lb"/>the Atharvaveda,<sup>3</sup> though the text has {%apaśrayaḥ,%} which is
<div n="lb"/>accepted as possible by Roth.<sup>4</sup> In both cases the term clearly
<div n="lb"/>means something connected with a couch ({@Āsandī@} in the
<div n="lb"/>Atharvaveda, {@Paryaṅka@} in the Kauṣītaki Upaniṣad). Aufrecht,<sup>5</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Roth,<sup>6</sup> and Max Müller<sup>7</sup> render it as ‘coverlet’ or ‘cushion,’
<div n="lb"/>but Whitney<sup>8</sup> seems evidently right in holding that it must
<div n="lb"/>mean a ‘support’ or something similar.
