{@Āji@}¦ is constantly used in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and the later
<div n="lb"/>literature to express the sense of ‘a race,’ and only seldom
<div n="lb"/>denotes ‘a battle.’ {@Horse@}-racing was one of the favourite
<div n="lb"/>amusements of the {@Vedic@} Indian,<sup>2</sup> the other being dicing
[Page1-054+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>({@Akṣa@}). The racecourse, called Kāṣṭhā<sup>3</sup> or Āji<sup>4</sup> itself, appears
<div n="lb"/>from the Atharvaveda<sup>5</sup> to have been a quasi-circular one to
<div n="lb"/>a mark ({%kārṣman%}<sup>6</sup> ) and back again. In the Rigveda the
<div n="lb"/>course is described as broad ({%urvī%}) and the distance as
<div n="lb"/>measured out ({%apāvṛktā aratnayaḥ%}).<sup>7</sup> Prizes ({%dhana%}) were
<div n="lb"/>offered ({%dhā%}),<sup>8</sup> and eagerly competed for. Other words for
<div n="lb"/>victory and the prize are {%kāra%}<sup>9</sup> and {%bhara;%}<sup>10</sup> and to ‘run a
<div n="lb"/>race’ is described by the expressions {%ājim aj, i, dhāv, sṛ.%}<sup>11</sup>
<div n="lb"/>The person who instituted a race is referred to as {%āji-sṛt,%}<sup>12</sup> and
<div n="lb"/>Indra is called {%āji-kṛt%}<sup>13</sup> (‘race-maker’), and {%āji-pati%}<sup>14</sup> (‘lord
<div n="lb"/>of the race’).
<div n="P"/>The swift steeds ({%vājin, atya%}) used for the races were often
<div n="lb"/>washed and adorned.<sup>15</sup> According to Pischel<sup>16</sup> the name of
<div n="lb"/>one swift mare is preserved — viz., Viśpalā,<sup>17</sup> whose broken leg
<div n="lb"/>was replaced by the Aśvins in a race; but the interpretation
<div n="lb"/>is very doubtful. Geldner<sup>18</sup> has also found a comic picture of
<div n="lb"/>a horse-chariot race in the Mudgala hymn in the Rigveda,
<div n="lb"/>but Bloomfield<sup>19</sup> has shown that that interpretation is un-
<div n="lb"/>sound. Pischel<sup>20</sup> also seeks to show that races were run in
<div n="lb"/>honour of gods, but the evidence for the theory is inadequate.<sup>21</sup>
[Page1-055+ 46]
<div n="lb"/>A formal race, however, is a feature of the ritual of the Rājasūya
<div n="lb"/>or royal consecration.<sup>22</sup>
