{@Jātū-karṇya,@}¦ ‘descendant of {@Jātūkarṇa,@}’ is the patronymic
<div n="lb"/>of several persons.
<div n="P"/>({%a%}) A pupil of {@Āsurāyaṇa@} and {@Yāska@} bears this name in
<div n="lb"/>a Vaṃśa (list of teachers) of the Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad in
<div n="lb"/>the Kāṇva recension.<sup>1</sup> In the Mādhyaṃdina<sup>2</sup> he is a pupil
<div n="lb"/>of {@Bhāradvāja.@}
<div n="P"/>({%b%}) A Kātyāyanī-putra, ‘son of Kātyāyanī,’ bears this name
<div n="lb"/>in the Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyaka.<sup>3</sup>
<div n="P"/>({%c%}) A Jātūkarṇya is mentioned in the Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa<sup>4</sup>
<div n="lb"/>as a contemporary of {@Alīkayu Vācaspatya@} and other sages.
<div n="P"/>({%d%}) Jātūkarṇya is in the Sūtras<sup>5</sup> frequently a patronymic of
<div n="lb"/>teachers whose identity cannot be determined. The same
<div n="lb"/>person or different persons may here be meant.
