<L>303<pc>1-074<k1>AsrAva<k2>AsrAva
{@Ā-srāva@}¦ (‘discharge’) denotes a disease, thrice referred to in
<div n="lb"/>the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup> the precise nature of which is uncertain.
<div n="lb"/>The Scholiast<sup>2</sup> in one place interprets it as painful urination
<div n="lb"/>({%mūtrātisāra%}), while Lanman<sup>3</sup> suggests diabetes. Bloomfield<sup>4</sup>
<div n="lb"/>takes it to be diarrhœa, and Zimmer<sup>5</sup> argues that as the remedy
<div n="lb"/>is called ‘wound healer’ ({%arus-srāṇa%}), the sense is ‘the flux from
<div n="lb"/>unhealed wounds.’ Whitney<sup>6</sup> renders it ‘flux,’ and questions
<div n="lb"/>Bloomfield's rendering. Ludwig<sup>7</sup> translates it vaguely by ‘sick-
<div n="lb"/>ness’ and ‘cold.’
