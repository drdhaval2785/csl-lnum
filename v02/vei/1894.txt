{@Puṣkara@}¦ is the name in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and later<sup>2</sup> of the blue
<div n="lb"/>lotus flower. The Atharvaveda<sup>3</sup> mentions its sweet perfume.
<div n="lb"/>The lotus grew in lakes, which were thence called {%puṣkariṇī,%}
<div n="lb"/>‘lotus-bearing.’<sup>4</sup> That the flower was early used for personal
<div n="lb"/>adornment is shown by an epithet of the Aśvins, ‘lotus-
<div n="lb"/>crowned’ ({%puṣkara-sraj%}).<sup>5</sup>
<div n="P"/>Presumably because of its likeness in shape to the flower
<div n="lb"/>of the lotus, the bowl of the ladle is called Puṣkara, perhaps
<div n="lb"/>already in the Rigveda,<sup>6</sup> and certainly in the Aitareya Brāh-
<div n="lb"/>maṇa.<sup>7</sup> Moreover, according to the Nirukta,<sup>8</sup> Puṣkara means
<div n="lb"/>‘water,’ a sense actually found in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa.<sup>9</sup>
