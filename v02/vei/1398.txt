{@Drapsa@}¦ is a common word from the Rigveda onwards<sup>1</sup> for
<div n="lb"/>a ‘drop’: according to Sāyaṇa,<sup>2</sup> a ‘thick drop’ as opposed to
<div n="lb"/>{%stoka,%} a ‘small drop.’ Hence there frequently occurs the ex-
<div n="lb"/>pression {%dadḥi-drapsa,%}’ ‘drop of curds.’<sup>3</sup> In the Rigveda<sup>4</sup> the word
<div n="lb"/>normally denotes the thick drops of Soma or the Soma itself.
<div n="P"/>In two passages<sup>5</sup> Roth<sup>6</sup> sees the sense of ‘banner,’ which is
<div n="lb"/>adopted by Oldenberg.<sup>7</sup> Geldner,<sup>8</sup> on the other hand, con-
<div n="lb"/>siders that ‘dust’ is meant, but this interpretation is not very
<div n="lb"/>probable. Max Müller<sup>9</sup> renders the word ‘rain-drop’ in one
<div n="lb"/>of the passages.
