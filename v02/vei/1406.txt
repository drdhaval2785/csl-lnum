{@Druhyu@}¦ is the name of a people mentioned several times in
<div n="lb"/>the Rigveda. In one passage<sup>1</sup> it occurs, in the plural, with the
<div n="lb"/>{@Yadus, Turvaśas, Anus,@} and {@Pūrus,@} suggesting that these are
<div n="lb"/>the famous five peoples of the Rigveda.<sup>2</sup> Again, the Druhyu
<div n="lb"/>king shared in the defeat of his allies by {@Sudās,@} and appears to
<div n="lb"/>have perished in the waters.<sup>3</sup> In a second passage Druhyu,
<div n="lb"/>Anu, Turvaśa, and Yadu are all mentioned in the singular,<sup>4</sup>
<div n="lb"/>while in another Pūru and Druhyu occur.<sup>5</sup> From the tribal
<div n="lb"/>grouping it is probable that the Druhyus were a north-western
<div n="lb"/>people,<sup>6</sup> and the later tradition of the Epic connects Gāndhāra
<div n="lb"/>and Druhyu.<sup>7</sup>
