{@Pra-stotṛ@}¦ is the name of an assistant of the Udgātṛ priest
<div n="lb"/>who sings the Prastāva,<sup>1</sup> or prelude of the Sāman chant. His
<div n="lb"/>not being mentioned by name in the Rigveda is merely an
<div n="lb"/>accident, for he is clearly referred to in one passage,<sup>2</sup> and in the
<div n="lb"/>later literature<sup>3</sup> he is a frequent figure. Ludwig<sup>4</sup> erroneously
<div n="lb"/>thinks that {@Praśāstṛ@} is the earlier name of the Prastotṛ.
<F>1) Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa, xii. 10, 7;
<div n="lb"/>Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, iii. 23; Śatapatha
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa, viii. 7, 4, 6; Chāndogya
<div n="lb"/>Upaniṣad, i. 10, 9; ii. 2, 1, etc.</F>
<F>2) viii. 81, 5 ({%pra stoṣat%}). See Olden-
<div n="lb"/>berg, {%Religion des Veda,%} 393, n. 3.</F>
<F>3) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, iii. 3, 2, 1;
<div n="lb"/>vi. 6, 3, 1; Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa, i. 8,
<div n="lb"/>2, 3; Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, v. 34; vii. 1;
<div n="lb"/>Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, iv. 2, 5, 3; v. 4,
<div n="lb"/>5, 22; xii. 1, 1, 6, etc.; Chāndogya
<div n="lb"/>Upaniṣad, i. 10, 8, etc.</F>
