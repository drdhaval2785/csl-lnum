{@Carman,@}¦ denoting ‘hide’ in general, is a common expression
<div n="lb"/>from the Rigveda onwards.<sup>1</sup> The oxhide was turned to many
<div n="lb"/>uses, such as the manufacture of bowstrings, slings, and reins
<div n="lb"/>(see {@Go@}). It was especially often employed to place above the
<div n="lb"/>boards<sup>2</sup> on which the Soma was pressed with the stones.<sup>3</sup> It
<div n="lb"/>was possibly also used for making skin bags.<sup>4</sup> Carmaṇya
<div n="lb"/>denotes leather-work generally in the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa.<sup>5</sup>
<div n="P"/>The art of tanning hides ({%mlā%}) was known as early as the
<div n="lb"/>Rigveda,<sup>6</sup> where also the word for ‘tanner’ ({%carmamna%}) occurs.<sup>7</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Details of the process are lacking, but the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>8</sup>
<div n="lb"/>refers to stretching out a hide with pegs ({%śañkubhiḥ%}), and the
<div n="lb"/>Rigveda<sup>9</sup> mentions the wetting of the hide.
