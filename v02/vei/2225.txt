{@Brāhmaṇāc-chaṃsin@}¦ (‘reciting after the Brāhmaṇa — {%i.e.,%}
<div n="lb"/>Brahman’) is the name of a priest in the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>1</sup> In the
<div n="lb"/>technical division of the sacrificial priests ({@Ṛtvij@}) he is classed
<div n="lb"/>with the Brahman,<sup>2</sup> but it is clear that he was really a Hotraka
<div n="lb"/>or assistant of the Hotṛ.<sup>3</sup> According to Oldenberg,<sup>4</sup> he was
<div n="lb"/>known to the Rigveda as {@Brahman.@} This is denied by
<div n="lb"/>Geldner,<sup>5</sup> who sees in Brahman merely the ‘superintending
<div n="lb"/>priest’ or the ‘priest.’
