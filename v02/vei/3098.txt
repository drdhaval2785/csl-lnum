<div n="lb"/>Puruṣamedha (‘human sacrifice’) in the Yajurveda,<sup>1</sup> is of
<div n="lb"/>uncertain signification. According to Sāyaṇa,<sup>2</sup> the compound
<div n="lb"/>denotes a ‘driver out of cows.’ Perhaps it means a ‘tormentor
<div n="lb"/>of cows,’ as the St. Petersburg Dictionary takes it. Weber<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>renders it as ‘knacker of cows,’ Eggeling as ‘one who
<div n="lb"/>approaches cows.’
