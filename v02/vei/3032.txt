2. {@Vṛśa Jāna@}¦ (‘descendant of Jana’) is the name of a famous
<div n="lb"/>Purohita, who was unfortunate enough, while with his royal
<div n="lb"/>master, {@Tryaruṇa,@} to see a boy killed by the chariot which the
<div n="lb"/>king drove too fast. He thereupon recalled the boy to life.
<div n="lb"/>The story is told briefly in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa,<sup>1</sup> the
<div n="lb"/>Śāṭyāyanaka,<sup>2</sup> the Tāṇḍaka,<sup>3</sup> was also narrated in the Bhāllavi
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa,<sup>4</sup> and is preserved in the Bṛhaddevatā,<sup>5</sup> Sieg<sup>6</sup> has
<div n="lb"/>endeavoured to trace the story in part in the Rigveda,<sup>7</sup> but
<div n="lb"/>there is a consensus of opinion<sup>8</sup> against the correctness of
<div n="lb"/>such a view.
