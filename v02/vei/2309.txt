<div n="lb"/>slaying of an embryo,’ are terms expressing a crime which is
<div n="lb"/>repeatedly and severely censured in the later Saṃhitās,<sup>1</sup> where
<div n="lb"/>it is said to be the greatest of all crimes, and one of which the

<div n="lb"/>guilt cannot be removed. In many later passages<sup>2</sup> the same
<div n="lb"/>crime is referred to, always with reprobation: this fact alone
<div n="lb"/>shows the erroneousness of the theory<sup>3</sup> that daughters could be
<div n="lb"/>allowed, once born, to die if their fathers so desired.
