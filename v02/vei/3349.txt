{@Śaunaka,@}¦ ‘descendant of Śunaka,’ is a common patronymic.
<div n="lb"/>It is applied to {@Indrota@}<sup>1</sup> and {@Svaidāyana.@}<sup>2</sup> A Śaunaka appears
<div n="lb"/>as a teacher of {@Rauhiṇāyana@} in the Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad.<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>A Śaunaka-yajña, or Śaunaka sacrifice, occurs in the Kauṣitaki
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa.<sup>4</sup> In the Chāndogya Upaniṣad<sup>5</sup> {@Atidhanvan@}
<div n="lb"/>Śaunaka appears as a teacher. That Upaniṣad<sup>6</sup> and the
<div n="lb"/>Jaiminīya Upaniṣad Brāhmaṇa<sup>7</sup> mention a Śaunaka Kāpeya
<div n="lb"/>who was a contemporary of {@Abhipratārin Kākṣaseni,@} whose
<div n="lb"/>Purohita Śaunaka was according to another passage<sup>8</sup> of the
<div n="lb"/>latter Upaniṣad. In the Sūtras, the Bṛhaddevatā, etc., a
<div n="lb"/>Śaunaka appears as a great authority on grammatical, ritual,
<div n="lb"/>and other matters.<sup>9</sup>
