{@Vaira@}¦<sup>1</sup> and {@Vaira-deya@}<sup>2</sup> seem to have in the later Saṃhitās
<div n="lb"/>and the Brāhmaṇas the definite and technical sense of ‘wergeld,’
<div n="lb"/>the money to be paid for killing a man as a compensation to
<div n="lb"/>his relatives. This view is borne out by the Sūtras of Āpa-
<div n="lb"/>stamba<sup>3</sup> and Baudhāyana.<sup>4</sup> Both prescribe the scale of
<div n="lb"/>1,000 cows for a {@Kṣatriya,@}<sup>5</sup> 100 for a {@Vaiśya,@} 10 for a {@Śūdra,@}
<div n="lb"/>and a bull over and above in each case. Āpastamba leaves the
<div n="lb"/>destination of the payment vague, but Baudhāyana assigns it
<div n="lb"/>to the king. It is reasonable to suppose that the cows were
<div n="lb"/>intended for the relations, and the bull was a present to the
<div n="lb"/>king for his intervention to induce the injured relatives to
<div n="lb"/>abandon the demand for the life of the offender. The Āpa-
<div n="lb"/>stamba Sūtra<sup>6</sup> allows the same scale of wergeld for women, but
<div n="lb"/>the Gautama Sūtra<sup>7</sup> puts them on a level with men of the
<div n="lb"/>Śūdra caste only, except in one special case. The payment is
<div n="lb"/>made for the purpose of {%vaira-yātana%} or {%vaira-niryātana,%} ‘requital
<div n="lb"/>of enmity,’ ‘expiation.’
<div n="P"/>The Rigveda<sup>8</sup> preserves, also, the important notice that a
<div n="lb"/>man's wergeld was a hundred (cows), for it contains the epithet
<div n="lb"/>{%śata-dāya,%} ‘one whose wergeld is a hundred.’ No doubt the
<div n="lb"/>values varied, but in the case of {@Śunaḥśepa@} the amount is a
<div n="lb"/>hundred (cows) in the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa.<sup>9</sup> In the Yajurveda
<div n="lb"/>Saṃhitās<sup>10</sup> {%śata-dāya%} again appears.

<div n="P"/>The fixing of the price shows that already public opinion, and

<div n="lb"/>of the system shows how weak was the criminal authority of
<div n="lb"/>the king ({%cf.%} {@Dharma@}).
