AṄGADA I¦. A son of Bāli.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu in the following
<div n="lb"/>order: Brahmā-Kaśyapa-Indra-Bāli-Aṅgada.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Birth.%} Aṅgada was the son of Bāli (the son of
<div n="lb"/>Indra) born of his wife Tārā. (Mahābhārata, Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 82, Stanza 28).
<div n="NI"/>a) Aṅgada was a member of the group of monkeys
<div n="lb"/>sent by Sugrīva to find out Sītā. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa,
<div n="lb"/>Araṇyakāṇḍa).
<div n="NI"/>b) He was the foremost among the group of monkeys
<div n="lb"/>who entered Madhuvana and ate the berries in the
<div n="lb"/>garden, on their return after the search for Sītā.
<div n="NI"/>c) Aṅgada was sent to the court of Rāvaṇa as a
<div n="lb"/>messenger by Śrī Rāma. (Rāmāyaṇa, Yuddhakāṇḍa).
<div n="NI"/>d) In the battle between Rāma and Rāvaṇa Aṅgada
<div n="lb"/>combated with Indrajit. (Rāmāyaṇa, Yuddhakāṇḍa).
<div n="NI"/>e) After his combat with Indrajit, Aṅgada and his
<div n="lb"/>followers led an attack on the army of Rāvaṇa. (Rāmā-
<div n="lb"/>yaṇa, Yuddhakāṇḍa).
<div n="NI"/>f) After the battle, Śrī Rāma anointed Aṅgada as the
<div n="lb"/>heir-apparent to the Kingdom of Kiṣkindhā. The neck-
<div n="lb"/>lace which Bāli had given on his death to Sugrīva, was
<div n="lb"/>returned to Aṅgada. (Uttara Rāmāyaṇa).
<div n="NI"/>g) Śrī Rāma returned to Ayodhyā after his forest
<div n="lb"/>life and celebrated a horse sacrifice. The sacrificial
<div n="lb"/>horse was caught and detained by King Suratha. Com-
<div n="lb"/>ing to know of this Śatrughna sent Aṅgada to deal
<div n="lb"/>with Suratha, who said that the horse was detained
<div n="lb"/>with the intention of meeting with Śrī Rāma personally.
<div n="lb"/>Aṅgada returned and told Śatrughna what Suratha had
<div n="lb"/>said to him. (Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla Khaṇḍa).
