KUNTIBHOJA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A King of the Yadu dynasty; son of the
<div n="lb"/>sister of Śūrasena, who was the father of Vasudeva and
<div n="lb"/>grandfather of Śrī Kṛṣṇa. (For genealogy see under
<div n="lb"/>Śrī Kṛṣṇa). Kuntibhoja was also the foster-father of
<div n="lb"/>Kuntī, the daughter of Śūrasena. (See Para 1, under
<div n="lb"/>Kuntī 1).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%} (i) Sahadeva, during his
<div n="lb"/>triumphal march over the southern kingdoms subjugat-
<div n="lb"/>ed Kuntibhoja. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 31, Verse
<div n="lb"/>16).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He participated in the Rājasūya Yajña of Yudhi-
<div n="lb"/>ṣṭhira. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 34, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) The son of Kuntibhoja also became famous under
<div n="lb"/>the same name, and Purujit was the son of this
<div n="lb"/>Kuntibhoja. Both of them were uncles of the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 6, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) On the first day of the Kurukṣetra war Kunti-
<div n="lb"/>bhoja and his sons fought with Vinda and Anuvinda.
<div n="lb"/>(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45, Verse 72),
<div n="NI"/>(v) It was Kuntibhoja who occupied the netrasthāna
<div n="lb"/>(eye-position) of the Krauñcavyūha set up by
<div n="lb"/>Dhṛṣṭadyumna. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 50, Verse
<div n="lb"/>47).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Kuntibhoja and Śatānīka occupied the Pāda-
<div n="lb"/>sthāna (foot position) of the Makaravyūha on the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍava side. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 75, Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) He possessed a noble and high-bred horse.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 23, Verse 46).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) In the great war he fought with Alambuṣa.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 16, Verse 183).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Ten of his children were killed by Aśvatthāmā.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 96, Verse 18).
