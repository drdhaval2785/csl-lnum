ASITADEVALA¦.
<div n="NI"/>{%Main details.%} (1) He is one of those who spread the story
<div n="lb"/>of Mahābhārata to the public. Vyāsa wrote the Mahā-
<div n="lb"/>bhārata into a lakh of books and first taught the great
<div n="lb"/>epic to his son, the sage Śuka. He later expounded it
<div n="lb"/>to many other disciples. Nārada spread the story among
<div n="lb"/>the devas, Devala among the Pitṛs, Śuka among
<div n="lb"/>Gandharvas and Yakṣas and Vaiśampāyana among men.
<div n="lb"/>(Ślokas 107 & 108, Chapter 1, Ādi Parva, M.B.).
<div n="NI"/>(2) He was present at the Sarpasatra of Janamejaya.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 8, Chapter 53, Ādi Parva, M.B.).

<div n="NI"/>(3) He attended the coronation of Yudhiṣṭhira along
