SUDAKṢIṆA¦. II. A king of Kāmboja (Kabul). The
<div n="lb"/>following information is taken from Mahābhārata
<div n="lb"/>about this king.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Sudakṣiṇa had been present at the Svayaṃvara
<div n="lb"/>marriage of Draupadī. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 185,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 15).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He came with an akṣauhiṇī of army to help
<div n="lb"/>Duryodhana against the Pāṇḍavas in the battle of
<div n="lb"/>Bhārata. (M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 19, Verse
<div n="lb"/>21).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Sudakṣiṇa was a valiant fighter in the army of
[Page750-b+ 63]
<div n="lb"/>the Kauravas. (M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 160,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 1).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) On the first day of the battle of Bhārata. there
<div n="lb"/>was a combat between Śrutakarmā and Sudakṣiṇa.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45, Verse 66).
<div n="NI"/>(v) There was another combat between Abhimanyu
<div n="lb"/>and Sudakṣiṇa. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 66).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Arjuna killed Sudakṣiṇa in the battle of Bhārata.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 92, Verse 61).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) The younger brother of Sudakṣiṇa confronted
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna to avenge the death of his brother and was
<div n="lb"/>killed by Arjuna. (M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 56,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 110).
