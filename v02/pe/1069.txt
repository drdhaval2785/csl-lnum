BHĀRATA II¦. (Bhāratavarṣa. India).
<div n="NI"/>The purāṇas describe the Earth to be constituted of
<div n="lb"/>seven continents: Jambūdvīpa, Plakṣadvīpa, Śālmalī-
<div n="lb"/>dvīpa, Kuśadvīpa, Krauñcadvīpa, Śākadvīpa and
<div n="lb"/>Puṣkaradvīpa. Of these India is Jambūdvīpa.
<div n="NI"/>The Bhāratavarṣa was constituted of nine islands at
<div n="lb"/>that time in the following positions: In the centre
<div n="lb"/>was Ilāvṛta and to the east was Bhadravarṣa. To the
<div n="lb"/>south-east was Hiraṇyaka and to the south was Bhārata.
<div n="lb"/>To the southwest was Hari and to the west was Ketu-
<div n="lb"/>māla. To the north-west was Cambaka and to the north
<div n="lb"/>was Kuruvarṣa. Beautiful Kiṃpuruṣa varṣa stood to the
<div n="lb"/>north-east of Ilāvṛta. There was neither the passage of
<div n="lb"/>time nor the fear of ageing or death in any of these
<div n="lb"/>continents excepting Bhāratavarṣa.
<div n="NI"/>This Bhāratavarṣa itself is a group of nine islands
<div n="lb"/>each separated from the other by oceans and thus made
<div n="lb"/>not easily accessible between each other. Indradvīpa,
<div n="lb"/>Kaśerumān, Tāmraparṇa, Gabhastimān, Nāgadvīpa,
<div n="lb"/>Kaṭāha, Siṃhala and Vāruṇa are the eight others and the
<div n="lb"/>ninth is this island of Bhāratavarṣa. This island is called
<div n="lb"/>Kumāra also. On the eastern boundary of this island live
<div n="lb"/>Kirātas, on the western side, Yavanas, on the southern
<div n="lb"/>boundary Āndhras and on the north Turuṣkas. In the
<div n="lb"/>Centre live the Brāhmins, Kṣatriyas, Vaiśyas and Śūdras.
<div n="lb"/>The eight sacred mountains of this place are Mahendra,
<div n="lb"/>Malaya, Sahya, Śuktimān, Ṛkṣaparvata, Vindhya and
<div n="lb"/>Pāriyātra. There are several other mountains also in
<div n="lb"/>Madhyapradeśa. They are: Kolāhala, Vaibhrāja, Man-
<div n="lb"/>dara, Durddhara, Vātadhūma, Vaidyuta, Maināka,
<div n="lb"/>Sarasa, Tuṅgaprastha, Nāgagiri, Govardhana, Ujjayan-
<div n="lb"/>ta, Puṣpagiri, Arbuda, Raivataka, Ṛṣyamūka, Gomanta,
<div n="lb"/>Citrakūṭa, Kṛtasmara, Śrīparvata and Koṅkaṇa. This
<div n="lb"/>place is inhabited by Āryas and Mlecchas. The rivers
<div n="lb"/>which they use for drinking water are the following:
<div n="NI"/>Sarasvatī, Pañcarūpā, Kālindī, Hiraṇvatī, Śatadru,
<div n="lb"/>Candrikā, Nīla, Vitastā, Irāvatī, Kuhū, Madhurā,
<div n="lb"/>Hārarāvi, Uśīrā, Dhātakī, Rasā, Gomatī, Dhūtapāpā,
<div n="lb"/>Bahudā, Dṛṣadvatī, Nisvarā, Gaṇḍakī, Citrā, Kauśikī,
<div n="lb"/>Vadhūsara, Sarayū and Lohitya. All these rivers origi-
<div n="lb"/>nate from the base of Himavān. Those originating
<div n="lb"/>from Pāriyātra are: Vedaśmṛti, Vedasvanī, Vṛtraghnī,
<div n="lb"/>Sindhu, Parṇāśā, Nandinī, Pāvanī, Mahī, Śarā, Carm-
<div n="lb"/>maṇvatī, Lūpi, Vidiśā, Veṇumatī, Citrā and Oghavatī.
<div n="lb"/>Those flowing down from the Ṛkṣaparvata are: Śoṇa
<div n="lb"/>alias Mahānada, Narmadā, Surasā, Kriyā, Mandākinī,
<div n="lb"/>Daśārṇa, Citrakūṭā, Devikā, Citrotpalā, Tamasā, Kara-
<div n="lb"/>toyā, Piśācikā, Pippalaśreṇī, Vipāśā, Vañjjulāvatī,
<div n="lb"/>Satsantajā, Śuktimatī, Cakriṇī, Tridivā, Vasu, Valgu-
<div n="lb"/>vāhinī. From the base of Vindhya flow down the follow-
[Page124-a+ 63]
<div n="lb"/>ing rivers: Śivā, Payoṣṇī, Nirvindhyā, Tāpī, Veṇā, Niṣa-
<div n="lb"/>dhāvatī, Vaitaraṇī, Sinībāhu, Kumudvatī, Toyā, Revā,
<div n="lb"/>Mahāgaurī, Durggyā and Antaśśivā. Those originating
<div n="lb"/>from the base of Sahya are the following great rivers:
<div n="lb"/>Godāvarī, Bhīmarathī, Kṛṣṇā, Veṇyā, Saridvatī, Viṣa-
<div n="lb"/>mādrī, Suprayogā, Vāhyā, Kāverī, Dugdhodā, Nalinī,
<div n="lb"/>Vārisenā, and Kalasvanā. Those arising from Śuktimān
<div n="lb"/>are the following: Kṛtamālā, Tāmravarṇī, Vañjjulā,
<div n="lb"/>Utpalāvatī, Śunī and Sudāmā. All these rivers are very
<div n="lb"/>sacred and are considered to be absolvers of sins,
<div n="lb"/>mothers of the universe and wives of oceans. There are
<div n="lb"/>bad rivers also besides these.
