GAYA I¦. See Gayātīrtha.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A King of ancient India Gaya was a
<div n="lb"/>Rājarṣi and son of Amūrtarayas. The Rājarṣi had
<div n="lb"/>conducted many yajñas. A very well-known yajña of
<div n="lb"/>his is described in verse 18, Chapter 75 of Vana Parva.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Particular Information.%} (1) Gaya was a member in
<div n="lb"/>Yamarāja's assembly. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 8, Verse
<div n="lb"/>18).
<div n="NI"/>(2) He had earned reputation and wealth by visiting
<div n="lb"/>the sacred temples in India. (Vana Parva, Chapter 94,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 18).
<div n="NI"/>(3) The Rājarṣi had come in his vimāna (aeroplane)
<div n="lb"/>to witness the fighting between Arjuna and Kṛpācārya
<div n="lb"/>in the war, which broke out as a result of the Kauravas
<div n="lb"/>lifting the cows of the Virāṭa King. (Virāṭa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 56, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Śrī Kṛṣṇa met Gaya on his (Kṛṣṇa's) way to
<div n="lb"/>Hastināpura from Dvārakā. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>83, Verse 27).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Once King Māndhātā subjugated Gaya. (Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 62, Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Sarasvatī devī once attended a yajña conducted by
<div n="lb"/>Gaya, assuming for herself the name Viśālā.
<div n="NI"/>(7) He once gifted away land to Brahmins. (Śānti
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 234, Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Gaya did not eat flesh. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>115, Verse 59).
