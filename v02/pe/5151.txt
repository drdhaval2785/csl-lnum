PAVITRĀROPAṆA¦. A Pūjāvidhi (a mode of
<div n="lb"/>worship). If you perform a Pavitrāropaṇa worship you
[Page587-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>will get the benefit of worshipping Viṣṇu for a year.
<div n="lb"/>This worship is to be conducted in the months of
<div n="lb"/>Āṣāḍha (July), Śrāvaṇa (August) Prauṣṭhapada
<div n="lb"/>(September), Āśvina (October) and Kārttika (Novem-
<div n="lb"/>ber) A sacred Pavitra (sacred thread or ring of Kuśa
<div n="lb"/>grass) is to be prepared either in gold, silver, copper,
<div n="lb"/>cotton or silk. A specially purified cotton thread is
<div n="lb"/>also enough The Pavitra is to be made of three threads
<div n="lb"/>woven together. The Pavitra is to be made holy by
<div n="lb"/>reciting 108 times the Gāyatrī mantra or even half
<div n="lb"/>of that number is enough. Reciting 108 times or more
<div n="lb"/>is considered to be Uttama (best); half of it is considered
<div n="lb"/>Madhyama (tolerable) and less than it is considered
<div n="lb"/>adhama (worst). The Pavitra should then be tied
<div n="lb"/>to maṇḍalas and the mantra to be recited at the time
<div n="lb"/>of tying it, is this:
<Poem>Oṃ Nārāyaṇāya vidmahe Vāsudevāya Dhīmahi
<div n="lb"/>tanno Viṣṇuḥ pracodayāt.</Poem>
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 34, Agni Purāṇa).
