BHAGADATTA¦. Bhagadatta was King of Prāgjyotiṣa-
<div n="lb"/>pura. Mahābhārata furnishes the following information
<div n="lb"/>about him.
<div n="NI"/>1) Bhagadatta was born from a limb of the asura
<div n="lb"/>called Bāṣkala. (M.B., Ādi Parva, Chapter 67, Verse
<div n="lb"/>9).
<div n="NI"/>2) He was present at the wedding of Pāñcālī. (Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 185, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>3) Known also as lord of the Yavanas (Yavanādhipa).
<div n="lb"/>Bhagadatta was a friend of Pāṇḍu, and that friendship
<div n="lb"/>was transferred to Yudhiṣṭhira too. (Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 14, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>4) The triumphal tour of Arjuna in connection with
<div n="lb"/>the Rājasūya yajña performed by Yudhiṣṭhira evoked
<div n="lb"/>feelings of jealousy in the heart of Bhagadatta, and he
<div n="lb"/>fought against Arjuna. The courage and prowess Arjuna
<div n="lb"/>exhibited in the fight astonished Bhagadatta, who
<div n="lb"/>congratulated Arjuna on his supreme courage and
<div n="lb"/>admitting defeat yielded to Arjuna. (Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 26, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>5) Bhagadatta, along with the Yavanas was present at
<div n="lb"/>the Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira. He also made presents of
<div n="lb"/>horses of very high pedigree and very costly gems etc.
<div n="lb"/>(Sabhā Parva, Chapter 51, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>6) Karṇa once defeated Bhagadatta in fight. (Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 254, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>7) In the great war he, with his army, fought on the
<div n="lb"/>side of Duryodhana. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 19,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 15).
<div n="NI"/>8) During the first day of the war Bhagadatta fought
<div n="lb"/>a duel with the King of Virāṭa. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 45, Verse 49).
<div n="NI"/>9) He was defeated in his fight with Ghaṭotkaca.
<div n="lb"/>(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 64, Verse 59).
<div n="NI"/>10) In the fight which took place after the above de-
<div n="lb"/>feat Bhagadatta caused Bhīmasena to faint in the field.
<div n="lb"/>(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 64, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>11) And after that he defeated Ghaṭotkaca in fight.
<div n="lb"/>(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 83, Verse 40).
<div n="NI"/>12) Next ensued the combat with King Daśārṇa in
<div n="lb"/>which the King was defeated. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>95, Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>13) It was not long after this that Bhagadatta cut off
<div n="lb"/>the hands of the Kṣatradeva. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>95, Verse 73).
<div n="NI"/>14) Viśoka, the charioteer of Bhīma-sena, hit by the
<div n="lb"/>arrows of Bhagadatta fell down and fainted. (Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 76).
<div n="NI"/>15) Bhagadatta and Sātyaki fought against each other.
<div n="lb"/>(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 111, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>16) Chapters 113 and 114 of the Bhīṣma Parva des-
<div n="lb"/>cribe the combats fought by Bhagadatta with Bhīma-
<div n="lb"/>sena and Arjuna.
<div n="NI"/>17) Bhagadatta and Drupada fought with each other.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 14, Verse 40).
<div n="NI"/>18) Bhagadatta killed king Daśārṇa. (Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 26, Verse 38).

<div n="NI"/>19) Bhagadatta killed Ruciparvan. (Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 26, Verse 52).
<div n="NI"/>20) He shot the Vaiṣṇavāstra (arrow given by Viṣṇu)
<div n="lb"/>on Arjuna. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 29, Verse 17).
<div n="NI"/>21) Arjuna killed Bhagadatta. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>29, Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>22) After his death, his son, Vajradatta became King
<div n="lb"/>of Prāgjyotiṣa. Arjuna killed Vajradatta also. (Aśva-
<div n="lb"/>medha Parva, Chapter 76).
<div n="NI"/>23) King Śailālaya, who was Bhagadatta's grandfather,
<div n="lb"/>attained Indraloka on account of the greatness of his
<div n="lb"/>tapas. (Āśramavāsika Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 10).
