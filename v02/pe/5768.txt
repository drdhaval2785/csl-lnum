ROHIṆĪ III¦. The mother of Balabhadrarāma.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} Vasudeva had two wives Devakī
<div n="lb"/>and Rohiṇī. Śrī Kṛṣṇa was born from Devakī and
<div n="lb"/>Balabhadrarāma from Rohiṇī.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Previous Birth of Rohiṇī.%} Vasudeva was the rebirth
<div n="lb"/>of Prajāpati Kaśyapa. When Kaśyapa took birth as
<div n="lb"/>Vasudeva, his two wives Aditi and Surasā took birth as
<div n="lb"/>Devakī and Rohiṇī respectively. (They took birth thus,
<div n="lb"/>because of the curse of Varuṇa. For details of the
<div n="lb"/>curse see under Kaśyapa, para 6).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%The birth of Balabhadra.%} The seventh child of
<div n="lb"/>Devakī was placed in the womb of Rohiṇī and Bala-
<div n="lb"/>bhadrarāma was born. (For this story see under
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa, para 6, Kaṃsa, para 6 and Ṣaḍarbhaka).
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Death.%} As soon as a messenger named Dāruka
<div n="lb"/>brought the news that the entire race of the Yādavas
<div n="lb"/>had perished in Dvārakā,
<div n="NI"/>“because of grief, Vasudeva, Devakī and Rohiṇī forsook
<div n="lb"/>their bodies.” (Bhāgavata, Skandha 11).
