SENĀBINDU I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} A Kṣatriya King who lived in
<div n="lb"/>the age of the Purāṇas. This King was born from a
<div n="lb"/>portion of an asura called Tuhuṇḍa. (Mahābhārata
<div n="lb"/>Āḍi Parva, Chapter 67).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Senābindu was present at the Svayaṃvara marriage
<div n="lb"/>of Draupadī. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 185, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Arjuna, during his regional conquest of the north,
<div n="lb"/>with the King of Ulūka had attacked this King who
<div n="lb"/>lost his kingdom. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 27,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) The Pāṇḍavas had invited this King to take part
<div n="lb"/>in the Bhārata battle. (M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>4, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It is said that Senābindu was known by another
<div n="lb"/>name Krodhahantā. He was as valiant as Śrī Kṛṣṇa
<div n="lb"/>and Bhīmasena. (M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 171,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 20).
<div n="NI"/>(v) He was killed in the battle of Bhārata. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Karṇa Parva, Chapter 6 Verse 32).
