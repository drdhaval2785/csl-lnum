TRASAREṆU¦. An ancient measure of weight of metals.
<Poem>Jālāntarāgate bhānau
<div n="lb"/>Yat sūkṣmaṃ dṛśyate rajaḥ /
<div n="lb"/>Prathamaṃ tat pramāṇānāṃ
<div n="lb"/>Trasareṇuṃ pracakṣate. //</Poem>
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 132, Chapter 8, Manusmṛti).
<div n="lb"/>Eight trasareṇus make one Īru. Three īrus make one
<div n="lb"/>Maṇkaṭuku. Three maṇkaṭukus make one Veṇkaṭuku.
<div n="lb"/>Six veṇkaṭukus make one Madhyastrīyava. Three
<div n="lb"/>madhyastrīyavas make one kunnikkuru. Five kunnikkurus
<div n="lb"/>make one Māṣa. Sixteen māṣas make one Suvarṇa.
<div n="lb"/>This is the weight of two and a half varāhas. Four
<div n="lb"/>suvarṇas make one Pala. Ten palas make one Dharaṇa.
