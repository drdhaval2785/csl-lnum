AGNIPURĀṆA¦(M).
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} This is one of the eighteen
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇas ascribed to Vyāsa. It is believed that this
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa was originally given orally (as advice) by Agni-
[Page016-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>deva to many sages, devas and Sage Vasiṣṭha. It is a
<div n="lb"/>vast comprehensive work dealing with every subject of
<div n="lb"/>importance. To give a copy of this book to a good
<div n="lb"/>Brahmin on the Full Moon day in the month of
<div n="lb"/>Mārga Sīrṣa is supposed to be a highly virtuous and
<div n="lb"/>meritorious deed.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Contents.%} This large Purāṇa consists of about 420
<div n="lb"/>chapters. It deals in detail with the following subjects:
<div n="lb"/>The Daśāvatāras of Mahāviṣṇu; Rāmāyaṇa; Mahā-
<div n="lb"/>bhārata; rules and injunctions relating to the worship of
<div n="lb"/>various gods (devapūjāvidhis); installation of idols in
<div n="lb"/>temples (devatāpratiṣṭhā); Svapnamantras; astrology;
<div n="lb"/>architecture and sculpture; Āyurveda; Viṣavaidya
<div n="lb"/>(treatment of poisons from Snakebite etc.); the princi-
<div n="lb"/>ples of the drama (Nāṭaka) and other allied arts;
<div n="lb"/>figures of speech and all alaṃkāras in general; and
<div n="lb"/>physiology of the human body. All these subjects are
<div n="lb"/>treated in a detailed and scientific manner.
