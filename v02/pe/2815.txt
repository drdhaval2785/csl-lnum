JAHNU¦. A hermit King born in the family of Pūru.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu in the following
<div n="lb"/>order:—Brahmā—Atri—Candra—Budha—Purūravas—
<div n="lb"/>Āyus—Nahuṣa—Yayāti—Pūru—Janamejaya—Prācinvān
<div n="lb"/>-Pravīra—Namasyu—Vītabhaya—Śuṇḍu—Bahuvidha
<div n="lb"/>-Saṃyāti—Rahovādi—Raudrāśva—Matināra—
<div n="lb"/>Santurodha—Duṣyanta—Bharata—Suhotra—Bṛhatputra
<div n="lb"/>-Ajamīḍha—Jahnu.
<div n="NI"/>Ajamīḍha had three wives, Dhūminī, Nīlī and Keśinī.
<div n="lb"/>Ṛkṣa was born from Dhūminī, Duṣyanta and Para-
<div n="lb"/>meṣṭhi from Nīlī and Jahnu from Keśinī. The descen-
<div n="lb"/>dants of Jahnu are called the Kuśikas. Jahnu handed
<div n="lb"/>over his kingdom to his son Balākāśva and went to
<div n="lb"/>perform penance. Kuśika was the son of Balākāśva.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Drank up the river Ganges.%} The river Gaṅgā, which
<div n="lb"/>flowed through the earth in accordance with the
<div n="lb"/>request of Bhagīratha, submerged the hermitage of
<div n="lb"/>Jahnu. Jahnu became angry at this haughtiness of
<div n="lb"/>Gaṅgā and drank up the river, but at the entreaty of
<div n="lb"/>Bhagīratha pushed Gaṅgādevī out through his ear.
<div n="lb"/>(See under Gaṅgā). From that day onwards Gaṅgā
<div n="lb"/>got the name Jāhnavī.
