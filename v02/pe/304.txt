AṄGARĀJAVAṂŚA¦. (THE DYNASTY OF AṄGA
<div n="lb"/>KINGS). Descended from Viṣṇu in the following order:
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā-Atri-Candra-Budha-Purūravas-Āyus-Nahuṣa-
<div n="lb"/>Yayāti. Yadu, Turvasu, Druhyu and Anudruhyu were
<div n="lb"/>the four sons of Yayāti. The Aṅga dynasty starts from
<div n="lb"/>Turvasu, one of the four. The following are the descen-
<div n="lb"/>dants of Turvasu. Varga was the son of Turvasu,
<div n="lb"/>Gobhānu, the son of Varga, Traiśāni the son of Gobhānu,
<div n="lb"/>Karandhama, the son of Traiśāni, Marutta, the son of
<div n="lb"/>Karandhama, Duṣyanta, the son of Marutta, Varūtha,
[Page039-a+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>the son of Duṣyanta, Gāṇḍira the son of Varūtha, and
<div n="lb"/>Gāndāra the son of Gāṇḍira. The five powerful peoples,
<div n="lb"/>the Gāndhāras, the Colas, the Keralas, the Pāṇḍyas
<div n="lb"/>and the Kolas have descended from Gāndhāra.
<div n="NI"/>Two sons, Druhyu and Babhrusetu were born to Gāndhāra.
<div n="lb"/>Babhrusetu begot Purovasu; Purovasu begot Gāndhārī.
<div n="lb"/>From Gāndhārī Gharma was born, from Gharma Ghṛta
<div n="lb"/>was born, from Ghṛta Viduṣa was born, and from Viduṣa
<div n="lb"/>Pracetas was born. Pracetas got a hundred children of
<div n="lb"/>whom the prominent were Anidra, Sabhānara, Cākṣuṣa and
<div n="lb"/>Parameṣu. To Sabhānara was born Kālānala and Sṛñ-
<div n="lb"/>jaya to Kālānala, Purañjaya to Sṛñjaya and Janamejaya
<div n="lb"/>to Purañjaya. Mahāsāla was the son of Janamejaya;
<div n="lb"/>Mahāmanas the son of Mahāsāla, and Uśinara the son
<div n="lb"/>of Mahāmanas. To Uśīnara were born Nṛga, Nara,
<div n="lb"/>Kṛmi, Suvrata and Śibi by his wives Nṛgā, Narā, Kṛmi,
<div n="lb"/>Daśā and Dṛṣadvatī respectively. To Śibi were born
<div n="lb"/>four sons called Pṛthudarbha, Vīraka, Kaikaya and
<div n="lb"/>Bhadraka. Four separate kingdoms arose in the names
<div n="lb"/>of them. Uśīnara had another son called Titikṣu. From
<div n="lb"/>Titikṣu was born Ruṣadratha; from Ruṣadratha was
<div n="lb"/>born Paila, from Paila was born Sutapas and from
<div n="lb"/>Sutapas was born the great hermit Bāli. From Bāli,
<div n="lb"/>Aṅga, Vaṅga, Kaliṅga, Puṇḍra, Bāleya and Bālayogi
<div n="lb"/>were born. To Aṅga was born Dadhivāhana. The King
<div n="lb"/>Draviratha was the son of Dadhivāhana, Dharmaratha
<div n="lb"/>the son of Draviratha, Citraratha the son of Dharma-
<div n="lb"/>rātha, and Satyaratha was the son of Citraratha. To
<div n="lb"/>Satyaratha was born Lomapāda; to Lomapāda was
<div n="lb"/>born Caturaṅga; to Caturaṅga was born Pṛthulākṣa, to
<div n="lb"/>Pṛthulākṣa was born Campa; to Campa was born Har-
<div n="lb"/>yaṅga, to Haryaṅga was born Bhadraratha, to Bhadra-
<div n="lb"/>ratha was born Bṛhatkarmā, to Bṛhatkarmā was born
<div n="lb"/>Bṛhadbhānu, to Bṛhadbhānu was born Bṛhadātmā,
<div n="lb"/>to Bṛhadātmā was born Jayadratha, to Jayadratha was
<div n="lb"/>born Bṛhadratha and to Bṛhadratha was born Viśvajit.
<div n="lb"/>After that Karṇa became the King of Aṅga. The son of
<div n="lb"/>Karṇa was Vṛṣasena and the son of Vṛsasena was
<div n="lb"/>Pṛthusena. These are the kings of the Aṅga dynasty.
<div n="lb"/>(Agni Purāṇa, Chapter 277.)
