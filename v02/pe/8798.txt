YUYUTSU I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} Son of Dhṛtarāṣṭra by a Vaiśya woman.
<div n="lb"/>He is not included in the 100 sons of Dhṛtarāṣṭra.
<div n="lb"/>Yuyutsu is referred to by another name, “Karaṇa”
<div n="lb"/>in Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 68, Verse 113.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) It was Yuyutsu who disclosed to the Pāṇḍavas that
<div n="lb"/>once Duryodhana gave poisoned food to Bhīmasena.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 128, Verse 37).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Yuyutsu was present at the Svayaṃvara of Drau-
<div n="lb"/>padī. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 185, Verse 2).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) In Bhārata Yuddha, Yuyutsu joined the Pāṇḍava
<div n="lb"/>party. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 43, Verse 100).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) He was an eminent warrior and an honest and
[Page900-b+ 43]
<div n="lb"/>mighty hero. Many Kings attacked him in the city
<div n="lb"/>Vāraṇāvata. He was not able to fulfil his desire to kill
<div n="lb"/>all of them. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 10, Verse
<div n="lb"/>58).
<div n="NI"/>(v) In Bhārata Yuddha he fought with Subāhu and
<div n="lb"/>cut off his hands. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 25, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) The bullocks tied to Yuyutsu's chariot were killed
<div n="lb"/>by Bhagadatta's elephant. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>26, Verse 56).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Yuyutsu severely rebuked the Kauravas who re-
<div n="lb"/>joiced over the death of Bhagadatta. (M.B. Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 72, Verse 60).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Yuyutsu was defeated in his fight with Ulūka.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 25. Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) As advised by Śrī Kṛṣṇa and Dharmaputra,
<div n="lb"/>Yuyutsu went to Hastināpura with the ladies of the
<div n="lb"/>royal family. (M.B. Śalya Parva, Chapter 29, Verse
<div n="lb"/>86).
<div n="NI"/>(x) On his return after the battle, Yuyutsu described
<div n="lb"/>the details of the battle to Vidura. (M.B. Śalya Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 29. Verse 21).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) At the suggestion of Dharmaputra, Yuyutsu began
<div n="lb"/>to attend on Dhṛtarāṣṭra to serve him. (M.B. Śānti
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 41, Verse 17).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) Yuyutsu was guarding Hastināpura when the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas went to the Himālayas to get money from
<div n="lb"/>Marutta. (Aśvamedha Parva, Chapter 63, Verse 24).
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) Under the leadership of Yuyutsu, the Pāṇḍavas
<div n="lb"/>offered Jalāñjali (offering libations of water) to
<div n="lb"/>Dhṛtarāṣṭra. (Āśramavāsika Parva, Chapter 39, Verse
<div n="lb"/>12).
<div n="NI"/>(xiv) When the Pāṇḍavas departed on their Mahā-
<div n="lb"/>prasthāna after handing over the reign of the country
<div n="lb"/>to Parīkṣit, it was Yuyutsu who was entrusted with the
<div n="lb"/>task of supervising Parīkṣit and the country. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Mahāprasthāna Parva Chapter 1, Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(xv) In Mahābhārata the following names are given
<div n="lb"/>for Yuyutsu:—Dhārtarāṣṭra, Dhṛtarāṣṭraja, Dhṛta-
<div n="lb"/>rāṣṭraputra, Karaṇa, Kauravya, Kaurava and Vaiśyā-
<div n="lb"/>putra.
