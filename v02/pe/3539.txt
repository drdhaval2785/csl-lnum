KIMPURUṢA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A King called Agnīdhra was born in the
<div n="lb"/>dynasty of Priyavrata, son of Manu. He became lord
<div n="lb"/>of the Jambū island, and married an apsarā woman
<div n="lb"/>named Pūrvacitti. To them were born nine sons called
<div n="lb"/>Nābhi, Kimpuruṣa, Hari, Ilāvṛta, Ramyaka, Hiraṇ-
<div n="lb"/>maya, Kalva, Bhadrāśva and Ketumāla. Agnīdhra
<div n="lb"/>partitioned the kingdom among the nine sons. The
<div n="lb"/>land Kimpuruṣa ruled over was known as Kimpuruṣa
<div n="lb"/>or Kimpuruṣavarṣa. It lay to the south of Hemakūṭa
<div n="lb"/>mountain. It was here that Hanūmān spent his last
<div n="lb"/>days worshipping Śrī Rāma.
<div n="NI"/>“The son of the wind God (Hanūmān) lives in the
<div n="lb"/>country called Kimpuruṣavarṣa in the worship of Śrī
<div n="lb"/>Rāma.” (Bhāgavata, 5th Skandha).
<div n="NI"/>According to the Sabhā Parva (Chapter 23, Verses 1, 2)
<div n="lb"/>Kimpuruṣavarṣa guarded by the sons of Druma is
<div n="lb"/>situated to the north of the Himālayas facing the
<div n="lb"/>Dhavala mountain, and this country was once conquer-
<div n="lb"/>ed by Arjuna.

<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Kimpuruṣas were the sons of Pulaha Prajāpati.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 66, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) They witnessed Agastya drinking up the ocean
<div n="lb"/>dry. (Vana Parva, Chapter 104, Verse 21).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) They guard the lotus ponds kept by Kubera to
<div n="lb"/>sport with his beloved ones. (Vana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>15, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) In his conflict with Rāvaṇa, Kubera left behind
<div n="lb"/>him Laṅkā and his Puṣpaka Vimāna, escaped towards
<div n="lb"/>the north and settled down on mount Gandhamādana
<div n="lb"/>with the help of the Kimpuruṣas. (Vana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>275, Verse 33).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Yakṣa women were their mothers. (Śānti Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 207, Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) They were present at the aśvamedha of Yudhi-
<div n="lb"/>ṣṭhira. (Aśvamedha Parva, Chapter 88, Verse 37).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Śuka Brahmarṣi reached Bhārata, crossing
<div n="lb"/>Kimpuruṣavarṣa. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 325).
