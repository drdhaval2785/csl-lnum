PURANDARA III¦. The name of Indra in Vaivasvata
<div n="lb"/>Manvantara. (See under Manvantara). In Matsya
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa Purandara has been considered to be one among
<div n="lb"/>the eighteen Vāstuśāstrakāras (adepts in house building).
<div n="lb"/>The other seventeen are: Bhṛgu, Atri, Vasiṣṭha, Viśva-
<div n="lb"/>karmā, Maya, Nārada, Nagnajit, Viśālākṣa, Brahmā,
<div n="lb"/>Kumāra, Nandīśa, Śaunaka, Garga, Vāśudeva, Śukra,
<div n="lb"/>Bṛhaspati and Aniruddha. (Matsya Purāṇa, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>252, verses 2 and 3).
<div n="NI"/>According to Mahābhārata Lord Śiva wrote a book
<div n="lb"/>“Vaiśālākṣa” containing ten thousand chapters dealing
<div n="lb"/>with Dharmārthakāmas. Purandara condensed it into
<div n="lb"/>a book of five thousand chapters called ‘Bāhudantaka.’

<div n="lb"/>mother who was called Bāhudantī. (Chapters 59, 89
<div n="lb"/>and 90, Śānti Parva).
