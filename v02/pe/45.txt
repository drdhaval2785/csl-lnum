<div n="lb"/>several āśramas connected with sage Agastya. The
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas during their exile visited an āśrama of this
<div n="lb"/>name. This is situated near Pañcavaṭī twentyfour miles
<div n="lb"/>to the south-east of Nāsik. It is known as Agastya-giri
<div n="lb"/>now. (Śloka 20, Chapter 80; and Śloka 1, Chapter 96
<div n="lb"/>of Vana Parva, M.B.).
<div n="NI"/>The Mahābhārata mentions another Agastya-Āśrama
<div n="lb"/>near Prayāga. Dharmaputra along with Saint Lomaśa
<div n="lb"/>stayed here for some time. Vālmīki describes an āśrama
<div n="lb"/>of this name which Rāma and Lakṣmaṇa visited during
<div n="lb"/>their exile in the forests. (Chapter 11, Āraṇya Kāṇḍa
<div n="lb"/>of the Rāmāyaṇa).
