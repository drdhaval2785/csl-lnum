MITRA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} One of the twelve Sūryas. The
<div n="lb"/>twelve Sūryas born to Aditi of Kaśyapaprajāpati are
<div n="lb"/>Viṣṇu, Śakra, Aryamā, Dhātā, Tvaṣṭā, Pūṣā, Vivasvān,
<div n="lb"/>Savitā, Mitra, Varuṇa, Bhaga and Aṃśa. (Chapter 15,
<div n="lb"/>Aṃśa 1, Viṣṇu Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>Mitrāvaruṇas are two devatās of great intimacy. They
<div n="lb"/>are always found together. If you pray to Mitrāvaruṇas
<div n="lb"/>you will get plenty of rains. (Śūkta 2, Anuvāka 1,
<div n="lb"/>Maṇḍala 1, Ṛgveda).
<div n="NI"/>See para 2 under Agastya to understand the story of
<div n="lb"/>how Mitrāvaruṇas became the father of Agastya.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Mitra came and stood in the sky at the time of the
<div n="lb"/>birth of Arjuna. (Śloka 66, Chapter 122, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Mitra was one among those sent by Indra to fight
<div n="lb"/>against Kṛṣṇa and Arjuna at the time of the burning
<div n="lb"/>of the forest Khāṇḍava. (Śloka 36, Chapter 226 Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Mitra was a member of the Indra sabhā. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>21, Chapter 7, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) He presented two Pārṣadas named Suvrata and
<div n="lb"/>Satyasandha to Subrahmaṇya. (Śloka 41, Chapter 45,
<div n="lb"/>Śalya Parva).
