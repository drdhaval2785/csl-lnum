SAṂVARTA¦. Son of Aṅgiras.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} Aṅgiras had eight sons called Bṛhaspati,
<div n="lb"/>Utathya, Saṃvarta, Payasya, Śānti, Ghora, Virūpa and
<div n="lb"/>Sudhanvā. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 85, Verse 30).
<div n="lb"/>Saṃvarta was the third among the sons and he lived on
<div n="lb"/>inimical terms with his eldest brother Bṛhaspati. When
<div n="lb"/>once Bṛhaspati forsook king Marutta it was Saṃvarta,
<div n="lb"/>who managed for the king his yajña. (Śānti Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 29, Verse 20).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Saṃvarta is a member of Indra's court. (Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 7, Verse 19).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He lives in Brahmā's court and worships him.
<div n="lb"/>(Sabhā Parva, Chapter 11, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) He once got conducted at Plakṣāvataraṇa tīrtha
<div n="lb"/>for King Marutta a yajña. (Vana Parva, Chapter 129,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Saṃvarta and Bṛhaspati disliked each other.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 55, Verse 38; Śānti Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 29, Verse 29).
<div n="NI"/>(v) He was one of those who visited Bhīṣma on his bed
<div n="lb"/>of arrows. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 47, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) It was he, who recited the praises of Śiva to king
<div n="lb"/>Marutta so that the latter got gold. (M.B. Southern text,
<div n="lb"/>Aśvamedha Parva, Chapter 8).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Once he paralysed Indra's Vajrāyudha. (Aśva-
<div n="lb"/>medha Parva, Chapter 10, Verse 17).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) It was he, who invited and got down Indra to the
<div n="lb"/>yajña conducted by Marutta. (Aśvamedha Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 10, Verse 25).
