MEPPATTŪR¦ NĀRĀYAṆA BHAṬṬATIRI. A Sans-
<div n="lb"/>krit poet who lived in Kerala.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information%} It is believed that this poet
<div n="lb"/>lived during the period from 1560 to 1648 A.D. He was
<div n="lb"/>born in the village of Kurumāttūr in Malabar district
<div n="lb"/>The illam (house of nambūtiris) which became famous
<div n="lb"/>by his birth was a mile to the east of the Bhagavatī
<div n="lb"/>temple, Candanakkāvu, which itself is situated just
<div n="lb"/>two furlongs to the north of the well known Tirunāvāya
<div n="lb"/>temple. Meppattūr illam became impoverished and was
<div n="lb"/>merged with another illam of name Maravañceri
<div n="lb"/>Tekkeṭattu.
<div n="NI"/>Nārāyaṇa Bhaṭṭatiri had a brother named Mātṛdatta.
<div n="lb"/>He was younger to Nārāyaṇa and his work was to take
[Page502-a+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>down the verses which Nārāyaṇa composed. Meppattūr
<div n="lb"/>(Nārāyaṇa is more well-known under the name of his
<div n="lb"/>illam) studied under different preceptors. His father
<div n="lb"/>taught him Mīmāṃsā and such other subjects. He
<div n="lb"/>studied Veda under Mādhavācārya and Tarka under
<div n="lb"/>Dāmodarācārya. Acyuta Piṣāroṭi taught him Vyākaraṇa.
<div n="lb"/>The poet became a rheumatic patient in his old age
<div n="lb"/>and he stayed in the Guruvāyūr temple worshipping
<div n="lb"/>the deity there. His famous work Nārāyaṇīyam was
<div n="lb"/>written sitting in the temple of Guruvāyūr and the
<div n="lb"/>maṇḍapa (raised dais) where he sat and wrote is even
<div n="lb"/>now preserved for pilgrims to see. He took hundred
<div n="lb"/>days to write the Nārāyaṇīyam.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%His works.%} It is not yet known how many books
<div n="lb"/>Meppattūr had written but the following have been
<div n="lb"/>recognised as definitely written by him: (1) Nārā-
<div n="lb"/>yaṇīyam (2) Śrīpādasaptati (3) Guruvāyupureśastotram
<div n="lb"/>(4) Gośrīnagaravarṇanam (5) Māṭamahīśapraśasti (6)
<div n="lb"/>Śailābdhīśvarapraśasti (7) Sūktaślokas (8) Āśvalāyana-
<div n="lb"/>kriyākrama (9) Prakriyāsarvasvam (10) Dhātukāvyam
<div n="lb"/>(11) Apāṇinīyaprāmāṇyasādhanam (12) Mānapariccheda
<div n="lb"/>of Mānameyodaya (13) Tantravārttikanibandhana (14)
<div n="lb"/>Rājasūyam (15) Dūtavākyam (16) Pāñcālīsvayaṃ-
<div n="lb"/>varam (17) Nālāyanīcaritam (18) Sundopasundopā-
<div n="lb"/>khyānam (19) Subhadrāharaṇam (20) Kaunteyāṣṭakam

<div n="lb"/>tāram (24) Nṛgamokṣam (25) Niranunāsika or Śūrpa-
