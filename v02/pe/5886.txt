SĀDHYA¦ (S).
<div n="NI"/>1. {%General.%} A Gaṇadevatā. These Devatās often used to
<div n="lb"/>play an important role in Purāṇic movements and inci-
<div n="lb"/>dents. The Sādhyas were born from the seed Virāṭ
<div n="lb"/>Puruṣa. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 1, Verse 35). But accord-
<div n="lb"/>ing to Viṣṇu Purāṇa (Part 1, Chapter 35) the Sādhyas
<div n="lb"/>were the grand-children of Dakṣa-prajāpati. Of the sixty
<div n="lb"/>daughters of the Prajāpati by his wife Asiknī, ten were
<div n="lb"/>married by Dharmadeva; The Viśvadevas were the
<div n="lb"/>sons of Dharmadeva by his wife Viśvā and the Sādhyas
<div n="lb"/>were his sons by Sādhyā.

<div n="NI"/>2. {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) The Sādhyas fought with Garuḍa, who went to Deva-
<div n="lb"/>loka for Amṛta and got defeated. (Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>32, Verse 16).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) The Sādhyas feared Viśvāmitra. (Anuśāsana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 71, Verse 39).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Sādhyagaṇas participated in the birthday celebra-
<div n="lb"/>tions of Arjuna. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 122, Verse 70).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Sādhyas took their place in planes above the palace
<div n="lb"/>of Drupada to witness Draupadī's wedding. (Ādi Parva
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 186, Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(v) They were present at the Devayajña conducted at
<div n="lb"/>Naimiṣa forest. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 195, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) They were present with various Kinds of arrows at
<div n="lb"/>the battle between Śrī Kṛṣṇa and Arjuna on the occa-
<div n="lb"/>sion of the burning of the Khāṇḍava forest. (Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 226 Verse 38).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) They live in Indra's court. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>7, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) They go to the court of Brahmā also and worship
<div n="lb"/>him. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 11, Verse 44).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) In the battle between Subrahmaṇya and Tārakāsura
<div n="lb"/>they fought on the side of the former. (Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 231, Verse 71).
<div n="NI"/>(x) Once they made a prayer to Dattātreya muni.
<div n="lb"/>(Udyoga Parva, Chapter 36, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) On the occasion of the battle between Karṇa and
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna at Kurukṣetra the Sādhyas wished success for
<div n="lb"/>the flatter. (Śalya Parva, Chapter 44, Verse 29).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) They served as store-keepers at the yajña perform-
<div n="lb"/>ed by king Marutta. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 29, Verse
<div n="lb"/>22).
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) They remain on Mount Muñjavān worshipping
<div n="lb"/>Śiva. (Āśvamedhika Parva, Chapter 8, Verse 1).
