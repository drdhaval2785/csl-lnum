MAGHĀ II¦. One of the twentyseven constellations. The
<div n="lb"/>importance of this constellation is mentioned in the
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata in several places.
<div n="NI"/>(i) When the planet Kuja (Mars), during its retrograde
<div n="lb"/>motion, comes near the constellation of Maghā many
<div n="lb"/>unhappy incidents occur in the world. (Śloka 14,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 3, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) If Candra stays near Maghā it is an ill omen. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>2, Chapter 17, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) If one gives away land in charity on the day of
<div n="lb"/>Maghā one will be rich in children and cattle. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>12, Chapter 64, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) If one gives pudding to the poor on the day of
<div n="lb"/>Maghā which comes in the black half of the month
<div n="lb"/>of Tulā (October) the Manes will be pleased. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>7, Chapter 88, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(v) If one worships the Manes on the day of Maghā
<div n="lb"/>sitting in the shade of an elephant, the manes will be
<div n="lb"/>satisfied. (Śloka 8, Chapter 88, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) If one conducts the obsequial rites and acts of
<div n="lb"/>charity on the day of Maghā one would become the
<div n="lb"/>most excellent member of the family. (Śloka 5. Chapter
<div n="lb"/>89, Anuśāsana Parva).
