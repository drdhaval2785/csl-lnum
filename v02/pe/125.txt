AJAMĪḌHA I¦. A famous king of the Pūru Vaṃśa.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu in this order:—
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā-Atri-Candra-Budha-Purūravas-Āyus-
<div n="lb"/>Nahuṣa-Yayāti-Pūru-Janamejaya-Prācinvā-Mana-
<div n="lb"/>syu-Vītabhaya-Śunḍu-Bahuvidha-Saṃyāti-Raho-
<div n="lb"/>vādi-Bhadrāśva-Matināra-Santurodha-Duṣyanta-
<div n="lb"/>Bharata-Bṛhatkṣetra-Hasti-Ajamīḍha.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Birth.%} Several dynasties like Yadu Vaṃśa, Pūru
<div n="lb"/>Vaṃśa etc. take their origin from Yayāti. Duṣyanta be-
<div n="lb"/>longs to that dynasty. King Bharata was born as Duṣ-
<div n="lb"/>yanta's son by Śakuntalā. Suhotra-Suhota-Gaya-
<div n="lb"/>Gardda-Suketu and Bṛhatkṣetra were Bharata's sons.
<div n="lb"/>Bṛhatkṣetra had four children, who were: Nara, Mahā-
<div n="lb"/>vīra, Garga and Hasti. Of them Hasti had three sons:
<div n="lb"/>Purumīḍha, Ajamīḍha and Dvimīḍha.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Other details.%} Ajamīḍha had three queens—Dhū-
<div n="lb"/>minī, Nīlī and Keśinī. Of them, Dhūminī had a son,
<div n="lb"/>Ṛkṣa and Nīlī's son was Duṣyanta (This was not Śakun-
<div n="lb"/>talā's husband, Duṣyanta) and Keśinī's sons were Jahnu,
<div n="lb"/>Praja and Rūpiṇa. Parameṣṭī was another name of
<div n="lb"/>Keśinī. (M.B., Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verses 30-32;
<div n="lb"/>Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 4, Verse 2).
