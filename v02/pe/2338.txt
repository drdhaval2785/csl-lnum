GAṄGĀDVĀRA¦(M). The place or locality in the Indo-
<div n="lb"/>Gangetic plane where the river Ganges falls from the
<div n="lb"/>Himālayas. This place is known as Haridvāra also.
<div n="lb"/>arHidvāra has an epic importance.
[Page279-a+ 63]
<div n="NI"/>(1) It was here that King Pratīpa did tapas. (Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 97, Verse 1).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Sage Bharadvāja had stayed on the banks of the
<div n="lb"/>Gaṅgā, at Haridvāra. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 129, Verse
<div n="lb"/>33).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Arjuna visited Haridvāra during his tour or Pilgri-
<div n="lb"/>mage. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 213).
<div n="NI"/>(4) This is the entrance to heaven. A bath here in the
<div n="lb"/>Koṭitīrtha is as beneficial as the Puṇḍarīka Yajña.
<div n="lb"/>(Vana Parva, Chapter 34, Verse 27).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Sage Agastya and his wife Lomapādā once did
<div n="lb"/>tapas here. (Vana Parva, Chapter 97, Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(6) It was here that Śiva appeared to Jayadratha, who
<div n="lb"/>did tapas. (Vana Parva 72, Verse 24).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Dakṣaprajāpati had once performed tapas at
<div n="lb"/>Kanakhala in Haridvāra. (Śalya Parva, Chapter 38,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 27).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Those who bathe at Kuśāvarta, Vilvaka, Nīla-
<div n="lb"/>parvata and Kanakhala in Haridvāra will attain
<div n="lb"/>heaven. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 25, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Bhīṣma did the funeral rites of his father at the
<div n="lb"/>mouth of the Gaṅgā. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 34,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Gāndhārī, Kuntī and others died in
<div n="lb"/>wild fire in the forest at Gaṅgādvāra, and Yudhiṣṭhira
<div n="lb"/>conducted their funeral rites there itself. (Āśramavāsika
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 39, Verse 14).
