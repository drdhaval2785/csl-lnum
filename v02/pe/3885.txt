KURU IV¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy and birth.%} The very famous King Kuru
<div n="lb"/>was born in the Puru dynasty. His genealogy is given
<div n="lb"/>hereunder. Descended from Viṣṇu thus: Brahmā-
<div n="lb"/>Atri-Candra-Budha-Purūravas-Āyus-Nahuṣa-
<div n="lb"/>Yayāti-Puru-Janamejaya-Prācinvān-Pravīra-
<div n="lb"/>Namasyu-Vītabhaya-Śuṇḍu-Bahuvidha-Saṃyāti-
<div n="lb"/>Rahovādī-Raudrāśva-Matināra-Santurodha-
<div n="lb"/>Duṣyanta-Bharata-Suhotra-Suhotā-Gala-Garda-
<div n="lb"/>Suketu-Bṛhatkṣatra-Hasti-Ajamīḍha-Ṛkṣa-
<div n="lb"/>Saṃvaraṇa-Kuru.
<div n="NI"/>To Kuru were born four sons called Parīkṣit, Sudha-
<div n="lb"/>nus, Jahnu and Niṣadhāśva. The genealogy of the
<div n="lb"/>Kuru Kings is as follows: Sudhanus-Cyavana-Kṛti-
<div n="lb"/>Uparicaravasu-Bṛhadratha-Kuśāgraja-Ṛṣabha-
<div n="lb"/>Puṣpavān-Juhu.
<div n="NI"/>Jarāsandha was another son of Bṛhadratha. Jarāsandha
<div n="lb"/>had four sons called Soma, Sahadeva, Turya and
<div n="lb"/>Śrutaśru. From Jahnu, the following sons were
<div n="lb"/>born:—Suratha-Viḍūratha-Sārvabhauma-Jayat-
<div n="lb"/>sena,-Ravīya-Bhāvuka-Cakroddhata-Devātithi-
<div n="lb"/>Ṛkṣa-Bhīma and Pratīca. Pratīca had three sons
<div n="lb"/>called Devāpi, Śantanu and Bālhīka. Śantanu is
<div n="lb"/>known as Mahābhiṣak also. Dhṛtarāṣṭra and Pāṇḍu
<div n="lb"/>were the successors of Śantanu, and they came to be
<div n="lb"/>known as Kauravas also as they belonged to the
<div n="lb"/>dynasty of Kuru. But, since the sons of Pāṇḍu were
<div n="lb"/>born of Devas they may not be called Kauravas.
<div n="lb"/>2) {%Other Information.%} (1) Kurukṣetra became holy
[Page445-a+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>and sanctified on account of Kuru's tapas. (Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 94, Verse 80).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) While Kuru was once ploughing a land in
<div n="lb"/>Kurukṣetra Indra appeared there, and they had a
<div n="lb"/>talk. (Śalya Parva, Chapter 53 Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) While Kuru was once performing a yajña at
<div n="lb"/>Kurukṣetra, the river Sarasvatī went there under the
<div n="lb"/>name Sureṇu (Oghavatī) and watered the land.
<div n="lb"/>(Śalya Parva, Chapter 38, Verses 26 and 27).
