VṚṢAPARVĀ¦. II. An ancient royal hermit. It is stated
<div n="lb"/>in Mahābhārata, Vana Parva, Chapter 156, Stanza 15,
<div n="lb"/>that an ethereal voice was heard by the Pāṇḍavas, who
<div n="lb"/>were living in the forest, that they should visit this royal
<div n="lb"/>hermit. Accordingly the Pāṇḍavas visited the hermit
<div n="lb"/>and he received them cordially. This King rendered
<div n="lb"/>them various helps such as giving them directions for
<div n="lb"/>going through the forests. It is stated in Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 177, that on the return journey also the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas entered the hermitage of this hermit and
<div n="lb"/>received hospitality.
