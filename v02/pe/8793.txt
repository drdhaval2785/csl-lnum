YUVANĀŚVA I¦. A King of the Ikṣvāku dynasty.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} From Viṣṇu were descended in the
<div n="lb"/>following order:—Brahmā-Marīci-Kaśyapa-Vivasvān-
[Page900-a+ 44]
<div n="lb"/>Vaivasvata Manu-Ikṣvāku-Vikukṣi-Śaśāda-Kakutstha
<div n="lb"/>(Purañjaya)-Anenas-Pṛthulāśva-Prasenajit-Yuvanāśva.
<div n="lb"/>This Yuvanāśva was the father of Māndhātā.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Yuvanāśva performed many Yāgas. (M.B. Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 126, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Once he drank water which had been subjected to
<div n="lb"/>special mantras (incantations) and as a result he be-
<div n="lb"/>came pregnant. Māndhātā was born by cutting open
<div n="lb"/>his belly. (For more details see under Māndhātā).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Yuvanāśva once received a wonderful sword from
<div n="lb"/>King Raivata. He presented that sword to King Raghu.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Śānti Parva, Chapter 166, Verse 78).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) In Mahābhārata, Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 115,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 61, it is stated that he had received “Parāvarata-
<div n="lb"/>ttva” (ability to know the highest and the lowest) be-
<div n="lb"/>cause he had renounced meat-eating.
