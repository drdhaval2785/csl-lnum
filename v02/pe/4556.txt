MLECCHA¦. A tribe of people of ancient India. This tribe
<div n="lb"/>was born from the tail of the celestial cow Nandinī,
<div n="lb"/>kept by Vasiṣṭha for sacrificial purposes when there was
<div n="lb"/>a fight between Viśvāmitra and Vasiṣṭha. Mahābhārata
<div n="lb"/>gives the following information regarding them.
<div n="NI"/>(1) The mlecchas who sprang up from the tail of the
<div n="lb"/>celestial cow Nandinī sent the army of Viśvāmitra
<div n="lb"/>flying in terror. (Śloka 38, Chapter 174, Ādi Parva).
[Page504-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(2) Bhīmasena defeated the mlecchas living in the
<div n="lb"/>coastal regions and took from them several valuable
<div n="lb"/>diamonds as tax. (Śloka 25, Chapter 30, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(3) The mlecchas living in the coastal area were once
<div n="lb"/>defeated by Sahadeva, one of the Pāṇḍavas. (Śloka 66,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 31, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Nakula also once defeated the mlecchas. (Śloka 16,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 32, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Bhagadatta was the King of the mlecchas. (Śloka 14,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 51, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Bhagadatta accompanied by the mlecchas living on
<div n="lb"/>the coasts attended the Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>14, Chapter 51, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Different tribes of mlecchas will be born on earth
<div n="lb"/>at the beginning of the era of Pralaya. (Śloka 34,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 188, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Kalkī, the incarnation of Viṣṇu, will destroy the
<div n="lb"/>mlecchas who are found everywhere in the world.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 97 Chapter 190, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Karṇa during his world campaign conquered many
<div n="lb"/>mleccha countries. (Śloka 19, Chapter 254, Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(10) A place of habitation in Bhārata is called
<div n="lb"/>Mleccha. (Śloka 57, Chapter 9. Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(11) Aṅga, a mleccha warrior was killed in the battle
<div n="lb"/>by Bhīmasena. (Śloka 17, Chapter 26, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(12) Once the mlecchas attacked Arjuna with arrows.
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna killed the hairy soldiers. (Śloka 43, Chapter 93,
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(13) Sātyaki killed many mleccha soldiers in the great
<div n="lb"/>war. (Śloka 43, Chapter 119, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(14) Nakula killed Aṅga, a mleccha King. (Śloka 18,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 22, Karṇa Parva),
<div n="NI"/>(15) Arjuna had to face a great army of mlecchas to
<div n="lb"/>protect the Yāgāśva. (Śloka 23, Chapter 73, Aśvamedha
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(16) The wealth that remained in the Yāgaśālā of
<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira after the distribution as gifts to brahmins
<div n="lb"/>was taken away by the mlecchas. (Śloka 26, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>89, Aśvamedha Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(17) The mlecchas drove angered elephants on to the
<div n="lb"/>army of the Pāṇḍavas. (Śloka 10, Chapter 22, Karṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
