VĀṬADHĀNA¦(M). A country in ancient India. The
<div n="lb"/>people of this country were called the Vāṭadhānas. The
<div n="lb"/>following information is available about them from the
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Nakula defeated the Vāṭadhānas who were Kṣatri-
<div n="lb"/>yas at the time of his regional conquest. (M.B. Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 32, Stanza 8).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Once the Kauravas surrounded this country which
<div n="lb"/>abounded in wealth and food. (M.B. Udyoga Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 19, Stanza 31).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Vāṭadhāna was one of the foremost countries in
<div n="lb"/>India. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 9, Stanza 47).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) In the Garuḍa Vyūha of the army formed by
<div n="lb"/>Bhīṣma, Aśvatthāmā and the Vāṭadhānas stood at the
<div n="lb"/>head of disposition, in the Bhārata-Battle. (M.B. Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 27, Stanza 4).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Once Śrī Kṛṣṇa defeated the Vāṭadhānas. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa Parva, Chapter 11, Stanza, 17).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Karṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 73, Stanza 17 that in Bhārata-battle Arjuna
<div n="lb"/>destroyed all the armies of the country of Vāṭadhāna.
