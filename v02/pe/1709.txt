DAṆḌA I¦. A king who was the son of Ikṣvāku. It is
<div n="lb"/>mentioned in Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 67,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 45, that this King was the rebirth of Krodha-
<div n="lb"/>hantā, an asura.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Birth.%} Ikṣvāku had one hundred sons. Of them
<div n="lb"/>Vikukṣi, Nimi and Daṇḍa were famous. Daṇḍa became
<div n="lb"/>a famous archer when he grew up. In the Brahmāṇḍa
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa mention is made that this King Daṇḍa took
<div n="lb"/>part in the Devāsura battle (Battle between the gods
<div n="lb"/>and the demons) and killed several thousand asuras
<div n="lb"/>(demons).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Administration.%} The king Ikṣvāku gave his son
<div n="lb"/>Daṇḍa the country between the mountains Himālaya
<div n="lb"/>and Vindhya and anointed him the King of that coun-
<div n="lb"/>try. Daṇḍa built a capital city known as Madhumatta
<div n="lb"/>and began to rule the country. He had an army of
<div n="lb"/>Caturaṅga (four parts, elephant, chariot, horse and
<div n="lb"/>infantry). The hermit Śamana was the priest of King
<div n="lb"/>Daṇḍa. (Uttararāmāyaṇa).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%The origin of Daṇḍakāranya (the forest of Daṇḍaka).%}
<div n="lb"/>Daṇḍa once raped Arā, the daughter of hermit Śukra,
<div n="lb"/>who had been doing penance in a forest in the middle
<div n="lb"/>of the country of Daṇḍa. Śukra the hermit got angry and
<div n="lb"/>destroyed the country of Daṇḍa by a shower of fire.
<div n="lb"/>From that day onwards that country was known as
<div n="lb"/>Daṇḍakāraṇya. (For further information see under
<div n="lb"/>Arā).
