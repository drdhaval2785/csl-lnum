UNMĀDINĪ¦. A beautiful woman. She was the daughter
<div n="lb"/>of a Vaiśya in the city of Śrāvastī. He went to the King
<div n="lb"/>Devasena and requested him to marry his daughter.
<div n="lb"/>Because of the interference of his favourites, the King
<div n="lb"/>did not marry her. The King's general married her.
<div n="lb"/>Once the King happened to see her by chance. When
<div n="lb"/>he saw how beautiful she was, he felt sorry that he did
<div n="lb"/>not marry her. From that day onwards the King grew
<div n="lb"/>morbidly torpid and finally died.
<div n="NI"/>This story was told by Yaugandharāyaṇa, minister of
<div n="lb"/>Udayana, the King of Vatsa. (Kathāsaritsāgara,
<div n="lb"/>Lāvāṇakalambaka; Taraṅga 1).
