SOMAKA II¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} A King of Pāñcāla. This gener-
<div n="lb"/>ous King was the son of Sahadeva and the grandson of
<div n="lb"/>Subhāsa. In accordance with the advice of Brahmins,
<div n="lb"/>the King once sacrificed his son. It is mentioned in
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata, Vana Parva, Chapter 128 that on
<div n="lb"/>completion of the sacrifice he got one hundred sons.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) This King Somaka travelled with his priests,
<div n="lb"/>through the holy worlds and hell and returned. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Vana Parva, Chapter 128, Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) King Somaka made offerings of cows as alms and
<div n="lb"/>attained heaven. (M.B. Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 76,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Never had he eaten flesh in his life. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 115, Verse 63).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Mention is made in Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 8, Stanza 8, that this Somaka stays in the
<div n="lb"/>palace of Yama praising him.
