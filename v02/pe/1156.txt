BHĪṢMAKA¦. King of Vidarbha and born in the Bhoja
<div n="lb"/>dynasty, Bhīṣmaka was the father of Rukmiṇī, the wife
<div n="lb"/>of Śrī Kṛṣṇa. He had five sons. A friend of Bhīṣma,
<div n="lb"/>he conquered and ruled over a fourth of the world.
<div n="lb"/>He defeated the Krathas, Pāṇḍyas and Kaiśikas. He
<div n="lb"/>was very much devoted to Jarāsandha. (Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 14, Verse 21). Sahadeva, who was on a trium-
<div n="lb"/>phal tour in connection with the Pāṇḍava's aśvamedha
<div n="lb"/>yajña fought and defeated Bhīṣmaka at Bhojakaṭanagara.
<div n="lb"/>Bhīṣmaka was also called Hiraṇyaroma. (Sabhā Parva,

<div n="lb"/>Chapter 31, Udyoga Parva, Chapter 158, Śānti Parva,
