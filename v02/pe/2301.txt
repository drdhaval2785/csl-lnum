GĀDHI I¦. Father of Viśvāmitra.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu thus: Brahmā-
<div n="lb"/>Atri-Candra-Budha-Purūravas-Āyus-Nahuṣa-Yayāti
<div n="lb"/>Pūru-Janamejaya-Prācinvān-Pravīra-Namasyu-Vīta-
<div n="lb"/>bhaya-Śuṇḍu-Bahuvidha-Saṃyāti-Rahovādi-Raudrāśva-
<div n="lb"/>Matināra-Santurodha-Duṣyanta-Bharata (Vitatha)-
<div n="lb"/>Suhotra-Bṛhatputra-Ajamīḍha-Jahnu-Balākāśva-Kuśika
<div n="lb"/>(Kuśa)-Gādhi.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Birth.%} Kuśa had by his wife Vaidarbhī four sons
<div n="lb"/>called Kuśāmba, Kuśanābha, Asūrtarajas and Vasu.
<div n="lb"/>Of the four sons Kuśāmba built a city called Kauśāmbī,
<div n="lb"/>Kuśanābha built the city of Mahodayapura, Asūrta-
<div n="lb"/>rajas the city of Dharmāraṇya and Vasu the city of
<div n="lb"/>Girivraja, and they administered their respective cities.
<div n="lb"/>Kuśanābha had hundred daughters by his wife Ghṛtācī,
<div n="lb"/>an Apsarā woman. He felt very sad that he had no
<div n="lb"/>sons. At last he performed the Putrakāmeṣṭi yajña,
<div n="lb"/>and Gādhi was born to him.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Gādhi's children.%} A son named Viśvāmitra and a
<div n="lb"/>daughter called Satyavatī were born to Gādhi. Satya-
<div n="lb"/>vatī was married to sage Ṛcīka. After the wedding of
<div n="lb"/>his daughter Gādhi crowned Viśvāmitra as the king
<div n="lb"/>and left for the forest for tapas. During his stay in the
<div n="lb"/>forest Gādhi was put up in the āśrama of Ṛcīka for a
<div n="lb"/>long time and he also went on many pilgrimages. (Śānti
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 49). (See also Satyavatī and Viśvā-
<div n="lb"/>mitra.
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Death.%} Gādhi entered mahāsamādhi and went to
<div n="lb"/>heaven. (Śalya Parva, Chapter 16).
