KĀKṢĪVĀN¦. A son of Maharṣi Gautama. Mahābhārata,
<div n="lb"/>Sabhā Parva, Chapter 21, Verse 3 states that at the
<div n="lb"/>time when Gautama was living in Girivraja, he had
<div n="lb"/>connection with a Śūdra woman of Uśīnara land and
<div n="lb"/>as a result of it Kākṣīvān was born to her. This
<div n="lb"/>Gautama was not the husband of Ahalyā.
<F>1) It is also stated elsewhere that Kākṣīvān was the son of Dīrghatamas, the father of Gautama. See under Diṛghatamas.</F>
<div n="lb"/>Kākṣīvān had a son named Caṇḍakauśika and two
<div n="lb"/>daughters named Bhadrā and Ghoṣā. Mahābhārata,
<div n="lb"/>Sabhā Parva, Chapter 4, Verse 17 states that Kākṣīvān
<div n="lb"/>flourished in Yudhiṣṭhira's assembly.
