SIṂHALA¦. Modern Ceylon. In ancient days this place
<div n="lb"/>was called Siṃhala and the inhabitants were called
<div n="lb"/>Siṃhalas. The following statements about Siṃhala
<div n="lb"/>occur in the Mahābhārata.
[Page718-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(1) The low caste people of the Siṃhalas originated
<div n="lb"/>from the sides of Nandinī, the cow of the Devas. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Ādi Parva, Chapter 174, Verse 37).
<div n="NI"/>(2) The king of Siṃhala had taken part in the sacrifice
<div n="lb"/>Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira. (Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 34, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(3) The Kṣatriyas of Siṃhala gave Yudhiṣṭhira, Chry-
<div n="lb"/>soprases, pearls and such other wealth of the sea as pre-
<div n="lb"/>sent. The people of Siṃhala wore clothes studded with
<div n="lb"/>jewels. They were of dark complexion with eyes the
<div n="lb"/>ends of which were red. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>58, Verse 35).
<div n="NI"/>(4) The Siṃhalas took the side of the Kauravas in the
<div n="lb"/>battle of Bhārata. In the Garuḍa vyūha formed by
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa, the Siṃhalas were stationed in the position of
<div n="lb"/>the neck. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 6).
