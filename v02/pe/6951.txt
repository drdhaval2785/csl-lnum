SUDHANVĀ II¦. Son of hermit Aṅgiras. Some inform-
<div n="lb"/>ation gathered from Ṛgveda and Mahābhārata is
<div n="lb"/>given below.
<div n="NI"/>(i) There arose a contest between Sudhanvā and
<div n="lb"/>Virocana, the son of Prahlāda for marrying the damsel
<div n="lb"/>Keśinī. (For detailed story see under Keśinī V).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) This hermit visited Bhīṣma in his bed of arrows.
<div n="NI"/>(M.B. Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 26, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Sudhanvā was the eighth son of Aṅgiras.

<div n="NI"/>(iv) It is stated in Mahābhārata, Anuśāsana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 86, Verse 24, that this Sudhanvā gave
<div n="lb"/>Subrahmaṇya a chariot, in the battle between the
<div n="lb"/>Devas and the asuras.
<div n="NI"/>(v) Sudhanvā had three sons called Ṛbhu, Vibhvā
<div n="lb"/>and Vāja. (Ṛgveda, Maṇḍala 1, Anuvāka 16, Sūkta
