BĀṢKALA I¦. A king of the asuras.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Birth.%} Two sons, Hiraṇyākṣa and Hiraṇyakaśipu
<div n="lb"/>and a daughter Siṃhikā, were born to Kaśyapa prajā-
<div n="lb"/>pati of his wife Diti. Siṃhikā became the wife of Vipra-
<div n="lb"/>citti. Four sons, Anuhrāda, Hrāda, Prahlāda and Saṃh-
<div n="lb"/>lāda, were born to Hiraṇyakaśipu. Hrāda was the son
[Page108-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>of Hrada. Three sons Āyuṣmān, Śibi and Bāṣkala were
<div n="lb"/>born to Saṃhrāda.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Later history.%} Bāṣkala became one of the ministers
<div n="lb"/>of the famous and mighty hero Mahiṣāsura. Cikṣura
<div n="lb"/>was the minister of law and order. Tāmra was the Finance
<div n="lb"/>Minister. Asiloma was the Prime Minister and Biḍāla
<div n="lb"/>the minister of foreign affairs. Udarkka was the general
<div n="lb"/>of the army; Bāṣkala, Trinetra and Kālabandhaka were
<div n="lb"/>members of the administrative council. Śukrācārya was
<div n="lb"/>the minister of education. Bāṣkala played an important
<div n="lb"/>part in the battle between Mahiṣāsura and Indra.
<div n="lb"/>When soldiers were killed by thousands, Mahiṣāsura
<div n="lb"/>sent lastly, Bāṣkala and Durmukha. Bāṣkala engaged
<div n="lb"/>himself in a combat with Devī who fought on the side
<div n="lb"/>of the Devas. Bāṣkala became angry and aiming at the
<div n="lb"/>breast of Devī he gave a severe blow with his club.
<div n="lb"/>But Devī evaded him with her club and gave him a
<div n="lb"/>severe thrust with her trident at his breast and Bāṣkala
<div n="lb"/>fell dead. (Agni Purāṇa, Chapter 19; Devī Bhāgavata,
<div n="lb"/>Skandha 5).
