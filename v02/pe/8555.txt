VIŚVĀVASU II¦. A Gandharva King. The following
<div n="lb"/>information about this King is taken from the Purāṇas.
<div n="NI"/>(i) The father of this Devagandharva was Prajāpati
<div n="lb"/>Kaśyapa and his mother was Pradhā. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 25, Stanza 47).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Pramadvarā was born to Viśvāvasu by the celestial
<div n="lb"/>maid Menakā. (See under Pramadvarā).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Viśvāvasu was present at the Birth-festival of
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 122, Stanza 22).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) This Viśvāvasu learned from Soma, Cākṣuṣīvidyā
<div n="lb"/>(the art of seeing all) and taught Citraratha, a
<div n="lb"/>Gandharva the same art. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 169,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 43).
<div n="NI"/>(v) He was present at the Svayaṃvara (marriage) of
<div n="lb"/>Draupadī. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 186, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Viśvāvasu stays in the palace of Indra glorifying
<div n="lb"/>him. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 7, Stanza 22).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 10, Stanza 25, that he stays in the palace of
<div n="lb"/>Kubera praising him.
<div n="NI"/>(viii) This Gandharva recited a poem at the sacrifice
<div n="lb"/>performed by Jamadagni. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>90, Stanza 18).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Kabandha, the asura, who stopped Rāma and
[Page877-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>Lakṣmaṇa, was the changed form of Viśvāvasu by a
<div n="lb"/>curse (See under Kabandha).
<div n="NI"/>(x) It was this Viśvāvasu who played the lute in the
<div n="lb"/>sacrifice performed by emperor Dilīpa. (M.B. Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 61, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) Once Viśvāvasu asked hermit Yājñavalkya twenty-
<div n="lb"/>four questions. When he got satisfactory answers, the
<div n="lb"/>Gandharva returned to heaven. (M.B. Śānti Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 318, Stanza 26).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) It was Viśvāvasu and some other Gandharvas who
<div n="lb"/>took away Urvaśī from Purūravas. (See under Purūra-
<div n="lb"/>vas).
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) At the time of emperor Pṛthu, when the earth was
<div n="lb"/>milked, the thing the Gandharvas got were those befitt-
<div n="lb"/>ing them, In the course of the milking Viśvāvasu stood
<div n="lb"/>as the calf. (Bhāgavata, Skandha 4).
