VASUMĀN I¦. (VASUMANAS). A King of the dynasty
<div n="lb"/>of Ikṣvāku.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu in the following
<div n="lb"/>order. Brahmā-Marīci-Kaśyapa-Vivasvān-Vaivasvata
<div n="lb"/>Manu-Ikṣvāku-Vikukṣi-Śaśāda-Kakutstha (Purañjaya)-
<div n="lb"/>Anenas-Pṛthulāśva-Viṣvagaśva-Prasenajit-Yuvanāśva-
<div n="lb"/>Māndhātā-Purukutsa-Trasadasyu-Anaraṇya-Haryaśva-
<div n="lb"/>Vasumanas.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Birth.%} Haryaśva, the King of Ayodhyā married
<div n="lb"/>Mādhavī, the daughter of Yayāti. Vasumanas was their
<div n="lb"/>son. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 86, Stanza 56).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Marriage proposal.%} A damsel named Hariṇī was
<div n="lb"/>proposed to be given in marriage to Vasumanas. But
<div n="lb"/>another man carried her away. To know the story see
<div n="lb"/>under Durmada II.
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Once Yayāti who fell from heaven on the earth
<div n="lb"/>met with Vasumanas. Because of the merit of mingl-
<div n="lb"/>ing with good people Yayāti attained heaven again.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 86, Stanza 56).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Vasumanas entered heaven along with Kings
[Page839-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>like Aṣṭaka and others. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 93,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 16).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Vasumanas sits in the palace of Yama and praises
<div n="lb"/>him. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 8, Stanza 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) He went on pilgrimages and earned fame and
<div n="lb"/>wealth. (M.B. Vana Parva Chapter 94, Stanza 17).
<div n="NI"/>(v) He was present at the horse-sacrifice of Aṣṭaka the
<div n="lb"/>son of Viśvāmitra. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 198,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 1).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) He once got into the aerial chariot of Indra and
<div n="lb"/>came and stood over the city of Virāṭa and witnessed
<div n="lb"/>the battle between Arjuna and the teacher Kṛpa.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 56, Stanza 9).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) It was near Vasumanas and others who had been
<div n="lb"/>performing the sacrifice of Vājapeya and worshipping
<div n="lb"/>Mahāviṣṇu, that Yayāti fell from heaven. (M.B. Udyoga
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 121, Stanza 10).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Vasumanas became famous under the name
<div n="lb"/>Dānapati (lord of donations) because he had given Yayāti,
<div n="lb"/>the fruits of his good deeds. (M.B. Udyoga Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 122, Stanza 9).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) While he was the King of Kosala he conducted a
<div n="lb"/>consultation with Bṛhaspati as to how he could bring
<div n="lb"/>about the prosperity of the country. (M.B. Śānti Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 68, Stanza 6).
