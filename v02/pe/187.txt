AMĀVĀSĪ¦. In Amarakośa we read about Amāvāsī:
<div n="lb"/>“Amāvāsyā tvamāvasyā darśaḥ sūryendusaṃgamaḥ”.
<div n="lb"/>Amāvāsī means New Moon. “Amā” means “Saha”.
<div n="lb"/>So Amāvāsī is the Union of Sun and Moon in the same
<div n="lb"/>rāśi.
<div n="NI"/>Once Bhṛgu Mahaṛsi cursed Agni. At that time Agni
<div n="lb"/>explained the importance of Amāvāsī. The oblations
<div n="lb"/>which are offered as homa into the fire become the food
<div n="lb"/>of the Devas and Pitṛs. Substances offered as homa on
<div n="lb"/>Purṇamāsī become food of the Devas and those offered
<div n="lb"/>on Amāvāsī become food of the Pitṛs. (M.B., Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 7).
