DEVĪPĪṬHA¦. The dead body of Satīdevī crumbled into
<div n="lb"/>small pieces and fell in different places in Bhārata.
<div n="lb"/>Each place where a piece of the dead body fell, is known
<div n="lb"/>by the name Devīpīṭha. There is a reason why the
<div n="lb"/>dead body crumbled into pieces.
<div n="NI"/>Dakṣa performed a sacrifice to which Śiva was not invit-
<div n="lb"/>ed and Satīdevī came to the sacrifice, and committed
<div n="lb"/>suicide by jumping into the fire. Śiva who became furi-
<div n="lb"/>ous, killed Dakṣa and carrying the dead body of his
<div n="lb"/>wife on his shoulder, walked the length and breadth of
<div n="lb"/>Bhārata like a mad man. It seemed that he was not
<div n="lb"/>going to recover from this mania, and the devas (gods)
<div n="lb"/>were worried at this. To rescue Śiva from this mental
<div n="lb"/>disposition, Mahāviṣṇu, unseen by others, followed
<div n="lb"/>Śiva with a bow and arrow. Whenever it was conve-
<div n="lb"/>nient Mahāviṣṇu sent an arrow at the body of Satīdevī,
<div n="lb"/>on the shoulder of Śiva. By the hitting of the arrows
<div n="lb"/>the body was crumbled into pieces and fell here and
<div n="lb"/>there. Thus within a few days the body of Satīdevī
<div n="lb"/>came to an end and Śiva walked to Kailāsa. The
<div n="lb"/>pieces of the dead body fell in 108 places, and thus 108
<div n="lb"/>Devīpīṭhas came into existence. The names of the
<div n="lb"/>places and the names by which the Devī is known in
<div n="lb"/>such places are given below.
