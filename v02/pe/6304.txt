SARAYŪ I¦. A river very famous in the Purāṇas. The
<div n="lb"/>most important things associated with the river are
<div n="lb"/>given below:
<div n="NI"/>(i) Seven tributaries of Gaṅgā originate from the
<div n="lb"/>golden peaks of the Himālayas and Sarayū is one of
<div n="lb"/>them. Those who bathe in the river will be absolved
<div n="lb"/>from sins. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 169, Verse 20)
<div n="NI"/>(ii) The river exists in Varuṇa's court worshipping him.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 8).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Śrī Kṛṣṇa, Arjuna and Bhīma, on their way to
<div n="lb"/>Girivraja from Indraprastha crossed this river. (Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 28).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It was at Gotāra (or Gopratara) in this river that
<div n="lb"/>Śrī Rāma drowned himself to death and attained
<div n="lb"/>Viṣṇupāda. (Vana Parva, Chapter 84, Verse 70).
<div n="NI"/>(v) This river is the source of Agni (fire). (Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 222, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Vasiṣṭha once blocked the course of Gaṅgā on its
<div n="lb"/>way to Kailāsa at Mānasasarovara. But, Gaṅgā broke
<div n="lb"/>the obstruction and flowed on, and Sarayū is the
<div n="lb"/>stream that started from there. (Anuśāsana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 155, Verse 23).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) It is one of the rivers to be remembered both at
<div n="lb"/>dawn and dusk. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 165, Verse
<div n="lb"/>21).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The city of Ayodhyā is situated on the banks of
<div n="lb"/>Sarayū. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Bālakāṇḍa, Canto 5,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 5).
