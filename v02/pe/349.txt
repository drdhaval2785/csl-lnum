ANTARĪKṢA II¦. A Rājā named Antarīkṣa is men-
<div n="lb"/>tioned in the Bhāgavata.
<div n="NI"/>{%Genealogy.%} Viṣṇu, Brahmā, Svāyambhuva, Priyavrata,
<div n="lb"/>Agnīdhra, Nābhi, Ṛṣabha, Antarīkṣa. Svāyambhuva
<div n="lb"/>Manu had two sons, Uttānapāda and Priyavrata.
<div n="lb"/>Dhruva was the son of Uttānapāda. Priyavrata married
<div n="lb"/>Barhiṣmatī, and they had eleven children including
<div n="lb"/>Agnīdhra. Agnīdhra married Pūrvacitti, a Deva wo-
<div n="lb"/>man, and they became parents to nine sons, viz. Nābhi,
<div n="lb"/>Kimpuruṣa, Hari, Ilāvṛta, Ramyaka, Hiraṇmaya,
<div n="lb"/>Kuru, Bhadrāśva and Ketumāla. Nābhi wedded Meru-
<div n="lb"/>devī, and to them were born 100 sons. The eldest son
<div n="lb"/>was Bharata, after whom this country (India) is
<div n="lb"/>named, i.e., Bhārata. Bhārata had 9 younger brothers,
<div n="lb"/>namely Kuśāvarta, Ilāvarta, Brahmāvarta, Malaya,
<div n="lb"/>Ketu, Bhadrasena, Indraspṛk, Vidarbha and Kīkaṭa.
<F>*) Rāmānuja's Bhāgavata (Malayalam) mentions one Āryāvarta also as Bharata's younger brother. But the name does not occur in the
<div n="lb"/>original. (See Bhāgavata, Chapter 4, Verse 10.)</F>
<div n="NI"/>These brothers had nine younger brothers who were all
<div n="lb"/>highly evolved yogīs. They were Kavi, Hari, Anta-
<div n="lb"/>rīkṣa, Prabuddha, Pippalāyana, Āvirhotra, Dramiḍa,
<div n="lb"/>Camasa and Karabhājana. (Bhāgavata, Pañcama
<div n="lb"/>Skandha, Chapters 1-4).
