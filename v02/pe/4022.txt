LOMAPĀDA I¦. (ROMAPĀDA). A King of the country
<div n="lb"/>of Aṅga.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descending in order from Viṣṇu: Brahmā—
<div n="lb"/>Atri—Candra—Budha—Purūravas—Āyus—Nahuṣa—
<div n="lb"/>Yayāti—Turvasu Vahni—Bharga-Bhānu-Tribhānu—
<div n="lb"/>Karandhama—Marutta—(Marutta adopted Duṣyanta)
<div n="lb"/>Duṣyanta—Varūtha—Gāṇḍīra—Gāndhāra—Kerala—
<div n="lb"/>Cola—Pāṇḍya—Kola—Druhyu—Babhrusetu—Puro-
<div n="lb"/>vasu—Gharma—Kṛta—Vidūṣa—Pracetas—Sabhānara—
<div n="lb"/>Kālānala—Śṛñjaya—Purañjaya—Janamejaya—Mahā-
<div n="lb"/>śāla—Mahāmanas—Uśīnara—Titikṣu—Ruṣadratha—
<div n="lb"/>Paila—Sutapas—Bali—Aṅga—Dadhivāhana—Dravī-
<div n="lb"/>ratha—Dharmaratha—Citraratha—Satyaratha—Loma-
<div n="lb"/>pāda.
[Page457-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) He was a friend of Daśaratha. (Śloka 53, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>110, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Once there was no rain in the country of Loma-
<div n="lb"/>pāda. It was due to a curse from the brahmins and to
<div n="lb"/>remove the curse Lomapāda brought Ṛṣyaśṛṅga to his
<div n="lb"/>country. Śāntā, daughter of Daśaratha was living with
<div n="lb"/>Lomapāda as his adopted daughter at that time.
<div n="lb"/>Lomapāda gave her in marriage to Ṛṣyaśṛṅga and
<div n="lb"/>made him live in his country. The country got rains
<div n="lb"/>from that time onwards. (See under Ṛṣyaśṛṅga and
<div n="lb"/>Śāntā).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Lomapāda constructed a new hermitage for Ṛṣya-
<div n="lb"/>śṛṅga. (Śloka 9, Chapter 113, Vana Parva).
