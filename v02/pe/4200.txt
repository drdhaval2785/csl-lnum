MĀHIṢMATĪ¦. An ancient city on the banks of river
<div n="lb"/>Narmadā. There is reference to this place in many
<div n="lb"/>places in the Purāṇas and a few of them are given
<div n="lb"/>below:
<div n="NI"/>(i) King Nīla was reigning in a country with Māhiṣ-
<div n="lb"/>matī as its capital. Sahadeva, one of the Pāṇḍavas
<div n="lb"/>during his victorious march to the south conquered
<div n="lb"/>this country. (Chapter 31, Sabhā Parva).
[Page468-a+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Daśāśva, tenth son of Ikṣvāku, had made this
<div n="lb"/>city his capital. (Śloka 6, Chapter 2, Anuśāsana
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Kārtavīryārjuna who had made Māhiṣmatī the
<div n="lb"/>capital of his kingdom had subdued many kings.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 3, Chapter 152, Anuśāsana Parva). (See under
<div n="lb"/>Kārtavīryārjuna.)
