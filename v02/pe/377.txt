ANUVINDA II¦. The purāṇas refer to one Anuvinda, a
<div n="lb"/>prince of Avanti. (1) He had a brother called Vinda.
<div n="lb"/>(2) He was defeated by Sahadeva who set forth to sub-
<div n="lb"/>jugate the kings of the south just before the Rājasūya
<div n="lb"/>yajña of Dharmaputra. (M.B., Sabhā Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>31, Verse 10). (3) An Akṣauhiṇī (division of army)
<div n="lb"/>was despatched by Anuvinda to help Duryodhana.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B., Udyoga Parva, Chapter 19, Verses 24, 25). (4)
<div n="lb"/>On the first day of the war Vinda and Anuvinda fought
<div n="lb"/>duels with Kuntibhoja. (M.B., Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>45, Verses 72-75). (5) Irāvān, son of Arjuna defeated
<div n="lb"/>both Vinda and Anuvinda in the battle. (M.B., Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 83, Verse, 18-22). (6) They fought with
<div n="lb"/>Bhīma, Arjuna and the king of Virāṭa. (M.B., Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 25, Verses 20). (7) Anuvinda was killed
<div n="lb"/>by Arjuna. (M.B., Droṇa parva, Chapter 399, Verses
<div n="lb"/>27-29). (8) Vinda and Anuvinda had a sister called
<div n="lb"/>Mitravindā. The three of them were the children of
<div n="lb"/>Rājādhidevī, sister of Śrī Kṛṣṇa's father. Śrī Kṛṣṇa
<div n="lb"/>married Mitravindā, which her brother did not like.
<div n="lb"/>This was their reason for quitting the Pāṇḍava camp
[Page045-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>and fighting on the side of the Kauravas. (Bhāgavata,
<div n="lb"/>Daśama Skandha, Chapter 58, Verses 30, 31).
