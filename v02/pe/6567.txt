ŚIBI II¦. Inhabitants of the country called Śibi; they are
<div n="lb"/>called Śaibyas also. The following information about
<div n="lb"/>the country is gathered from Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Sunandā, mother of King Śantanu was a princess
<div n="lb"/>of this country. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse
<div n="lb"/>44).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Govāsana, father-in-law of Yudhiṣṭhira, was a
<div n="lb"/>King of Śibi. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 76).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Nakula, during his triumphal tour of the western
<div n="lb"/>regions, conquered the kingdom of Śibi. (M.B. Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 7).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) People of Śibi attended the Rājasūya yajña
<div n="lb"/>conducted by Yudhiṣṭhira with presents. (Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 52, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(v) A King called Uśīnara, once ruled the country.
<div n="lb"/>(Vana Parva, Chapter 131, Verse 21).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) The kingdom of Śibi was once under the jurisdic-
<div n="lb"/>tion of Jayadratha. (Vana Parva, Chapter 267, Verse
<div n="lb"/>11).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Arjuna annihilated the armies of the kingdom of
<div n="lb"/>Śibi which followed Jayadratha to the battlefield.
<div n="lb"/>(Vana Parva, Chapter 271, Verse 28).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The mahārathas of Śibi, with their armies,
<div n="lb"/>arrayed under the banner of Duryodhana to fight the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 195, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Karṇa once subjugated the kingdom of Śibi.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 91, Verse 38).

<div n="NI"/>(x) The people of the country in earlier days were
<div n="lb"/>illiterate and ignorant. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 45,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 34).
