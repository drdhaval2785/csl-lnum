ŚAKA¦. A particular sect of people or caste. The following
<div n="lb"/>information about the Śakas is gathered from the
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Śakas were born from the breast of Nandinī, Vasiṣ-
<div n="lb"/>ṭha's cow. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 147, Verse 36).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Bhīmasena, during his triumphal tour of the eastern
<div n="lb"/>regions subjugated the Śakas. (Sabhā Parva. Chapter
<div n="lb"/>32, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Nakula conquered them. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>32, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) King of the Śakas participated in Yudhiṣṭhira's Rāja-
<div n="lb"/>sūya. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 51, Verse 32).
<div n="NI"/>(v) The Pāṇḍavas invited them to take part in the
<div n="lb"/>great war. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 4, Verse 15).
[Page667-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(vi) They did along with Sudakṣiṇa, king of Kāmboja,
<div n="lb"/>service in Duryodhana's army. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>19, Verse 21).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Śrī Kṛṣṇa once conquered them. (Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 119, Verse 45).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Karṇa once defeated them and conquered their
<div n="lb"/>country. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 8, Verse 18).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Śakas who were originally Kṣatriyas were demoted
<div n="lb"/>as Śūdras as they incurred the displeasure and anger of
<div n="lb"/>brahmins. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 33, Verse 21).
<div n="NI"/>(x) As the Śakas and the Yavanas had helped the
<div n="lb"/>Haihaya kings Paraśurāma, Sagara and Bharata defeat-
<div n="lb"/>ed them in war and drove them off from the country.
<div n="lb"/>(Bhāgavata, Navama Skandha).
