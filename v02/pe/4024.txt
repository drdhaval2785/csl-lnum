LOMAŚA¦ I (ROMAŚA) I. A sage, who was a great
<div n="lb"/>story-teller. Many of the stories found as episodes in the
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇas were told by this sage. Mahābhārata gives the
<div n="lb"/>following details about him.
<div n="NI"/>(1) Lomaśa was very virtuous and longlived. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>18, Chapter 31, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Once Lomaśa entered the court of Indra and spoke
<div n="lb"/>to Indra about Arjuna sitting sharing a half of his seat
<div n="lb"/>before him. (Śloka 1, Chapter 47, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Lomaśa returned to Kāmyakavana from the court
<div n="lb"/>of Indra and gave Yudhiṣṭhira the messages from Indra
<div n="lb"/>and Arjuna. (Śloka 33, Chapter 47, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(4) He prophesied to Arjuna that he would get divine
<div n="lb"/>weapons from Śiva. (Śloka 10, Chapter 91, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Lomaśa consoled Yudhiṣṭhira when he came to the
[Page458-a+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>forests in exile leaving all his happy surroundings in the
<div n="lb"/>country. (Śloka 17, Chapter 94, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(6) He told Dharmaputra the story of Agastya.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 96, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Lomaśa told Dharmaputra the stories of Śrī
<div n="lb"/>Rāma and Paraśurāma. (Śloka 40, Chapter 99, Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Asuras defeated all the Devas and the latter were
<div n="lb"/>perplexed. It was Lomaśa who then advised them to
<div n="lb"/>make weapons out of the bones of the sage Dadhīca.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 100, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Lomaśa mentioned to the Devas a trick to kill
<div n="lb"/>Vṛtrāsura. (Chapter 101, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(10) The story of Bhagīratha, the episode of Ṛṣyasṛṅga
<div n="lb"/>etc. were all told by Lomaśa. (Chapters 103 to 113,
<div n="lb"/>Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(11) Lomaśa told Yudhiṣṭhira the story of the Yāga of
<div n="lb"/>King Gaya, the story of the river Payoṣṇī, the glory of
<div n="lb"/>the mountain of Vaiḍūrya and the river Narmadā, and
<div n="lb"/>the story of Cyavana the great sage. (Chapter 121,
<div n="lb"/>Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(12) He told the Pāṇḍavas the story of Māndhātā.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 126, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(13) Lomaśa told Yudhiṣṭhira the story of Somaka and
<div n="lb"/>Jantu. (Chapter 127, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(14) He praised the glory of the holy place Yamunā-
<div n="lb"/>tīrtha, known as Plakṣaprasravaṇa also, near Kuru-
<div n="lb"/>kṣetra. Chapter 129, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(15) It was Lomaśa who told the story of the emperor
<div n="lb"/>Śibi who gave his own flesh to protect a dove which
<div n="lb"/>went to him for refuge. (Chapter 130, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(16) Lomaśa told the story of the sage Aṣṭāvakra to the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas. (Chapter 133, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(17) He described the story of Yavakrīta and Medhāvī
<div n="lb"/>to the Pāṇḍavas. (Chapter 135, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(18) Dharmaputra heard the stories of Bharadvāja,
<div n="lb"/>Raibhya, Arvāvasu and Parāvasu from Lomaśa.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 137, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(19) Lomaśa told Dharmaputra the story of Narakā-
<div n="lb"/>sura and the incarnation of Viṣṇu as Varāha. (Chapter
<div n="lb"/>142, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(20) Lomaśa visited Bhīṣma lying on his bed of arrows.
<div n="lb"/>(Śānti Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(21) Lomaśa praised the glory of giving rice as charity,
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 10, Chapter 67, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(22) Lomaśa was a sage from the north. (Śloka 46,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 165, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(23) Lomaśa relieved many nymphs like Pramohinī
<div n="lb"/>from their curses. (See under Pramohinī).
