<div n="lb"/>name is Rājagṛha. The Purāṇas mention many great
<div n="lb"/>Kings who had ruled Magadha. Bṛhadratha was once
<div n="lb"/>King of Magadha. (Śloka 30, Chapter 63, Ādi Parva)
<div n="lb"/>Jayatsena one of the Kālakeyas was once the King of
<div n="lb"/>Magadha. (Śloka 48, Chapter 67, Ādi Parva). Pāṇḍu
<div n="lb"/>father of the Pāṇḍavas once attacked Magadha and
<div n="lb"/>Dīrgha the then King of Magadha was killed by Pāṇḍu.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 112, Ādi Parva). While Bṛhadratha was ruling
<div n="lb"/>Magadha he passed an order that each and every house
<div n="lb"/>should worship the demoness Jarā as a house-goddess.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 10, Chapter 13, Sabhā Parva). During the time
<div n="lb"/>of the Mahābhārata Jarāsandha was the King of
<div n="lb"/>Magadha. Śrī Kṛṣṇa made Bhīmasena kill Jarāsandha.
<div n="lb"/>After his death Śrī Kṛṣṇa made Sahadeva brother of
<div n="lb"/>Jarāsandha the King of Magadha. (Śloka 43, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>24, Sabhā Parva). Bhīmasena conquered this country

<div n="lb"/>offered gifts to Yudhiṣṭhira at his Rājasūya. (Śloka 18,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 52, Sabhā Parva). The King of Magadha and
<div n="lb"/>the people there fought on the side of the Pāṇḍavas in
<div n="lb"/>the great battle. (Śloka 2, Chapter 53, Udyoga Parva).
