ŚAIBYA II¦. King of the Śibi land. Mahābhārata gives
<div n="lb"/>the following pieces of information about him:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) Govāsa, King of Śibi land was the father-in-law of
<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 76).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Śaibya adorned Yudhiṣṭhira's assembly. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Sabhā Parva, Chapter 4, Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Śaibya and the King of Kāśī had come to Upa-
<div n="lb"/>plavya city with an “Akṣauhiṇī” (division of the army)
<div n="lb"/>to attend the marriage of Abhimanyu. (M.B. Virāṭa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 72, Verse 16).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Duryodhana admitted that Śaibya was the greatest
<div n="lb"/>archer in the army of the Pāṇḍavas. (M.B. Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(v) During the Bhārata Yuddha, Śaibya and the King
<div n="lb"/>of Kāśī were standing to protect Dhṛṣṭadyumna's
<div n="lb"/>“Krauñcavyūha”. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 50,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 56).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) This Śaibya was the grandson of Uśīnara. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa Parva, Chapter 10, Verse 64).
