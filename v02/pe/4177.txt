MAHENDRA II¦. One of the holy mountains. This
<div n="lb"/>mountain has great Purāṇic importance.
<div n="NI"/>(1) After slaughtering the Kṣatriyas to extinction sage
<div n="lb"/>Paraśurāma made Mahendra his place of abode. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>53, Chapter 129, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Once Arjuna visited the mountain Mahendra.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 13, Chapter 214, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(3) The presiding deity of this mountain sits in the
<div n="lb"/>court of Kubera worshipping him. (Śloka 30, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>10, Sabhā Parva).

<div n="NI"/>(4) If one bathes in the pond of Rāmatīrtha on the
<div n="lb"/>top of this mountain one would get the benefit of per-
<div n="lb"/>forming an Aśvamedhayāga. (Śloka 16, Chapter 85,
<div n="lb"/>Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Brahmā once went to this mountain and conducted
<div n="lb"/>a yāga there. (Śloka 22, Chapter 87, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Yudhiṣṭhira during his pilgrimage visited this
<div n="lb"/>mountain. (Śloka 30, Chapter 114, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Paraśurāma gave a darśana (Interview) to Yudhiṣ-
<div n="lb"/>ṭhira on a Caturdaśī day on this mountain. (Śloka 16,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 117, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Hanūmān when he was going in search of Sītā
<div n="lb"/>visited this mountain also along with the other monkeys.
<div n="lb"/>(Sundara Kāṇḍa, Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa).
