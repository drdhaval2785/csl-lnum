<div n="NI"/>C. {%Ten Commandments.%} The commandments of Christ
<div n="lb"/>are ten in number. (1) There is no other God but Yeho-
<div n="lb"/>va. (2) Do not instal idols and worship them. (3) Do
<div n="lb"/>not use God's name vainly. (4) Do not do work on the
<div n="lb"/>Sabbath day but observe that day as sacred. (5) Respect
<div n="lb"/>your parents. (6) Do not kill. (7) Do not commit adul-
<div n="lb"/>tery. (8) Do not steal. (9) Do not give false evidence.
<div n="lb"/>(10) Do not desire for others' possessions.
<div n="NI"/>D. {%The ten Kāmāvasthās.%} (1) Abhilāṣa (desire). (2)

<div n="NI"/>E. {%The ten guṇas.%} The good qualities are ten in number.
<div n="lb"/>(1) Satya (truth) (2) Vinaya (modesty) (3) Devatā-

<div n="NI"/>F. {%Ṛṣi gotras.%} There are ten Ṛṣi Gotras (families of sages):

<div n="NI"/>G. {%Camatkāras.%} There are ten kinds of Camatkāra

<div n="NI"/>H. {%Cittāvasthās.%} There are ten states of mind. (1)
<div n="lb"/>Kāma (lust) (2) Saṅkalpa (imagination) (3) Vicikitsā
<div n="lb"/>(doubt) (4) Śraddhā (attention) (5) Aśraddhā (negli-

<div n="lb"/>Lajjā (shame) (9) Dhī (devotion) (10) Bhīti (fear).
<div n="NI"/>I. {%Nādas.%} There are ten nādas (sounds) according to

<div n="NI"/>Q. {%Rūpakas.%} (dramatic compositions). There are ten
<div n="lb"/>classes of rūpakas:
<div n="NI"/>1. {%Nāṭaka. (drama).%} The chief sentiment in a drama
<div n="lb"/>should be one of the three following rasas: (1)
<div n="lb"/>Vīrarasa (sentiment of heroism) (2) Śrṅgārarasa
<div n="lb"/>(sentiment of love) (3) Karuṇarasa (pathos). There
<div n="lb"/>should be five to ten acts. The singer should be a famous
<div n="lb"/>one. It must have Pañcasandhis (five junctures).
<div n="lb"/>Śākuntala and Uttararāmacarita are perfect Nāṭakas.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Prakaraṇa.%} In this kind of drama the plot is invented
<div n="lb"/>or fictitious and deals with the social life of the day.
<div n="lb"/>The hero of the play can be either a hero or a meek
<div n="lb"/>person. The heroine can be a noble lady or a harlot.
<div n="lb"/>The main sentiment should be Śṛṅgāra (love). Mālatī-
<div n="lb"/>mādhava, Mṛcchakaṭika are examples of Prakaraṇas.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Bhāṇa.%} There is only one character and one Act in
<div n="lb"/>this species. The plot is fictitious and the hero is an
<div n="lb"/>expert humorous libertine. Līlāmadhura and Mahiṣa-
<div n="lb"/>maṅgala Bhāṇa are examples.
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Prahasana.%} This is a farce. The plot is fictitious and
<div n="lb"/>is in the nature of a low comedy. Satire is the chief
<div n="lb"/>sentiment of this species.
<div n="NI"/>5) {%Ḍima.%} This has got four Acts. The plot will be fam-
<div n="lb"/>ous and the chief sentiment will be one of wrath and
<div n="lb"/>fierceness. There will be no Viṣkambha (interlude bet-
<div n="lb"/>ween Acts to give connection to the story between Acts)
<div n="lb"/>or Praveśaka (introducer explaining portions of the
<div n="lb"/>plot which may not be put on stage but an understand-
<div n="lb"/>ing of which is necessary to follow the story). There
<div n="lb"/>must be sixteen haughty and excited characters like a
<div n="lb"/>Deva, Gandharva, Yakṣa and Rākṣasa. Tripuradahana
<div n="lb"/>is an example.
<div n="NI"/>6) {%Vyāyoga.%} Female characters will be rare in this kind
<div n="lb"/>of dramas. There will be only one Act. The sentiments
<div n="lb"/>of satire or love or calmness should not be predomin-
<div n="lb"/>ant. The hero should be a famous divine sage. Saug-
<div n="lb"/>andhikāharaṇa is an example.
<div n="NI"/>7) {%Samavakāra.%} The plot should be based on a story of
<div n="lb"/>the devas and asuras. There will be three Acts. There
[Page586-a+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>must be twelve brave heroes in it. The chief sentiment
<div n="lb"/>will be heroism. The seizure of a city, declaration of
<div n="lb"/>war or a big fire should be part of the play. Sumudra-
<div n="lb"/>mathana is an example.
<div n="NI"/>8) {%Vīthi.%} This will have one Act and one actor. Any of
<div n="lb"/>the sentiments can be portrayed. The story must be com-
<div n="lb"/>pleted by Ākāśabhāṣita (speech to which reply is given
<div n="lb"/>on the stage as if spoken from the sky) Candrikā is an
<div n="lb"/>example.
<div n="NI"/>9) {%Aṅka.%} This has only one Act. The heroes should be
<div n="lb"/>people of the prākṛta type. Pathos is the chief sentiment.
<div n="lb"/>This should contain the pathetic wail of many women.
<div n="lb"/>This is called Utsṛṣṭikāṅka also. Śarmiṣṭhā-Yayāti is an
<div n="lb"/>example.
<div n="NI"/>10. {%Īhāmṛga.%} This will contain four Acts. The heroes of
<div n="lb"/>the play should be valiant. There must be ten Patākā-
<div n="lb"/>Nāyakas (producers of different kinds of episodical incid-
<div n="lb"/>ents in the drama). They will be Uddhatas (haughty
<div n="lb"/>and excited persons).
