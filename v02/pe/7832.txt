UTTARĀ¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} Daughter of Virāṭa, the King
<div n="lb"/>of Matsya. Her brother was called Uttara.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Up to marriage.%} The Pāṇḍavas led their incognito
<div n="lb"/>life in the capital of the Matsya Kingdom. Arjuna
<div n="lb"/>adopted the name Bṛhannala and was employed as the
<div n="lb"/>tutor of princess Uttarā in music and dancing. At the
<div n="lb"/>end of the life, King Virāṭa gave his daughter Uttarā
<div n="lb"/>in marriage to Abhimanyu the son of Arjuna. (See
<div n="lb"/>the paragraph Ajñātavāsa (incognito-life) under Arjuna).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Son.%} The son born to Abhimanyu and Uttarā was
<div n="lb"/>Parīkṣit, who became a very famous King later. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Stanzas 83 and 84). See the
<div n="lb"/>seventh paragraph under Aśvatthāmā).
<div n="NI"/>4) {%The death of her husband.%} Abhimanyu was killed in
<div n="lb"/>the battle between the Pāṇḍavas and the Kauravas. It
<div n="lb"/>is mentioned in the Mahābhārata, Droṇa Parva, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 78, Stanza 37 that Uttarā fell unconscious, when she
<div n="lb"/>heard of the death of her husband. Śrī Kṛṣṇa consoled
<div n="lb"/>Uttarā. In the Mahābhārata, Strī Parva, Chapter 20,
<div n="lb"/>Stanzas 4 to 28 the lamentation of Uttarā over the death
<div n="lb"/>of Abhimanyu is mentioned.
<div n="NI"/>5) {%To the forest.%} Uttarā also was seen among the
<div n="lb"/>women who accompanied Dhṛtarāṣṭra, to a little dis-
<div n="lb"/>tance when he went to the forest after the battle. (M.B
<div n="lb"/>Āśramavāsika Parva, Chapter 15, Stanza 10).
