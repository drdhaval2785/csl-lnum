RAIBHYA I¦. A hermit who was famous in the Palace of
<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira. Information about this hermit, found in
<div n="lb"/>the Mahābhārata is given below:
<div n="NI"/>(1) Raibhya who was a friend of Bharadvāja had two
<div n="lb"/>sons named Arvāvasu and Parāvasu. They were
<div n="lb"/>great scholars. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 135,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 12).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Bharadvāja once forbade his son Yavakrīta from
<div n="lb"/>going to the hermitage of Raibhya. Getting angry at
<div n="lb"/>this, Raibhya struck his matted hair on the ground and
<div n="lb"/>created a wicked fairy and ordered her to kill Yavakrīta.
<div n="lb"/>Knowing this Bharadvāja ran to that place and cursed
<div n="lb"/>Raibhya that his eldest son would kill him.
<div n="NI"/>Once Parāvasu, mistaking his father for a cruel animal
<div n="lb"/>killed him. By the effort of his second son Arvāvasu,
<div n="lb"/>Raibhya was brought to life again. (M.B. Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 138, Stanza 137).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Raibhya was the son of Aṅgiras. (M.B. Śānti
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 208, Stanza 26).
[Page626-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(4) Raibhya was once an assistant at the sacrifice of
<div n="lb"/>Uparicaravasu. (M.B. Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 33,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Raibhya was one of the hermits who visited Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>in his bed of arrows. (M.B. Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>26, Stanza 26).
