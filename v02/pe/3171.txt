KALPA III¦. The customary proceedings of Yāgas.
<div n="lb"/>These proceedings are made in the form of Sūtras. The
<div n="lb"/>Sūtras describe how the Brāhmaṇas and mantras are
<div n="lb"/>to be used. For each Saṃhitā there are separate
<div n="lb"/>Śrauta Sūtras. The Śrauta Sūtras for Ṛgveda Saṃhitā
<div n="lb"/>are Āśvalāyana, Śāṃkhāyana and Śaunaka. Those for
<div n="lb"/>Sāmaveda are Maśaka, Lāṭyāyana and Drāhyāyaṇa.
<div n="lb"/>Those for Kṛṣṇa Yajurveda are Āpastamba Bauddhā-
<div n="lb"/>yana, Satyāṣāḍha, Hiraṇyakeśī, Mānava, Bhāradvāja,
<div n="lb"/>Vādhūla, Vaikhānasa, Laugākṣi, Maitra, Kaṭha and
<div n="lb"/>Vārāha. For Śuklayajurveda it is Kātyāyana Srauta
<div n="lb"/>Sūtra. For Atharvaveda it is Kauśika Śrauta Sūtra.
<div n="lb"/>All these Sūtras contain only brief symbolic words and
<div n="lb"/>are difficult to understand without explanation.
