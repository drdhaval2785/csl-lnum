SAMANTAPAÑCAKA¦. A holy tīrtha (bath) founded
<div n="lb"/>by Paraśurāma.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} See under Paraśurāma, Para 7.
[Page677-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Paraśurāma made here five rivers through which
<div n="lb"/>blood flowed. There, with hands dipped in blood, he
<div n="lb"/>offered oblations to the manes. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 2, Verse 4).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) By the blessings of the ancestors of Paraśurāma
<div n="lb"/>this place became a holy bath. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 2, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) At the ending and the beginning periods of the
<div n="lb"/>two yugas Dvāpara and Kali, the Kauravas and the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas fought the great battle at this place. Because
<div n="lb"/>of that this place got the name Samantapañcaka.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 2, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It was according to the advice of Balabhadrarāma
<div n="lb"/>that this place was selected as the battlefield. (Śalya
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 55, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Duryodhana was killed at Samantapañcaka.
<div n="lb"/>(Śalya Parva, Chapter 89, Verse 40).
