DEVALA II¦. A muni, a very erudite scholar in the
<div n="lb"/>Vedas. He was the elder brother of Dhaumya maharṣi,
<div n="lb"/>and was present at the sarpa-satra (serpent yajña) of
<div n="lb"/>King Janamejaya. Once Śrī Kṛṣṇa on his way from
<div n="lb"/>Dvārakā to Hastināpura met Devala. After the great war
<div n="lb"/>was over he visited Yudhiṣṭhira.
<div n="NI"/>Devala had a daughter called Suvarcalā. In the svayaṃ-
<div n="lb"/>vara ceremony he held for his daughter to which sons
<div n="lb"/>of munis were invited she chose Śvetaketu as her husband.
<div n="lb"/>(Śānti Parva, Southern Text, Chapter 22).
<div n="NI"/>Devala had two very intelligent and forbearing sons.
<div n="lb"/>(Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Part 1, Chapter 15).
