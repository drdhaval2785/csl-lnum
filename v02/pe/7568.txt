TRASADASYU¦. A King of the Ikṣvāku dynasty. He
<div n="lb"/>accepted sannyāsa (ascetic life) and became a Rājarṣi.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descending in order from Viṣṇu-Brahmā-
<div n="lb"/>Marīci-Kaśyapa-Vivasvān-Vaivasvata Manu-Ikṣvāku-
<div n="lb"/>Vikukṣi-Śa āda-Purañjaya (Kakutstha)-Anenas-Pṛthu-
<div n="lb"/>lāśva-Prasenajit-Yuvanāśva-Māndhātā-Purukutsa-
<div n="lb"/>Trasadasyu.
[Page791-b+ 62]
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Because he made dasyus (evil people) ‘tras’ (to
<div n="lb"/>tremble with fear) he got the name of Trasadasyu.
<div n="lb"/>(7th Skandha, Devī Bhāgavata).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) The Aśvinīdevas once saved him from defeat in a
<div n="lb"/>fight. (Sūkta 112, Maṇḍala 1, Ṛgveda).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Once Agastya, Śrutarvā and Bradhnāśva, three
<div n="lb"/>eminent sages, came to the country of Trasadasyu. On
<div n="lb"/>hearing that the sages were coming, Trasadasyu
<div n="lb"/>abandoned all his work and went to receive the sages
<div n="lb"/>at the state boundary. He asked them the purpose of
<div n="lb"/>their visit and they said they wanted some money. The
<div n="lb"/>King then showed them his accounts and convinced
<div n="lb"/>them that he was poor. (Śloka 16, Chapter 98, Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Trasadasyu was one among those whose name
<div n="lb"/>should be remembered early in the morning. (Śloka 55,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 165, Anuśāsana Parva).
