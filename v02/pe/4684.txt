NAIMIṢA¦. (NAIMIṢĀRAṆYA).
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} Naimiṣa is very famous in the Purāṇas. It
<div n="lb"/>is considered to be a sacred place. Nimasar is the
<div n="lb"/>modern name for the place, and it is in the Sītāpur
<div n="lb"/>zilla of North India.
<div n="NI"/>Śaunaka conducted a yajña here which lasted for twelve
<div n="lb"/>years. All the reputed Ṛṣis participated in it, and there
<div n="lb"/>Sūta (Sauti) the son of Vyāsa recited to the Maharṣis
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata composed by Vyāsa. (Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>1; also see under Sūta).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>Devas once came to this place and conducted a yajña
<div n="lb"/>there. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 196, Verse 1).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Arjuna once visited here the river called Utpalinī.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 214, Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Half of the sin of those who visit Naimiṣa will at
<div n="lb"/>once be dispelled. The remaining portion of the sin also
<div n="lb"/>will be removed before they return from the place. He
<div n="lb"/>who stays for a month and bathes here will derive all the
<div n="lb"/>results of Gomeda yajña. He who quits his life here by
<div n="lb"/>fasting will attain all the sacred lokas. (Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 84, Verse 59).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) River Gomatī, Yajñabhūmi of the Devas and the
<div n="lb"/>yājñic vessel of Sūrya are found at this place. (Vana-
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 87, Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Dharmaputra once came to this place with his
[Page517-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>brothers and bathed here and made gifts of cows.
<div n="lb"/>(Vana Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 1).
<div n="NI"/>NAIMIṢAKUÑJA. An ancient sacred place on the
<div n="lb"/>borders of Kurukṣetra. The Ṛṣis of Naimiṣāraṇya built
<div n="lb"/>this place. A bath here gives the same results as an
<div n="lb"/>agniṣṭomayajña. (Vana Parva, Chapter 83, Verse
<div n="lb"/>108).
