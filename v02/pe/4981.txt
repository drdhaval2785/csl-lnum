PAÑCAŚIKHA¦. A sage of ancient times. The Purāṇas
<div n="lb"/>give the following details about him.
<div n="NI"/>He was a disciple of Āsuri. He was brought up breast-
<div n="lb"/>fed by Kapilā, wife of Āsuri and so he was known as
<div n="lb"/>Kāpila also. He dwelt in Pañcasrotas and performed a
<div n="lb"/>Yāga for a thousand years and got his name Pañcaśikha.
<div n="lb"/>He went to the assembly of the learned king Janaka and
<div n="lb"/>entered into a polemic contest with him and defeated
<div n="lb"/>him. The defeated King gave Pañcaśikha great respect
<div n="lb"/>and he lived in the court of Janaka as his Guru for a
<div n="lb"/>number of years. (Chapter 218, Śānti Parva).
