DAṆḌAM III¦. One of the four expedients. The four
<div n="lb"/>expedients are Sāma, Dāna, Bheda and Daṇḍa. A king
<div n="lb"/>should subdue an enemy by resorting to these four
<div n="lb"/>expedients. Of the four expedients, sāma and daṇḍa
<div n="lb"/>are considered to be the best.
<Poem>Sāmādīnāmupāyānāṃ
<div n="lb"/>Caturṇāmapi paṇḍitāḥ /
<div n="lb"/>Sāmadaṇḍau praśaṃsanti
<div n="lb"/>Nityaṃ rāṣṭrābhivṛddhaye. //</Poem>
<div n="lb"/>(Manusmṛti, Stanza 109).
<div n="NI"/>Wise people say that, of the four expedients Sāma and
<div n="lb"/>Daṇḍa are the most effective for the prosperity of the
<div n="lb"/>country.
