YUDHĀMANYU¦. A warrior who fought against the
<div n="lb"/>Kauravas from the side of the Pāṇḍavas. The following
<div n="lb"/>references are made to him in the Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Yudhāmanyu was a prince of the Pāñcāla royal
<div n="lb"/>family. (M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 17, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He took his position in the battle as the bodyguard
<div n="lb"/>of Arjuna. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 15, Verse 19).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Yudhāmanyu's war-horse was a wonderful
<div n="lb"/>animal, both in spirit and shape. (M.B. Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 23, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) In Bhārata Yuddha he fought against Kṛtavarmā,
<div n="lb"/>Duryodhana, Kṛpācārya, Citrasena the brother of
<div n="lb"/>Karṇa, and Aśvatthāmā. Kṛpācārya defeated him. He
<div n="lb"/>(Yudhāmanyu) killed Karṇa's brother Citrasena.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapters 92, 130; Karṇa Parva, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ters 61, 83).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Yudhāmanyu met with his death in his fight against
<div n="lb"/>Aśvatthāmā. (M.B. Sauptika Parva, Chapter 8, Verse
<div n="lb"/>38).
