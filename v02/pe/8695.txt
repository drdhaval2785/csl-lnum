YAJÑA¦. An incarnation of Mahāviṣṇu. Svāyambhuva
<div n="lb"/>Manu had two sons, Priyavrata and Uttānapāda and
<div n="lb"/>three daughters, Ākūti, Devahūti and Prasūti. Ruci
<div n="lb"/>Prajāpati married Ākūti. Yajña was their son. Devī
<div n="lb"/>Bhāgavata, 8th Skandha mentions that this Yajña was
<div n="lb"/>an incarnation of Ādi Nārāyaṇa.
<div n="NI"/>Yajña had a sister named Dakṣiṇā. They were twins.
<div n="lb"/>They married each other. The twelve sons who were born
<div n="lb"/>to them were the Devas known as Yāmas, during
<div n="lb"/>the Svāyambhuva Manvantara. (Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Part 1,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 7).
<div n="NI"/>Yajña's twelve sons were:—Toṣa, Pratoṣa, Santoṣa,
<div n="lb"/>Bhadra, Śānti, Iḍaspati, Iddhma, Kavi, Vibhu, Sraghna,
<div n="lb"/>Sudeva and Virocaka. Their father Yajña was the Indra
<div n="lb"/>of Svāyambhuva Manvantara. (Bhāgavata, 4th Skandha).
