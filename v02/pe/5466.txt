PUNARVASU¦ ĀTREYA. An ancient preceptor of
<div n="lb"/>Āyurveda. He was the Guru of Agnideva author of
<div n="lb"/>the book ‘Agniveśatantra’ which is the basis of Caraka-
<div n="lb"/>saṃhitā and also of his classmates like Bhela.
<div n="NI"/>Punarvasu was the son of the sage Atri who was one
<div n="lb"/>of the spiritual sons of Brahmā. In support of this
<div n="lb"/>statement it can be found in many places in Caraku
<div n="lb"/>saṃhitā his name referred to as ‘Atrisuta’ or Atri-
<div n="lb"/>nandana’.
<div n="NI"/>Atri maharṣi was also a learned preceptor of Āyurveda
<div n="lb"/>According to Kaśyapasaṃhitā Devendra taught Āyur
<div n="lb"/>veda to Kaśyapa, Vaṣiṣṭha, Atri and Bhṛgu. The in-
<div n="lb"/>complete work ‘Āyurvedacikitsātantra’ by Atri was
