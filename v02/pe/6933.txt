SUDEṢṆĀ I¦. The wife of Virāṭa, the King of Matsya.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} It is mentioned in Mahābhā-
<div n="lb"/>rata, Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 9, Verse 6, that this
<div n="lb"/>Sudeṣṇā, the daughter of the King of Kekaya, had the
<div n="lb"/>name Citrā also.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) During the pseudonymity, Pāñcālī lived with
<div n="lb"/>Sudeṣṇā in the guise of Sairandhrī. (M.B. Virāṭa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 9).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Sudeṣṇā agreed to the request of Kīcaka to get
<div n="lb"/>Sairandhrī for him, as he was fascinated by her beauty.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 14, Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Sudeṣṇā sent Kīcaka to the house of Sairandhrī.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 15).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Draupadī consoled Sudeṣṇā who was miserable at
<div n="lb"/>the death of Kīcaka. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 16,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Sudeṣṇā asked Draupadī to go away from the
<div n="lb"/>palace. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 24, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) In connection with the marriage of her daughter
<div n="lb"/>Uttarā, Sudeṣṇā went to Upaplavya with Draupadī.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 72, Verse 30).
