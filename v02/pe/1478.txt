CARMAṆVATĪ¦. A river in north India, now known
<div n="lb"/>as river Campā.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} King Śaśabindu, who ruled northern
<div n="lb"/>India in olden days once performed a yajña. The skins
<div n="lb"/>(carmans) of animals killed in the yajña lay there in a
<div n="lb"/>heap like a hill. When rain fell there flowed from the
<div n="lb"/>‘skin-hill’ a river and it was called Carmaṇvatī. (Devī-
<div n="lb"/>bhāgavata, Prathama Skandha).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%} (1) River Carmaṇvatī serves Varuṇa
<div n="lb"/>in his assembly. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 9, Verse
<div n="lb"/>21).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Once on the bank of this river Sahadeva defeated
<div n="lb"/>the son of Jaṃbhaka in fight. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 31, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>(3) He who bathes in this river will get the same result
<div n="lb"/>as from the Agniṣṭoma yajña. (M.B. Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 82, Verse 54).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Carmaṇvatī is one of the rivers responsible for the
<div n="lb"/>origin of Agni. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 222,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 23).
