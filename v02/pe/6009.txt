ŚĀLMALIDVĪPA¦. One of the seven islands.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} Śālmali island, which is double in extent
<div n="lb"/>of Plakṣa island is surrounded by Ikṣurasa (sugar-cane
<div n="lb"/>juice) ocean on all four sides of it. There are seven
<div n="lb"/>mountains there which produce gems and they are
<div n="lb"/>seven boundaries which separate the regions. Just like
<div n="lb"/>this, there are also seven rivers there. The seven rivers are
<div n="lb"/>Yoni, Toyā, Vitṛṇā, Candrā, Muktā, Vimocanī and
<div n="lb"/>Nirvṛti. Mere remembrance of the seven rivers is
<div n="lb"/>sufficient to annihilate all sins. Here people
<div n="lb"/>divided into the four classes live. Brahmins, Kṣatriyas
<div n="lb"/>Vaiśyas and Śūdras here are called Kapilas, Aruṇas,
<div n="lb"/>Pītas and Kṛṣṇas. These devout people worship Viṣṇu
<div n="lb"/>in the form of Vāyu by sublime yajñas. Devas are ever
<div n="lb"/>present on this most beautiful island. A tree named
<div n="lb"/>Sālmali which gives mental comfort grows in this
<div n="lb"/>island. The island is surrounded on all four sides by
<div n="lb"/>an ocean of Surā (liquor). (Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Part II,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 4).
<div n="NI"/>2). {%Śālmali island and nāgāstra.%} See under Nāgastra.
