VĀNARA¦ (MONKEY). Monkeys are given a prominent
<div n="lb"/>place in the Purāṇas. Considering them as born in the
<div n="lb"/>family of Hanūmān, an ardent devotee of Śrī Rāma,
<div n="lb"/>some worship monkeys. It is mentioned in Vālmīki
<div n="lb"/>Rāmāyaṇa that the monkeys got a prominent place
<div n="lb"/>first among the gods and spirits. It is stated in Vālmīki
<div n="lb"/>Rāmāyaṇa, Kiṣkindhā Kāṇḍa, Sarga 33, that the
<div n="lb"/>leaders of the monkeys were Bāli, Sugrīva, Aṅgada,
<div n="lb"/>Mainda, Gavaya, Dvivida, Gavākṣa, Gaja, Śarabha,
<div n="lb"/>Sūryākṣa, Hanūmān, Vidyunmālī, Vīrabāhu, Subāhu,
<div n="lb"/>Nala, Kumuda, Jāmbavān, Tāra, Supāṭala, Sunetra,
<div n="lb"/>Nīla and Dadhivaktra.
<div n="NI"/>The social life of the monkeys, is described as follows
<div n="lb"/>in the Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Kiṣkindhā Kāṇḍa, Sarga
<div n="lb"/>33. “Mahendra mountain, the Himālayas, the Vindhya
<div n="lb"/>mountain, the peaks of Kailāsa and Śveta mountain,
<div n="lb"/>Mandara mountain etc. are the places mostly inhabit-
<div n="lb"/>ed by monkeys. On the mountains shining like the
<div n="lb"/>rising sun, on the east of the western ocean also monkeys
<div n="lb"/>live. The Monkeys which inhabit black soil are blue
<div n="lb"/>in colour. Monkeys which live in red arsenic caves
<div n="lb"/>are yellow in colour. Those which live in Mahā Meru
<div n="lb"/>and Dhūmra (smoky) mountain have the colour of the
<div n="lb"/>rising sun and are drunkards.”
