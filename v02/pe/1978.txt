DHENUKA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} A fierce Rākṣasa (giant) with the
<div n="lb"/>figure of a donkey. He lived in the forest of Kālī (Tāla).
<div n="lb"/>People did not dare to pass by that way fearing this
<div n="lb"/>giant. Hearing about him, Śrī Kṛṣṇa and his elder
<div n="lb"/>brother Balabhadra Rāma went to the forest. There
<div n="lb"/>were several palm trees in the forest, the fruits of which
<div n="lb"/>they shook with force. Hearing the noise Dhenuka ran
<div n="lb"/>to them. Balabhadra and Śrī Kṛṣṇa beat the giant to
<div n="lb"/>death.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%} (1) In Bhāgavata, Skandha 10, it is
<div n="lb"/>stated that Pralambaka, Cāṇūra, Tṛṇāvarta, Muṣṭika,
<div n="lb"/>Ariṣṭaka, Keśi, Dhenuka and others were the followers
<div n="lb"/>of Kaṃsa.
<div n="NI"/>(2) In Bhāgavata, Skandha 10, there is another story
<div n="lb"/>that while Balabhadra Rāma, Śrī Kṛṣṇa and the other
<div n="lb"/>cowherds were looking after the cows an asura entered
<div n="lb"/>into the midst of the flock, in the shape of a cow
<div n="lb"/>(Dhenu). Rāma and Kṛṣṇa saw him, struck him against
<div n="lb"/>a tree and killed him.
