VATSA¦ III (VATSABHŪMI). A country in ancient
<div n="lb"/>India. The following information is given about this
<div n="lb"/>country in the Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Bhīmasena conquered this country during his region-
<div n="lb"/>al conquest. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 30, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>10).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Karṇa once brought this country under control.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 254, Stanza 9).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) During the battle of Bhārata, the people of Vatsa
<div n="lb"/>were on the side of the Pāṇḍavas. (M.B. Udyoga
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 53, Stanza 1.
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Deified beings (semigods) and heavenly singers had
<div n="lb"/>lived in this country. There is an asylum there for
<div n="lb"/>holy men. Ambā, the princess of Kāśī once lived in
<div n="lb"/>this hermitage. (M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 186,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 34).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Ambā became a river and still flows through this
<div n="lb"/>country under the name Ambā. (M.B. Udyoga Parva
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 186, Stanza 40).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) In the battle of Bhārata, the warriors of Vatsa
<div n="lb"/>stood on the left side of the disposition of the army call-
<div n="lb"/>ed Krauñcāruṇavyūha, formed by Dhṛṣṭadyumna. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 50, Stanza 53).
