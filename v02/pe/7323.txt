SUTASOMA¦. The son born to Bhīmasena by his wife
<div n="lb"/>Draupadī. The information regarding this Sutasoma,
<div n="lb"/>taken from Mahābhārata, is given below:
<div n="NI"/>(i) Sutasoma took birth from a portion of the Viśva-
<div n="lb"/>devas. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 67, Verse 127).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 23, Verse 28, that this prince was given the
<div n="lb"/>name Sutasoma, because he was born by the blessings
<div n="lb"/>of Candra (Moon).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Sutasoma had a combat with Vikarṇa on the
<div n="lb"/>first day of the battle of Bhārata. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 45, Verse 58).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) He rescued Śrutakarmā from the hold of Durmukha
<div n="lb"/>in the battle of Bhārata. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 79, Verse 39).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Sutasoma fought with Viviṃśati. (M.B. Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 25, Verse 24).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Sutasoma fought with Śakuni and was defeated.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 25, Verse 18).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) There was a severe fight between Sutasoma and
<div n="lb"/>Aśvatthāmā. (M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 55, Verse
<div n="lb"/>14).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Mention is made in Mahābhārata, Sauptika Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 8, Verse 55 that in the battle of Bhārata, Aśvat-
<div n="lb"/>thāmā entered the camp of the Pāṇḍava army in the
<div n="lb"/>night and killed Sutasoma.
