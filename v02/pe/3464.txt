KEKAYA¦(M).
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A Kingdom in ancient India. Kaikeyī, one
<div n="lb"/>of Daśaratha's wives, was a daughter of a Kekaya
<div n="lb"/>King.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Origin.%} The country got the name ‘Kekaya’ as it
<div n="lb"/>was ruled by King Kekayā. His genealogy is as follows.
<div n="lb"/>Descended from Viṣṇu thus:—Brahmā—Atri—Candra—
<div n="lb"/>Budha—Purūravas—Āyus—Nahuṣa—Yayāti—Anud-
<div n="lb"/>ruhyu—Sabhānara—Kālanara—Sṛñjaya—Uśīnara—
<div n="lb"/>Śibi—Kekaya. Śibi had four sons called Bhadra,
<div n="lb"/>Suvīra, Kekaya and Vṛṣādarpa. (Bhāgavata 9th
<div n="lb"/>Skandha).
<div n="lb"/>{%Other details.%} (1) The King and the people of Kekaya
<div n="lb"/>were called the Kekayas.
<div n="NI"/>(2) Five heroic Kekaya princes met with their death
<div n="lb"/>in fighting Droṇa. (Strī Parva, Chapter 25, Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Two Kekaya Princes, Vinda and Anuvinda fought
<div n="lb"/>on the Kaurava side. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 13).
