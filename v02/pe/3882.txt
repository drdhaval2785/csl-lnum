KURU I¦. A King called Kuru was born in Dhruva's
<div n="lb"/>dynasty.
<div n="NI"/>{%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu thus: Brahmā—
[Page444-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>Svāyambhuva Manu—Uttānapāda—Dhruva—Śiṣṭi—
<div n="lb"/>Ripu—Cākṣuṣa (Cākṣuṣa Manu)—Kuru.
<div n="NI"/>King Kuru was the grandfather of the reputed Vena and
<div n="lb"/>the great grandfather of emperor Pṛthu. Aṅga was the
<div n="lb"/>father of Vena. Not much is said about this Kuru in
<div n="lb"/>the Purāṇas. Kuru had ten brothers called Puru, Ūru,
<div n="lb"/>Śatadyumna, Tapasvī, Satyavāk, Śuci, Agniṣṭhu,
<div n="lb"/>Adhiratha, Sudyumna and Abhimanyu. Kuru had
<div n="lb"/>by his wife Ātreyī seven sons called Aṅga, Sumanas
<div n="lb"/>Svāti, Kratu, Aṅgiras, Gaya and Śibi, and to Aṅga
<div n="lb"/>was born by his wife Sunīthā the son, who became
<div n="lb"/>reputed as Vena. Pṛthu was Vena's son. Pṛthu had
<div n="lb"/>five sons called Antardhāna, Vādī, Sūta, Māgadha,
<div n="lb"/>Pālita. To Antardhāna was born of his wife Śikhaṇḍinī
<div n="lb"/>a son called Havirdhāna, to whom were born by his
<div n="lb"/>wife Dhiṣaṇā six sons called Prācīnabarhis, Śukra, Gaya,
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa, Vṛaja and Ajina. The above is the only inform-
<div n="lb"/>ation available about this Kuru dynasty in the
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇas. (Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Part 1, Chapter 13),
