ĀNARTTA¦. Grandson of Vaivasvatamanu. He was a
<div n="lb"/>king, son of Śaryāti.
<div n="NI"/>1. {%Genealogy.%} Descending in order from Viṣṇu-Brahmā-
<div n="lb"/>Marīci-Kaśyapa-Vivasvān-Vaivasvatamanu-Śaryāti-
<div n="lb"/>Ānartta.
<div n="NI"/>Sixteen sons were born to Vaivasvatamanu: Manu,
<div n="lb"/>Yama, Yamī, Aśvinīkumāra, Revanta, Sudyumna, Ikṣ-
<div n="lb"/>vāku, Nṛga, Śaryāti, Diṣṭa, Dhṛṣṭa, Karūṣa, Nariṣyanta,
<div n="lb"/>Nābhāga, Pṛṣadhra and Kavi. Of these Śaryāti got
<div n="lb"/>two children, Sukanyā and Anartta. Sukanyā became
<div n="lb"/>the wife of Cyavana.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%} It was this king who built the fortresses
<div n="lb"/>at the town of Kuśasthalī (Dvārakā). Varuṇa submerg-
[Page089-b+ 55]
<div n="lb"/>ed in the ocean all the fortresses built by him and the
<div n="lb"/>place for some time remained as a forest-land. (Daśama
<div n="lb"/>Skandha, Bhāgavata Kilippāttu). It was after that that
<div n="lb"/>Śrī Kṛṣṇa made that land beautiful and called it
<div n="lb"/>Dvārakā. The country ruled over by Ānartta was called
<div n="lb"/>Ānarttam. During his regime there lived in his land a
<div n="lb"/>very great scholar named Vedaratha. (Umeśānavrata,
<div n="lb"/>Śiva Purāṇa) See under Vedaratha.
