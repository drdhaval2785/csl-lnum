NĪLA III¦. A monkey-chief, who was a dependant of Śrī
<div n="lb"/>Rāma. He was Agni's son. ‘Nīla, son of Pāvaka (fire)
<div n="lb"/>shone forth like agni (fire). He stood foremost among
<div n="lb"/>the monkeys in the matter of effulgence, reputation and
<div n="lb"/>prowess’. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Bālakāṇḍa, Canto 17).
<div n="NI"/>This monkey-chief was also included in the set of
<div n="lb"/>monkeys deputed by Śrī Rāma to search for Sītā. In
<div n="lb"/>the Rāma-Rāvaṇa war Nīla killed Pramāthī, the younger
<div n="lb"/>brother of the Rākṣasa called Dūṣaṇa. (Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 287, Verse 27).
