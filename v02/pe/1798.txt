DEVABHĀGAŚRAUTĀRṢA¦. A ṛṣi, the son of Śruta
<div n="lb"/>and learned in yajñas. He had accurate knowledge as to
[Page208-a+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>which parts of the yajña-cow should be distributed to
<div n="lb"/>whom, and till death he did not impart this knowledge
<div n="lb"/>to anyone. But, sometime afterwards a non-human
<div n="lb"/>individual taught the subject to Girija, son of Babhru.
<div n="lb"/>(Aitareya Brāhmaṇa).
<div n="NI"/>At the time when the Sṛñjayas and the Kuru kings were
<div n="lb"/>living in amity consequent upon the dākṣāyaṇa yajña
<div n="lb"/>it was this Devabhāga who acted as the priest of both
<div n="lb"/>the parties. His theories on Sāvitrāgni are quoted in
<div n="lb"/>Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa.
