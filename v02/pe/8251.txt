VIDEHA¦ II The kingdom of Mithilā. This country
<div n="lb"/>which lies on the North East part of India was ruled
<div n="lb"/>by Kṣatriya kings of the dynasty of Videha. It is stat-
<div n="lb"/>ed in Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva, Chapter 29, that
<div n="lb"/>Bhīmasena had during his eastern regional conquest,
<div n="lb"/>conquered this country. About this kingdom which is
<div n="lb"/>the native country of Sītā, the following information
<div n="lb"/>is available in Mahābhārata.
[Page847-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(i) The hermitage of Paraśurāma was on the North
<div n="lb"/>of Videha. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 130, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>13).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) The army of Videha attacked Arjuna in the
<div n="lb"/>battle of Bhārata. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 117,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 32).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Karṇa defeated the Kṣatriya princes of the country
<div n="lb"/>of Janaka. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 4, Stanza 6).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Paraśurāma cut the Kṣatriyas of this country
<div n="lb"/>into pieces with his sharp arrows. (M.B. Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 70, Stanza 11).
<div n="NI"/>(v) This country had been giving tribute to Karṇa.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 9, Stanza 33).
