KRAUÑCAVYŪHA¦. A strategic formation of the army
<div n="lb"/>in the shape of Krauñca bird (snipe). In the battle
<div n="lb"/>of Bhārata, Bhīṣma made the formation of Krauñca.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 75). The Krauñca dis-
<div n="lb"/>position has eight strategical positions, the face, eyes,
<div n="lb"/>head, neck, stomach, left flank, right flank and thighs.
<div n="lb"/>In the disposition formed by Bhīṣma, Droṇa stood at
<div n="lb"/>the face, Aśvatthāmā and Kṛpa stood at the eyes,
<div n="lb"/>Hārdikya at the head and Śūrasena at the neck. The
<div n="lb"/>King of Prāgjyotiṣa stood at the stomach of the form-
<div n="lb"/>ation. The Tuṣāras, Yavanas, Śakas and Cūpuvas
<div n="lb"/>guarded the right flank. Śṛtāyus and Bhūriśravas kept
<div n="lb"/>the thighs.
