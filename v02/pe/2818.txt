<div n="NI"/>1) {%General Information.%} A hermit of the highest degree
<div n="lb"/>of learning. In the “History of Classical Sanskrit
<div n="lb"/>Literature” it is mentioned that Sumantu, Jaimini,
<div n="lb"/>Paila, Vaiśampāyana and Śuka were the five important
<div n="lb"/>disciples of Vyāsa. Of these Śuka was the son of
<div n="lb"/>Vyāsa. In Devī Bhāgavata, it is mentioned that
<div n="lb"/>Vyāsa had other disciples also in his hermitage, such
<div n="lb"/>as Asita, Devala and others. The five disciples men-
<div n="lb"/>tioned first gave publication to the work of Vyāsa
<div n="lb"/>called ‘Jaya’, which was the original of the Mahā-
<div n="lb"/>bhārata. Vaiśampāyana and Jaimini made some
<div n="lb"/>additions to the work ‘Jaya’. In Sanskrit there is
<div n="lb"/>another book called Jaimini Bhārata, which contains
<div n="lb"/>only Aśvamedha Parva. (See under Guruparamparā).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%} (1) It is mentioned in Brahmāṇḍa
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa, Chapter 1 that Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa is the
<div n="lb"/>story told by Jaimini to Hiraṇyanābha at Naimi-
<div n="lb"/>śāraṇya.
<div n="NI"/>(2) Jaimini was present at the sarpasatra (sacrifice to
<div n="lb"/>kill serpents) of Janamejaya. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 53, Stanza 6).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Jaimini had been a member of the council of

<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Śabhā Parva, Chapter 4, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>11).
<div n="NI"/>(4) This hermit visited Bhīṣma in his bed of arrows
<div n="lb"/>during the Bhārata Battle. (M.B. Śānti Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>46, Stanza 7).
