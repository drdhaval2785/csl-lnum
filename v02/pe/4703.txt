NĀLU¦ (FOUR). Various things mentioned in the
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇas having some association with four
<div n="lb"/>(Nālu) are given below:—
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Four distances.%} In the case of an elephant one must
<div n="lb"/>keep a distance of 1000 kols, a horse 100 kols,
[Page520-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>horned animals 10 kols, evil people, unlimited number
<div n="lb"/>of kols. (1 kol is equal to a metre).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Four Adhikārins.%} Manda (the very ordinary),
<div n="lb"/>Madhyama (medium standard), Uttama (best) and
<div n="lb"/>Uttamottama (the very best).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Four Anubandhas. (Factors).%} In philosophy there
<div n="lb"/>are four factors called Viṣaya (subject matter),
<div n="lb"/>Prayojana (purpose), Śambandha (relationship between
<div n="lb"/>factors) and Adhikārin (the deserving or eligible person).
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Four Antaḥkaraṇas (Internal organs).%} Manas (mind),
<div n="lb"/>Buddhi (intellect), Cittam (heart) and Ahaṅkāra (ego).
<div n="lb"/>Imagination is the function of the mind, decision that
<div n="lb"/>of Buddhi; to retain knowledge gained in orderly
<div n="lb"/>form is the function of Cittam and self-respect that of
<div n="lb"/>Ahaṅkāra.
<div n="NI"/>5) {%Four Anvavāyas.%} Satyam (truthfulness), Dama
<div n="lb"/>(Self control), Ārjavam (straightforwardness) and
<div n="lb"/>Ānṛśaṃsyam (not to be cruel).
<div n="NI"/>6) {%Anvavedas Four.%} Yajña (sacrifical offerings), Dāna
<div n="lb"/>(alms-giving, gifts), Adhyayana (learning) and Tapas
<div n="lb"/>(penance).
<div n="NI"/>7) {%Four Apātrīkaraṇas.%} (Not suited to the particular
<div n="lb"/>station or place in life). Brahmins are forbidden from
<div n="lb"/>receiving bribes, engaging in trades, service of Śūdras
<div n="lb"/>and uttering lies.
<div n="NI"/>8) {%Abhinayas Four. (Acting).%} Āṅgikam (where gestures
<div n="lb"/>and bodily actions are used to convey ideas). Sāttvika
<div n="lb"/>Subjective feelings expressed by perspiration etc.
<div n="lb"/>Āhāryam (extraneous), Vācikam (by words of mouth).
<div n="NI"/>9) {%Abhyāsaviṣayas Four.%} (Subjects for practice and
<div n="lb"/>training). Vinaya (humility), Damana (control of
<div n="lb"/>mind), Indriyanigraha (controlling the sense-organs)
<div n="lb"/>and Bhūtadayā (kindness).
<div n="NI"/>10) {%Amṛtas Four. (Nectar).%} Good wife, talk of children,
<div n="lb"/>present from King and honourable food.
<div n="NI"/>11) {%Alaṅkāras Four. (Ornaments).%} For the stars, Moon;
<div n="lb"/>for women, husband; for earth, King and for all, edu-
<div n="lb"/>cation (learning).
<div n="NI"/>12) {%Alaṅkārasādhanas Four.%} According to rhetorics,
<div n="lb"/>Atiśaya (excellence), Sāmya (simile), Vāstavam
<div n="lb"/>(matter of fact, as it is) and Śleṣa (one word with
<div n="lb"/>two meanings) are the Alaṅkārasādhanas.
<div n="NI"/>13) {%Avasthās Four. (States, conditions).%}
<div n="lb"/>{%A.%} Śaiśavam (childhood), Kaumāram (boyhood),
<div n="lb"/>Yauvanam (youth) and Vārdhakyam (old age).
<div n="lb"/>{%B.%} Jāgrat (wakefulness), Svapnam (dream), Suṣupti
<div n="lb"/>(sleep) and Turīyam (being one with the
<div n="lb"/>supreme soul).
<div n="NI"/>14) {%Astraprayogalakṣyas Four. (Objects of shooting arrows).%}
<div n="lb"/>Sthiram, Calam, Calācalam, Dvayacalam.
<div n="NI"/>When the archer and the object of his shooting
<div n="lb"/>remain motionless the object is called Sthiram. When
<div n="lb"/>the object is moving but the archer is not, the object
<div n="lb"/>is called Calam. When the case is just the opposite of
<div n="lb"/>the above it is Calācalam. When both are moving it
<div n="lb"/>is called Dvayacalam.
<div n="NI"/>15) {%Ākhyāyikāguṇas Four. (Factors of the novel).%}
<div n="lb"/>Kathābandha (plot or theme), Pātraprakaṭanam
<div n="lb"/>(exposition of characters), Rasapuṣṭi (sentiment)
<div n="lb"/>and Gadyarīti (prose style).
<div n="NI"/>16) {%Ābharaṇas Four (Ornaments).%} For man, shape or
<div n="lb"/>form; for form, quality or merit; for quality, knowledge
<div n="lb"/>or wisdom and for wisdom, patience or forbearance.
<div n="NI"/>17) {%Āyudhas Four. (Weapons).%} Mukta, Amukta, Muktā-
<div n="lb"/>mukta and Yantramukta. (See under Dhanurveda).
[Page521-a+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>18) {%Āvaraṇas Four. (Covering, Protection).%} For earth
<div n="lb"/>the sea, for house the compound wall, for country the
<div n="lb"/>King, for women chastity.
<div n="NI"/>19) {%Āhārādis Four.%} (Food etc.). Āhāra (food), Nīhāra
<div n="lb"/>(Evacuation), Maithuna (sexual act), Nidrā (sleep).
<div n="NI"/>20) {%Āhāravastus Four. (edibles).%} Khādyam (eaten by
<div n="lb"/>munching with teeth and chewing), Peyam (that which
<div n="lb"/>is drunk), Lehyam (licked with the tongue) and Bhojyam
<div n="lb"/>(that which is not included in the above three).
<div n="NI"/>21) {%Ṛṇas Four.%} (Obligations, debts). Debts due to
<div n="lb"/>Devas, Ṛṣis, Pitṛs and Men. One pays back one's debts
<div n="lb"/>to Devas by performing yajñas. By Svādhyāya (self-
<div n="lb"/>study) and tapas one pays the debt due to Ṛṣis; by
<div n="lb"/>procreation of children and libation offerings that due
<div n="lb"/>to Pitṛs and by truthfulness, hospitality etc. that due to
<div n="lb"/>people are repaid.
<div n="NI"/>22) {%Ṛtviks Four.%} Adhvaryu, Udgātā, Hotā and Brahmā.
<div n="lb"/>The first of the four should be an erudite scholar in
<div n="lb"/>Yajurveda, the second in Sāmaveda, the third in
<div n="lb"/>Atharvaveda and the fourth in all the four Vedas.
<div n="NI"/>23) {%Kavis Four. (Poets).%} He who boasts about himself
<div n="lb"/>in secret is called Udātta; he who cries down others
<div n="lb"/>and indulges in self-praise is known as Uddhata; he
<div n="lb"/>who proclaims others' merits is called Prauḍha and he
<div n="lb"/>who shows humility is called Vinīta.
<div n="NI"/>24) {%Kukkuṭaguṇas Four. (Traits of the Cock).%} To rise
<div n="lb"/>early in the morning, to struggle for existence, to share
<div n="lb"/>whatever is got with relations and to work and earn
<div n="lb"/>one's own food—these are the qualities of the Cock.
<div n="NI"/>25) {%Grāhyas Four. (Acceptables).%}
<div n="P"/>{%A.%} Nectar even from poison, good advice even from
<div n="lb"/>boys, good action even from enemies and noble and
<div n="lb"/>chaste brides even from low families are to be wel-
<div n="lb"/>comed.
<div n="P"/>{%B.%} Literacy (learning of alphabets) should be
<div n="lb"/>accepted from brahmins, food from mother, pan from
<div n="lb"/>wife and bangles from King.
<div n="NI"/>26) {%Caturaṅgas Four.%} Elephant, Horse, Chariot and
<div n="lb"/>Infantry.
<div n="NI"/>27) {%Āśramas Four.%} Brahmacarya (student life),
<div n="lb"/>Gārhasthya (married life), Vānaprastha (anchorite,
<div n="lb"/>forest-life) and Sannyāsa (Renunciation).
<div n="NI"/>28) {%Yugas Four.%} Kṛta, Tretā, Dvāpara and Kali.
<div n="NI"/>29) {%Vargas Four.%} Dharma, Artha, Kāma and Mokṣa.
<div n="NI"/>30) {%Upāyas Four.%} (Expedients). Sāma, Dāna, Bheda,
<div n="lb"/>Daṇḍa.
<div n="NI"/>31) {%Cikitsāpādas Four.%} (Four elements in the treatment
<div n="lb"/>of patients). Vaidya (doctor), Rogin (patient),
<div n="lb"/>Auṣadham (medicine) and Paricāraka (attendant).
<div n="NI"/>32) {%Jātis Four. (Castes).%} Brāhmaṇa, Kṣatriya, Vaiśya
<div n="lb"/>and Śūdra.
<div n="NI"/>33) {%Tyājyas Four. (things to be shunned).%}
<div n="P"/>{%A.%} Horse returning after bath, elephant in its rut,
<div n="lb"/>love-lorn bull and wicked scholar.
<div n="P"/>{%B.%} Evil action, unhealthy region, evil wife and bad
<div n="lb"/>foods.
<div n="NI"/>34) {%Dānas Four. (Gifts).%} Gifts daily given without
<div n="lb"/>expecting return or result is Nityadāna. Gifts given to
<div n="lb"/>scholars (pundits) for the sake of alleviation of or
<div n="lb"/>redemption from sin is Naimittikadāna. Gifts given for
<div n="lb"/>welfare and prosperity is Kāmyakadāna. Offering made
<div n="lb"/>to propitiate God is Vimala.
<div n="NI"/>35) {%Nāyakas Four. (Heroes)%} Dhīrodātta, Dhīroddhata,
<div n="lb"/>Dhīralalita, Dhīraśānta.
[Page521-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>36) {%Nārīs Four. (Women).%} Padminī, Śaṅkhinī, Citriṇī,
<div n="lb"/>and Hastinī.
<div n="NI"/>37) {%Pramāṇas Four. (Means of valid knowledge),%} Pratya-
<div n="lb"/>kṣa, Anumāna, Upamāna, and Śabda.
<div n="NI"/>38) {%Mokṣas Four. (Salvation).%} Sālokya, Sāmīpya,
<div n="lb"/>Sārūpya and Sāyujya.
<div n="NI"/>39) {%Four Yogas.%} Jñānayoga, Bhaktiyoga, Karmayoga
<div n="lb"/>and Dhyānayoga.
<div n="NI"/>40) {%Four Śatrus (Enemies).%} Mother leading an immo-
<div n="lb"/>ral life; father who incurs debt; foolish son and beauti-
<div n="lb"/>ful wife.
<div n="NI"/>41) {%Four Śāstras.%} Nītiśāstra, Tarkaśāstra, Manusmṛti
<div n="lb"/>and Kāmaśāstra.
