APARĀVIDYĀ¦. Knowledge is of two categories or
<div n="lb"/>types. Viz.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Parāvidyā.%} That science or knowledge, which helps
<div n="lb"/>us to know or realise Him (Brahma) who is without
<div n="lb"/>either beginning or end, who could neither be seen
<div n="lb"/>with the eyes nor perceived by any of the senses.
<div n="NI"/>(2) {%Aparāvidyā.%} This includes knowledge of the four
<div n="lb"/>Vedas Ṛg, Yajus, Sāma and Atharva, the six Vedāṅgas
<div n="lb"/>(sub-divisions of the Vedas) Śikṣā, Kalpa, Nirukta,
<div n="lb"/>Chandas, Vyākaraṇa, Apidhāna, Mīmāṃsā, Dharma-
<div n="lb"/>śāstra and the purāṇas, as also, Nyāya Śāstra (Logic),
<div n="lb"/>Vaidya Śāstra (medicine) Gandharva Śāstra, Dhanur-
<div n="lb"/>veda and Artha Śāstra (economics).
