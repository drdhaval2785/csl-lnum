VIRĀṬA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} The King of Matsya country.
<div n="lb"/>During the pseudonymity of the Pāṇḍavas this King
<div n="lb"/>sheltered them. At the end of the pseudonymity of one
[Page861-a+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>year, the Kauravas had stolen the cows of Virāṭa. In
<div n="lb"/>the fight which ensued Arjuna entered the battlefield
<div n="lb"/>with Uttara, the son of King Virāṭa, and defeated the
<div n="lb"/>Kauravas and proclaimed that the life of pseudonymity
<div n="lb"/>was over. After that Abhimanyu married Uttarā the
<div n="lb"/>daughter of Virāṭa. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) This Virāṭa, the King of Matsya was born from a
<div n="lb"/>portion of the Marudgaṇas. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>67, Stanza 82).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) King Virāṭa had two sons named Uttara and
<div n="lb"/>Śaṅkha. It was with these sons that he attended the
<div n="lb"/>Svayaṃvara marriage of Draupadī. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 185, Stanza 8).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) In the regional conquest conducted by Sahadeva,
<div n="lb"/>before the Imperial consecration-sacrifice of the Pāṇḍa-
<div n="lb"/>vas, Virāṭa fought with him and was defeated. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Sabhā Parva, Chapter 31, Stanza 2).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) King Virāṭa attended the Rājasūya (sacrifice of
<div n="lb"/>imperial consecration) of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 44, Stanza 20).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Virāṭa gave as a gift to Yudhiṣṭhira two thousand
<div n="lb"/>tuskers adorned with gold chains. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 52, Stanza 26).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) The name of the wife of King Virāṭa was Sudeṣṇā.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 9, Stanza 6).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) During the incognito-life of the Pāṇḍavas, King
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa sheltered them in his palace. He took Yudhi-
<div n="lb"/>ṣṭhira as a court-favourite, Bhīmasena as the over-seer
<div n="lb"/>of his dining hall, Arjuna as the dancing master, Nakula
<div n="lb"/>as the head of the stables, and Sahadeva as the head of
<div n="lb"/>the cow-herds. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapters 7, 8, 10,
<div n="lb"/>11 and 12).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The first wife of King Virāṭa was Surathā, the
<div n="lb"/>princess of Kosala. A son named Śveta was born to
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa by Surathā. After the death of Surathā, he
<div n="lb"/>married Sudeṣṇā the daughter of Sūta, King of Kekaya.
<div n="lb"/>To Sudeṣṇā two sons named Śaṅkha and Uttara and as
<div n="lb"/>the youngest, a daughter named Uttarā were born.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Dākṣiṇātya Pāṭha, Chapter 16).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) It is stated in Mahābhārata, Virāṭa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Dākṣiṇātyapāṭha, Chapter 26, that King Virāṭa had
<div n="lb"/>ten brothers.
<div n="NI"/>(x) Virāṭa had two brothers named Śatānīka and
<div n="lb"/>Madirākṣa. Sūryadatta is another name of Śatānīka.
<div n="lb"/>He was the commander of the army of Virāṭa. Madirā-
<div n="lb"/>kṣa was also called Viśālākṣa. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapters 31 and 32).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) At the time of the theft of the cows, King Virāṭa
<div n="lb"/>engaged in combat with Suśarmā. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 32, Stanza 28).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) Suśarmā caught hold of Virāṭa alive. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 33, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) Virāṭa was one of the seven prominent command-
<div n="lb"/>ers of the army of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Udyoga Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 157, Stanza 11).
<div n="NI"/>(xiv) On the first day of the battle of Bhārata there
<div n="lb"/>was a combat between Virāṭa and Bhagadatta. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45, Stanza 49).
<div n="NI"/>(xv) Virāṭa attacked Bhīṣma. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 73, Stanza 1).
<div n="NI"/>(xvi) In the fight between Virāṭa and Droṇa, Śaṅkha
<div n="lb"/>was killed. With that Virāṭa ran away. (M.B. Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 82, Stanza 14).
<div n="NI"/>(xvii) There was a combat between Virāṭa and Aśvat-
[Page861-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>thāmā. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 110, Stanza 16).
<div n="NI"/>(xviii) Virāṭa combatted with Jayadratha. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 116, Stanza 42).
<div n="NI"/>(xix) Virāṭa fought with Vinda and Anuvinda. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa Parva, Chapter 25, Stanza 20).
<div n="NI"/>(xx) In the fight with Śalya, Virāṭa fell down uncons-
<div n="lb"/>cious. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 167, Stanza 34).
<div n="NI"/>(xxi) In the battle which followed, Droṇācārya killed
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa. (M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 6, Stanza 6).
<div n="NI"/>(xxii) Mention is made in Mahābhārata, Strī Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 26, Stanza 33, that the funeral ceremony of
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa was conducted in a befitting manner and in
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata, Śānti Parva, Chapter 42, Stanza 4, that
<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira performed offering to the Manes for him.
<div n="NI"/>(xxiii) After death, Virāṭa entered heaven and joined
<div n="lb"/>the Marudgaṇas. (M.B. Svargārohaṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>5, Stanza 15).
<div n="NI"/>(xxiv) The synonyms used in Mahābhārata for Virāṭa
<div n="lb"/>are, Matsya, Matsyapati, Matsyarāṭ, Matsyarāja etc.
