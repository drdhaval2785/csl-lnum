VAIŚAMPĀYANA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} A prominent disciple of Vyāsa.
<div n="lb"/>It is mentioned in Devī Bhāgavata, Skandha 1, that
<div n="lb"/>the main disciples of Vyāsa were Asita, Devala,
<div n="lb"/>Vaiśampāyana, Sumantu, Jaimini, Paila and some
<div n="lb"/>others. (See under Bhārata).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) It was Vaiśampāyana who told the story of Bhārata
<div n="lb"/>composed by Vyāsa, to King Janamejaya. (M.B. Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 1, Stanza 20).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Vaiśampāyana told Janamejaya the story of
<div n="lb"/>Bhārata at the instruction of Vyāsa. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 60, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Vaiśampāyana praised Mahābhārata and spoke
<div n="lb"/>of its greatness. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 62, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>12).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Once Vaiśampāyana was overpowered by igno-
<div n="lb"/>rance, and he killed a Brahmin. It is mentioned in
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata, Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 6, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>36, that in spite of it he attained heaven.
