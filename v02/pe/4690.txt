NAKṢATRAYOGA¦. It is ordained in the Purāṇas that
<div n="lb"/>alms-giving on each star or day will be rewarded by
<div n="lb"/>particular attainments. This is called Nakṣatrayoga.
<div n="lb"/>The Nakṣatrayoga of each star is given below:—
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Aśvayuk:%}—Those who give horses and chariots as
<div n="lb"/>alms on this day, would be born again in a noble
<div n="lb"/>family.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Bharaṇī:%}—If lands and cows are given to Brahmins
<div n="lb"/>on this day, one would get a large number of cows and
<div n="lb"/>will become famous in heaven.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Kṛttikā:%}—If rice-pudding is given to Brahmins to
<div n="lb"/>their satisfaction on this day the giver will attain the
<div n="lb"/>prominent worlds after death.
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Rohiṇī:%}—If milk-rice and venison mingled with
<div n="lb"/>ghee is given to Brahmins on this day, his indebtedness
<div n="lb"/>to the manes would end.
<div n="NI"/>5) {%Mṛgaśiras:%}—(Candra nakṣatra). If a milch-cow
<div n="lb"/>is given as alms on this day, one would attain heaven.
<div n="NI"/>6) {%Ārdrā:%}—If fast is taken and gingelly oil is given as
<div n="lb"/>alms on this day, the giver will become capable of
<div n="lb"/>crossing mountains and trenches.
<div n="NI"/>7) {%Punarvasu:%}—He who gives bread on this day will be
<div n="lb"/>born again in a good family.
<div n="NI"/>8) {%Puṣya:%}—He who gives gold as alms on this day
<div n="lb"/>will enter the world of bright planets.
[Page518-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>9) {%Āśleṣā:%}—He who gives an ox made of silver as alms
<div n="lb"/>on this day, will become fearless.
<div n="NI"/>10) {%Maghā:%}—He who gives gingelly as alms on this
<div n="lb"/>day will become prosperous with cows.
<div n="NI"/>11) {%Pūrvaphalgunī:%}—If ghee-rice is given to Brahmins
<div n="lb"/>taking fast on this day, the giver would become happy
<div n="lb"/>and prosperous.
<div n="NI"/>12) {%Uttaraphalgunī:%}—He who gives rice of Nīvara
<div n="lb"/>variety mixed with milk and ghee on this day will be
<div n="lb"/>honoured in heaven.
<div n="NI"/>13) {%Hasta:%}—If one gives four horses and an elephant
<div n="lb"/>as alms, on this day, one will attain the world of bliss.
<div n="NI"/>14) {%Citrā:%}—He who gives oxen and perfumery as alms
<div n="lb"/>on this day, will enter the parks in which celestial
<div n="lb"/>maids play.
<div n="NI"/>15) {%Svāti:%}—He who gives any sort of wealth as alms
<div n="lb"/>on this day will become renowned in the worlds.
<div n="NI"/>16) {%Viśākhā:%}—He who gives as alms on this day, oxen,
<div n="lb"/>milch-cow, grain-box, cart, paddy and diamond will
<div n="lb"/>attain heaven.
<div n="NI"/>17) {%Anurādhā:%}—He who gives cloth, rice and blanket
<div n="lb"/>on this day will be honoured in heaven for a hundred
<div n="lb"/>yugas.
<div n="NI"/>18) {%Jyeṣṭhā:%}—If Brahmins are given yams and greens
<div n="lb"/>on this day one could realize one's wishes.
<div n="NI"/>19) {%Mūlā:%}—The manes will be contented, if Brahmins
<div n="lb"/>are given roots and nuts on this day.
<div n="NI"/>20) {%Pūrvāṣāḍhā:%}—If Brahmins who are expounders of
<div n="lb"/>Vedas are given as alms pots of curd, after observing
<div n="lb"/>fast, the givers will take birth again in families with
<div n="lb"/>many cows.
<div n="NI"/>21) {%Uttarāṣāḍhā:%}—He who gives milk and ghee to
<div n="lb"/>wise men will be honoured in heaven.
<div n="NI"/>22) {%Śravaṇa:%}—Those who give cloth and rug on
<div n="lb"/>this day will enter a white conveyance and reach
<div n="lb"/>heaven.
<div n="NI"/>23) {%Śraviṣṭhā:%}—Those who give cattle, cart and cloth
<div n="lb"/>on this day will enter heaven.
<div n="NI"/>24) {%Śatabhiṣak:%}—Those who give sandalwood and
<div n="lb"/>aloe wood on this day, will go to the world of devas
<div n="lb"/>(gods).
<div n="NI"/>25) {%Pūrvaproṣṭhapada:%}—He who gives coins on this day
<div n="lb"/>will reach the world of bliss.
<div n="NI"/>26) {%Uttaraproṣṭhapada:%}—Those who give mutton on
<div n="lb"/>this day will be pleasing the manes.
<div n="NI"/>27) {%Revatī:%}—He who gives cows which could give
<div n="lb"/>potfuls of milk, could reach any world. (M.B. Anu-
<div n="lb"/>śāsana Parva, Chapter 64).
