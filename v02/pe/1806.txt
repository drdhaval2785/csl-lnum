DEVADATTA¦(M). The divine conch of Arjuna. Maya
<div n="lb"/>got this conch from Varuṇa and he kept it in the sabhā
<div n="lb"/>of Vṛṣaparvan, which was in Bindusaras on Mount
<div n="lb"/>Maināka to the south of Mount Kailāsa. When Maya
<div n="lb"/>built the palace at Indraprastha for the Pāṇḍavas he
<div n="lb"/>went to Bindusaras and brought for Arjuna that conch
<div n="lb"/>called Devadattam, and a club for Bhīmasena. (Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 3).
<div n="NI"/>In the battle of Kurukṣetra Arjuna mounted a white
<div n="lb"/>horse and blew his conch Devadattam. (Bhīṣma Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 25, Verse 14).
