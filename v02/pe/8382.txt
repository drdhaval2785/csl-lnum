VIPĀŚĀ¦. A Purāṇically famous river in the region of
<div n="lb"/>five rivers (Punjab). Vasiṣṭha was stricken with grief
<div n="lb"/>at the death of his son Śakti and jumped into this river
<div n="lb"/>with the help of a rope to commit suicide. The waves
<div n="lb"/>of the river united the knots of the rope and saved him.
<div n="lb"/>From that day onwards this river came to be known as
<div n="lb"/>Vipāśā. (See under Kalmāṣapāda) Other information
<div n="lb"/>about this river given in the Purāṇas is given below:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) Vipāśā stays in the palace of Varuṇa serving him.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Chapter 9, Stanza 19).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Two devils named Bahi and Hīka live in this river.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 44, Stanza 41).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) If offering to the Manes is made in this river and
<div n="lb"/>celibacy is observed there for three days without anger,
<div n="lb"/>one could get away from death and birth. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 25, Stanza 24).
