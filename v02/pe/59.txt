AGNĪDHRA¦ (AGNĪDDHRA).
<div n="NI"/>1. {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu thus: Viṣṇu-
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā-Marīci-Kaśyapa-Vivasvān-Vaivasvatamanu-
<div n="lb"/>Priyavrata-Agnīdhra.
<div n="NI"/>2. {%Birth.%} Priyavrata, son of Vaivasvatamanu, married
<div n="lb"/>Barhiṣmatī, daughter of Viśvakarmā. Agnīdhra was one
<div n="lb"/>of their ten sons. The other nine sons were: Idhma-
<div n="lb"/>jihvā, Yajñabāhu, Mahāvīra, Hiraṇyaretas, Ghṛta-
<div n="lb"/>prṣṭha, Sava, Medhātithi, Vītihotra and Kavi. A
<div n="lb"/>daughter also was born to Priyavrata and Barhiṣmatī
<div n="lb"/>named Ūrjjasvatī. Śukra married her and Devayānī
<div n="lb"/>was their daughter.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Married life.%} Agnīdhra married a nymph named
<div n="lb"/>Pūrvacitti. They had nine children: Nābhi, Kiṃ-
<div n="lb"/>purusa, Hari, Ilāvrata, Ramyaka, Hirañcaya, Kuru,
<div n="lb"/>Bhadrāśva, and Ketumāla. It was from this Kuru that
<div n="lb"/>the Kuru Vaṃśa began.
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Other incidents.%} (1) Agnīdhra ruled over Jambu-
<div n="lb"/>dvīpa for a long time. (Devī Bhāgavata, Skandha 8).
<div n="NI"/>(2) While he was the ruler of Jambudvīpa Agnīdhra
<div n="lb"/>once went into a cave and did hard tapas there with-
<div n="lb"/>out eating any food. Brahmā then sent a beautiful
<div n="lb"/>nymph (Apsarā maiden) to him to tempt him and
<div n="lb"/>thwart his tapas. Agnīdhra fell a victim to the tempta-
<div n="lb"/>tion. His penance was shaken by that nymph named
<div n="lb"/>Vipracitti. He married her. (Bhāgavata, Skandha 5,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 2).
