MEDHĀTITHI I¦. Grandson of Svāyambhuva Manu.
<div n="lb"/>Svāyambhuva Manu had two sons named Priyavrata and
<div n="lb"/>Uttānapāda. Of these Priyavrata married Sarūpā and
<div n="lb"/>Barhiṣmatī, daughters of Viśvakarmaprajāpati. Medhā-
<div n="lb"/>tithi was the son born to Priyavrata of Sarūpā. Agnī-
<div n="lb"/>dhra, and others were the brothers of Medhātithi.
<div n="lb"/>Medhātithi became the King of Plakṣadvīpa after the
<div n="lb"/>death of Priyavrata. (8th Skandha, Devī Bhāgavata).
<div n="lb"/>Medhātithi got seven sons named Śāntahaya, Śiśira,
<div n="lb"/>Sukhodaya, Ānanda, Śiva. Kṣemaka and Dhruva. They
<div n="lb"/>all became Kings of Plakṣadvīpa. The countries they
<div n="lb"/>ruled were named after them as Śāntahayavarṣa, Śiśira-
<div n="lb"/>varṣa, Sukhodayavarṣa, Ānandavarṣa, Śivavarṣa, Kṣema-
<div n="lb"/>kavarṣa and Dhruvavarṣa. There are seven mountains
<div n="lb"/>showing the boundaries of these states and they are
<div n="lb"/>called Gomeda, Cāndra, Nārada, Dundubhi, Somaka,
<div n="lb"/>Sumana and Vaibhrāja. In these beautiful countries
<div n="lb"/>and grand mountains live a great many Devas,
<div n="lb"/>Gandharvas and virtuous men. (Chapter 4, Aṃśa 2,
<div n="lb"/>Viṣṇu Purāṇa).
