VASUDEVA¦. Father of Śrī Kṛṣṇa.
[Page838-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} See under Kṛṣṇa.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Previous birth and current birth.%} Vasudeva was the rebirth
<div n="lb"/>of Prajāpati Kasyapa. Aditi and Surasā, the wives of
<div n="lb"/>Kaśyapa took birth as Devakī and Rohiṇī respectively.
<div n="lb"/>(For details see under Kaśyapa).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Marriage and Family.%} Rohiṇī, the wife of Vasudeva,
<div n="lb"/>gave birth to Balarāma, the fighter Sāraṇa, Durdhara,
<div n="lb"/>Dama, Piṇḍāraka and Mahāhanu. Of Devakī who
<div n="lb"/>was also called Māyā and Amāvāsī, the younger son
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa and his beautiful sister Subhadrā were born.
<div n="lb"/>Vijaya, Rocamāna, Vardhamāna and Devala were sons
<div n="lb"/>born by Upadevī. The two sons Agāvaha and Mandaga,
<div n="lb"/>otherwise called Angada were born of Vṛkadevī.
<div n="lb"/>Revanta who could not be defeated in battle, was born
<div n="lb"/>from Saptamīdevī. The first son of Vasudeva was
<div n="lb"/>Kauśika, whose birth was from a Vaiśya woman called
<div n="lb"/>Śraddhā Devī when Vasudeva was travelling in the
<div n="lb"/>forest. Kapila, a mighty son was born by the queen
<div n="lb"/>Śrutandharā. Saubhadra head of the archers and
<div n="lb"/>Abhava were born by his wife Janā. (Padma Purāṇa,
<div n="lb"/>P. 1314).
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Vasudeva was the son of Śūrasena, and the husband
<div n="lb"/>of Devakī, the father of Śrī Kṛṣṇa, the brother of
<div n="lb"/>Kuntī, and the minister of Ugrasena. It was Vasudeva
<div n="lb"/>who sent the priest Kaśyapa to Śataśṛṅga to perform
<div n="lb"/>the purification ceremonies such as naming etc. of the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 123, Dākṣiṇātya-
<div n="lb"/>pāṭha).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) After the Bhārata-battle, Vasudeva asked Śrī
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa and learned from him the detailed news about
<div n="lb"/>the battle. (Aśvamedha Parva, Chapter 60).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Vasudeva fell into a swoon when he heard that
<div n="lb"/>Subhadrā had become unconscious. (M.B. Aśvamedha
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 61, Stanza 5).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) The ceremony of offering to the manes (Śrāddha)
<div n="lb"/>of Abhimanyu was performed by Vasudeva. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Aśvamedha Parva, Chapter 62, Stanza 1).
<div n="NI"/>(v) When the Yādavas fought among themselves after
<div n="lb"/>drinking liquor, Śrī Kṛṣṇa went to Dvārakā and visited
<div n="lb"/>Vasudeva, and requested him to protect his wives who
<div n="lb"/>were awaiting Arjuna. Then after kissing the feet of
<div n="lb"/>Vasudeva, Śrī Kṛṣṇa and Balabhadra Rāma went to
<div n="lb"/>do penance. (M.B. Mausala Parva, Chapter 4, Stanza 8).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) When the dynasty of Vṛṣṇi was completely destroy-
<div n="lb"/>ed Vasudeva thinking about the Supreme soul in his
<div n="lb"/>heart forsook his body. (M.B. Mausala Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>7, Stanza 15).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Arjuna performed the funeral rites of Vasudeva
<div n="lb"/>and aided his four wives to enter the funeral pyre.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Mausala Parva, Chapter 7, Stanza 19).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Vasudeva entered heaven and took his place
<div n="lb"/>among Viśvadevas (gods concerned with offering to the
<div n="lb"/>manes). (M.B. Svargārohaṇa Parva, Chapter 5, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>17).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) The synonyms of Vasudeva, used in Mahābhārata
<div n="lb"/>are Ānakadundubhi, Śauri, Śūraputra, Śūrasūnu,
<div n="lb"/>Yadūdvaha and so on.
