AKRŪRA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu in the following
<div n="lb"/>order: Brahmā-Atri-Candra-Budha-Purūravas-Āyus-
<div n="lb"/>Nahuṣa-Yayāti-Yadu (Chapter XII of Agni Purāṇa).
<div n="lb"/>Descending from Yadu in order were Sahasrajit-Śatajit-
<div n="lb"/>Hehaya-Dharma-Kunti-Bhadrasena-Dhanaka-Kṛtavīrya
<div n="lb"/>Kārttavīryārjuna-Madhu-Vṛṣṇi (Chapter XXIII of
<div n="lb"/>Navama Skandha, Bhāgavata). The Vṛṣṇi dynasty be-
<div n="lb"/>gins and from Vṛṣṇi in order descended Yudhājit-Śini-
<div n="lb"/>Satyaka-Sātyaki-Jaya-Kuṇi-Anamitra-Pṛśni-Śvaphalka-
<div n="lb"/>Akrūra. (Chapter XXIV of Navama Skandha, Bhāga-
<div n="lb"/>vata).
[Page022-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Birth.%} Śvaphalka of the Vṛṣṇi dynasty married
<div n="lb"/>Nandinī, daughter of the King of Kāśī and Akrūra was
<div n="lb"/>born to them. Akrūra was an uncle of Śrī Kṛṣṇa but is
<div n="lb"/>respected more as a worshipper of Kṛṣṇa.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Other details.%} (1) He became famous as a commander
<div n="lb"/>of the Yādava army. (Chapter 220 of Ādi Parva, M.B.).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Akrūra was also present for the Śvayaṃvara (wed-
<div n="lb"/>ding) of Pāñcālī. (Śloka 18, Chapter 185 of Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>M.B.).
<div n="NI"/>(3) At the time of Arjuna's eloping with Subhadrā, a
<div n="lb"/>grand festival was going on in the Raivata mountain and
<div n="lb"/>Akrūra was partaking in the same. (Śloka 10, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>218, Ādi Parva, M.B.).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Akrūra accompanied Kṛṣṇa with the dowry in-
<div n="lb"/>tended for Subhadrā. (Śloka 29, Chapter 220, Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>M.B.).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Akrūra came to the country called Upaplavya for
<div n="lb"/>attending the marriage of Abhimanyu. (Śloka 22, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 72, Virāṭa Parva, M.B.).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Akrūra and Āhuka always quarrelled with each
<div n="lb"/>other both alleging that the other sided with the opposite
<div n="lb"/>camp of Kṛṣṇa. (Ślokas 9 to 11, Chapter 81, Śānti
<div n="lb"/>Parva, M.B.).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Kaṃsa planning to kill Balabhadrarāma and Śrī
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa conducted a festival called Cāpapūjā (worship
<div n="lb"/>of the bow). It was Akrūra whom Kaṃsa sent to bring
<div n="lb"/>Balabhadra and Kṛṣṇa for the festival. Akrūra under-
<div n="lb"/>stood the plot, informed Kṛṣṇa about it and also advised
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa to kill Kaṃsa. (Daśama Skandha, Bhāgavata).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Akrūra fought against Jarāsandha on the side of
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa. (Daśama Skandha).
<div n="NI"/>(9) On another occasion Kṛṣṇa, Balabhadra and Ud-
<div n="lb"/>dhava sent Akrūra to Hastināpura to get tidings about
<div n="lb"/>Kuntī and the Pāṇḍavas. Akrūra met his sister Kuntī
<div n="lb"/>and talked to her for a long time and also met Dhṛta-
<div n="lb"/>rāṣṭra and talked to him after which he returned to
<div n="lb"/>Dvārakā. (Daśama Skandha).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Akrūra went to Hastināpura as a messenger from
<div n="lb"/>Śrī Kṛṣṇa. (Refer sub-para 3 of para 13 under Kṛṣṇa).
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Domestic life.%} Akrūra married Sutanū, daughter of
<div n="lb"/>Āhuka and got two sons named Devaka and Upadevaka.
<div n="lb"/>(Navama Skandha).
<div n="NI"/>5) {%Syamantaka and Akrūra.%} Refer para 2 under the word
<div n="lb"/>Kṛtavarmā.
