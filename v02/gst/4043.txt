{#anDaka#}¦ 1. m. f. n. ({#-nDakaH-nDikA-nDakam#}) Blind. E. {#anDa,#} taddh. aff. {#ka#}.
<div n="P">2. m. ({#-kaH#}) A proper name of: <sup>1</sup> a demon, a son of
<div n="lb">Kaśyapa and Diti ‘with thousand arms and heads, two
<div n="lb">thousand eyes and feet and called Andhaka because he walked
<div n="lb">like a blind man although he saw very well’; in his attempt
<div n="lb">of taking a Pārijāta tree of the Swarga he was slain by
<div n="lb">Śiva (see {#anDakaGAtin, anDakaripu#}); <sup>2</sup> a grandson of Kroṣṭṛ,
<div n="lb">and son of Yuddhājita, who together with his brother Vṛṣṇi
<div n="lb">is the ancestor of the celebrated family of the Andhaka-
<div n="lb">Vṛṣṇis; <sup>3</sup> a grandson of Vṛṣṇi (the brother of Andhaka)
<div n="lb">and son of Śvaphalka by Gāṇḍinī; <sup>4</sup> a son of Sattwat, be-
<div n="lb">longing to the same family, by Kauśalyā; <sup>5</sup> a son of Bhīma
<div n="lb">(of the same family) and father of Revata. [The forgoing
<div n="lb">lineage, 2--5, is taken from the Harivanśa. In the Linga
<div n="lb">Purāṇa an Andhaka is a son of Nahusha who, according
<div n="lb">to other Purāṇas, is the ancestor of Kroṣṭṛ; in the Kūrma
<div n="lb">Purāṇa an Andhaka is a son of Anśa and father of Sāttvata,
<div n="lb">while in the Viṣṇu P. a prince of that name is mentioned
<div n="lb">as the son of Sāttvata who is apparently the same as the
<div n="lb">Sattwat of the Hariv.] <sup>6</sup> The name of a Muni (in the Padma-
<div n="lb">purāṇa).
<div n="P">3. m. plur. ({#-kAH#}) The descendants of Andhaka (2. 2),
<div n="lb">(usually mentioned together with the Vṛṣṇis; see {#anDaka-#}
<div n="lb">{#vfzRi#}), apparently the ancient inhabitants of Berar.
<div n="P">4. f. ({#-nDikA#}) <sup>1</sup> Night. <sup>2</sup> A kind of play or gambling.
<div n="lb"><sup>3</sup> A disease of the penis (? the same as {#sarzapI#} q. v.). <sup>4</sup> A
<div n="lb">diseased eye. <sup>5</sup> A woman. [The two latter meanings from
<div n="lb">the Śabdaratnāvalī, where the word is thus explained: {#anDikA#}
