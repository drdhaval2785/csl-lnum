{#aprAptisama#}¦ Tatpur. m. f. ({#-maH-mA#}) (In the Nyāya philosophy.)
<div n="lb">One of the two sophisms on the irrelevancy of the notion
<div n="lb">‘cause’; viz. arguing that if a cause is stated the charac-
<div n="lb">teristics of which cannot be proved--as by saying that
<div n="lb">the Śyena sacrifice inflicts pain on enemies--, such charac-
<div n="lb">teristics prevent a cause from proving the effect, i. e. from
<div n="lb">being a valid cause; ({#‘yadi cAprAptaM liNgaM sADyabudDiM janayati#}
<div n="lb">{#sADyABAvabudDimeva kiM tena na janayet’#}); the refutation is:
<div n="lb">that such a cause is nevertheless valid, since it is an ideal
<div n="lb">cause, mentioned in the Veda.--For the other sophism
<div n="lb">on the irrelevancy of cause see {#prAptisama#}. E. {#aprApti#} and
<div n="lb">{#sama#}. (For the gender of this word and the ellipsis required
<div n="lb">to explain it, see the etym. of {#anityasama#}.)
