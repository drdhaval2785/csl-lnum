{%Ugrasena%} (I)¦ — A son of Āhuka of the Kukura family,
<div n="lb"/>father of Kamsa and other eight sons. Father of five
<div n="lb"/>daughters who were all married to the younger brothers of
<div n="lb"/>Vasudeva.<sup>1</sup> King of Sātvatas, Vṛṣṇis, Bhojas, and Dāśārhas.<sup>2</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Hated by his son Kamsa who desired even to kill him and
<div n="lb"/>who eventually threw him into prison.<sup>3</sup> Enthroned by
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa, after performing Kamsa's funeral rites;<sup>4</sup> got helpful
<div n="lb"/>service from Kṛṣṇa and welcomed him to Dvārakā.<sup>5</sup> In
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa's opinion Ugrasena was the proper person to wear
<div n="lb"/>the jewel Syamantaka; did not press it lest there should be
<div n="lb"/>a family feud.<sup>6</sup> When Jarāsandha beseiged Mathurā, Kṛṣṇa
<div n="lb"/>stationed Ugrasena to defend the northern gate; was pre-
<div n="lb"/>sented with spoils of war including jewels and ornaments.
<div n="lb"/>His part in defence against Jarāsandha's third attack on
<div n="lb"/>Mathurā. A member of the Yādava {%sabhā;%} was consulted
<div n="lb"/>by Kṛṣṇa as to failure of rains in Dvārakā.<sup>7</sup> Vāyu fetched
<div n="lb"/>from Indra the hall Sudharmā for Ugrasena's use;<sup>8</sup>
<div n="lb"/>attacked Pauṇḍraka and laughed at his message. Heard of
<div n="lb"/>Sāmba's imprisonment and urged war on the Kurus, order
<div n="lb"/>being communicated through Balarāma. According to the
<div n="lb"/>Kurus, a king only by sufferance.<sup>9</sup> Ordered the {%musala%}
<div n="lb"/>born to Sāmba to be powdered and thrown into the sea.<sup>10</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Sent to Dvārakā for defence after the {%Rājasūya.%}<sup>11</sup> Welcomed
<div n="lb"/>Balarāma on his return after a pilgrimage tour.<sup>12</sup> Went to
<div n="lb"/>Syamantapañcaka for solar eclipse.<sup>13</sup> Honoured Nanda.<sup>14</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Heard of Vṛṣṇis killing one another and of the decease of
<div n="lb"/>Rāma and Kṛṣṇa and bemoaned their loss.<sup>15</sup> Entered fire.<sup>16</sup>
