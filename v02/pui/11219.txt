{%Yamunā%} (I)¦ — ({%Kālindī%}) (R.) in Bhāratavarṣa from
<div n="lb"/>the Himālayas; a divine stream.<sup>1</sup> A mahānadī.<sup>2</sup> Here
<div n="lb"/>Vidura met Uddhava;<sup>3</sup> the co-wives who administered poison
<div n="lb"/>to Citraketu's son, had a purificatory bath in; Citraketu,
<div n="lb"/>who was initiated into the {%Vidyā%} by Nārada had his preli-
<div n="lb"/>minary bath in this river.<sup>4</sup> On its banks Vaivasvata Manu
<div n="lb"/>performed {%tapas%} for progeny, and Bharata celebrated his
<div n="lb"/>horse sacrifice. On its banks stands Madhuvana near
<div n="lb"/>Bṛṇdāvana. Kṛṣṇa, his brother, and others played there and
<div n="lb"/>once defeated the Asura Vatsa. Gopi girls bathed there as
<div n="lb"/>part of the {%Kātyayanī vratam.%} Nanda used to bathe here.<sup>5</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Immersed in its waters Saubhari performed penance.
<div n="lb"/>Poisoned by the Kālīya; Kṛṣṇa sent away the reptile to the
<div n="lb"/>sea and made its waters drinkable; unapproachable to
<div n="lb"/>Garuḍa by a curse of Saubhari.<sup>6</sup> Akrūra who took Kṛṣṇa
<div n="lb"/>and Rāma to Mathurā, had his bath on the way in this
<div n="lb"/>river. Inside the waters he saw both Kṛṣṇa and Rāma; also
<div n="lb"/>saw Hari on the Śeṣa. Here Kṛṣṇa killed Hamsa.<sup>7</sup> On its
<div n="lb"/>bank Jarāsandha encamped to besiege Mathurā. Here
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa had his {%avabhṛta%} after the horse sacrifice. Here
<div n="lb"/>again Kṛṣṇa and Arjuṇa met Kālindī.<sup>8</sup> During his pilgrimage
[Page3-017+ 43]
<div n="lb"/>Balarāma visited this.<sup>9</sup> Was in floods when Vasudeva
<div n="lb"/>crossed it for Gokula with the new born babe, Kṛṣṇa.<sup>10</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Sacred for śrāddha; sometimes too hot and sometimes too
<div n="lb"/>cold; daughter of Mārtāṇḍa and Samjñā and sister of Yama;
<div n="lb"/>got changed into a sacred river. On its northern bank is
<div n="lb"/>Prayāgā (Pratiṣṭhāna).<sup>11</sup> Sacred to Mṛgāvatī; sacred to the
<div n="lb"/>Pitṛs;<sup>12</sup> a boundary of the kingdom of Pitṛs;<sup>13</sup> in the chariot
<div n="lb"/>of Tripurāri;<sup>14</sup> one of the 16 wives of Havyavāhana (fire)<sup>15</sup>
<div n="lb"/>forms the left side of the Veda;<sup>16</sup> daughter of Sureṇu and
<div n="lb"/>Sūrya;<sup>17</sup> a sacred river.<sup>18</sup>
