{%Aṅgiras%} (I)¦ — a mānasa son of Brahmā, born of his mouth.<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Married Śraddhā, (Pitrī-{%Vi. P.%}) a daughter of Kardama.<sup>2</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Father of four daughters. His two sons were Utathya and
<div n="lb"/>Bṛhaspati.<sup>3</sup> Had not yet realised the Supreme Being.<sup>4</sup> With
<div n="lb"/>his pupils visited Bhīṣma lying on his bed of arrows.<sup>5</sup> Came
<div n="lb"/>to see Parīkṣit practising {%prāyopaveśa.%}<sup>6</sup> Married two of
<div n="lb"/>the daughters of Dakṣa. These were Svadhā and Satī. His
<div n="lb"/>sons were Pitṛ and Atharvāngiras. A Prajāpati.<sup>7</sup> Once
[Page1-023+ 39]
<div n="lb"/>called on Citraketu, the king of Śūrasenas, who was childless
<div n="lb"/>and blessed his first queen with a son. The jealous co-wives
<div n="lb"/>of the king administered poison to the baby and it died.
<div n="lb"/>Finding the king and queen weeping bitterly, Aṅgiras
<div n="lb"/>accompanied by Nārada came to the palace. These two
<div n="lb"/>sages appeared there in {%avadhūta%} form. Aṅgiras consoled
<div n="lb"/>the king and cited the story of King Bhoja.<sup>8</sup> Went back to
<div n="lb"/>{%Brahmaloka%} with Nārada.<sup>9</sup> Was appointed by King Rathi-
<div n="lb"/>kara to produce sons on his wife.<sup>10</sup> The sage presiding over
<div n="lb"/>the month of {%Nabha.%}<sup>11</sup> Was present at the anointing of
<div n="lb"/>Vāmana.<sup>12</sup> Came to see Kṛṣṇa at Syamantapañcaka.<sup>13</sup> Went
<div n="lb"/>to Dvārakā to ask Kṛṣṇa to go back to Vaikuṇṭha.<sup>14</sup> Had
<div n="lb"/>another son named Samvarta (s.v.).<sup>15</sup> Cursed Vidyādhara
<div n="lb"/>Sudarśana (s.v.) to become a reptile for mocking at him.<sup>16</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Performed a sacrifice when Śarayāti explained on the rituals
<div n="lb"/>of the second day.<sup>17</sup> Stood near the wheel of the chariot
<div n="lb"/>Tripurāri in defence; served Prayāga and lived in Benares;
<div n="lb"/>a {%mahaṛṣi%} and {%mantrakṛt.%}<sup>18</sup>
