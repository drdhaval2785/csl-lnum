<div n="lb">stanzas (18. 5. 47-50) occurring towards

<div n="P">The great sage Vyāsa having composed
<div n="lb">in the bygone days this Bhārata Saṃhitā,
<div n="lb">taught it to his son along with the four
<div n="lb">stanzas 18. 5. 46; contents of these stanzas

<div n="lb">(18. 5. 47-50): thousands of parents and
<div n="lb">hundreds of sons and wives with whom one
<div n="lb">lives in this world pass away and (many)
<div n="lb">others will pass away (in future) (47);
<div n="lb">only an ignorant, and not a wise person,
<div n="lb">experiences day by day thousands of occasions
<div n="lb">for delight and hundreds of occasions for
<div n="lb">fear (48); here I, (i. e. Vyāsa himself)
<div n="lb">with upraised hands, speak loudly (to
<div n="lb">people): why do you not practise {%dharma%}
<div n="lb">since from it proceed acquisition of profit
<div n="lb">and fulfilment of desire ({%ūrdhvabāhur%}
<div n="lb">{%viraumy eṣa na ca kaścic chṛṇoti me/%}
<div n="lb">{%dharmād arthaś ca kāmaś ca sa kimar-%}
<div n="lb">{%thaṃ na sevyate//%}) (49); one should not
<div n="lb">forsake {%dharma%} for the fulfilment of desire,
<div n="lb">nor out of fear, nor greed, nor even for
<div n="lb">saving one's life; (for), {%dharma%} is eternal,
<div n="lb">while happiness and misery are not eternal,
<div n="lb">{%jīva%} (soul) is eternal its cause ({%hetu%} i. e.
<div n="lb">body) is not eternal ({%na jātu kāmān na%}
<div n="lb">{%bhayān na lobhād dharmaṃ tyajej jīvi-%}

<div n="lb">(50); one who recites the Bhāratasāvitrī on
<div n="lb">waking up in the morning obtains the fruit
<div n="lb">of (reciting) the Bhārata and attains the
<div n="lb">highest {%brahman (sa bhārataphataṃ prāpya%}
<div n="lb">{%paraṃ brahmādhigacchati%}); (according to
<div n="lb">Nīlakaṇṭha on Bom Ed. 18. 5. 60 these
