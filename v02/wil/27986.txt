{#brahmacArin#}¦ m. ({#-rI#}) The {%Brahmacārī%} or religious student; the
young {%Brahman,%} from the time of his investiture with the cord, to the period
of his becoming a householder: it is also applied to a person, who continues
with his spiritual teacher, through life, studying the {%Vedas,%} and observing
the duties of the student; it is also given as a title to {%Paṇḍitas%} learned in
the {%Vedas;%} to a class of ascetics; by the {%Tantras,%} it is assigned to
persons whose chief virtue is the observance of continence; and it is assumed by
many religious vagabonds. f. ({#-riRI#}) A woman leading a life of continence.
.E. {#brahma#} the {%Veda,%} and {#cara#} to go or follow, aff. {#Rini#}.
