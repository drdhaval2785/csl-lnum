{#agnimAruti#}¦ m. ({#-tiH#}) A name of the saint, AGASTYA.
.E. {#agni#} fire, i. e. the fire of the stomach, the digestive faculty, and
{#mAruti#} a name of HANUMĀN; because the saint could digest as well as the
monkey: the epithet alludes to a story of his devouring and digesting VĀTĀPI,
a demon, who had assumed the form of a sheep.
